## Quick Summary

Alpha-bisabolol is a gentle, floral [terpene](/glossary/terpenes) renowned for its skin-soothing and anti-inflammatory properties. Found in chamomile and [cannabis](/glossary/cannabis), bisabolol is particularly valued in CBD [topicals](/glossary/topicals) and skincare products for its ability to reduce irritation and enhance absorption of other compounds.

## What Is Bisabolol?

Alpha-bisabolol (also called levomenol) is a monocyclic sesquiterpene [alcohol](/conditions/alcohol). It's the primary active compound responsible for chamomile's renowned calming and skin-soothing properties.

Natural sources:
- **German chamomile** — Primary commercial source
- **Candeia tree** — Brazilian source
- **Cannabis** — Present in various strains

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I use bisabolol if I'm allergic to chamomile?

Allergic reactions to pure alpha-bisabolol are extremely rare, even among those with chamomile [allergies](/conditions/allergies). The allergens in chamomile are typically proteins and other compounds, not the bisabolol itself. However, start with a small patch test on your inner wrist before applying bisabolol products to larger skin areas.

### How long does it take for bisabolol to work on irritated skin?

Most people notice reduced redness and irritation within 15-30 minutes of applying bisabolol-containing products. For ongoing skin issues like [eczema](/conditions/eczema) or minor wounds, consistent twice-daily application typically shows significant improvement within 3-7 days of regular use.

### What percentage of bisabolol should I look for in topical products?

Effective bisabolol concentrations in skincare products typically range from 0.1% to 2%. For sensitive skin or daily use, 0.1-0.5% is usually sufficient. Higher concentrations (1-2%) are better for targeted treatment of specific irritated areas or acute skin issues.

### Does bisabolol interact with other skincare ingredients?

Bisabolol is highly compatible with most skincare ingredients and actually enhances the absorption of other compounds. It pairs particularly well with CBD, hyaluronic acid, and vitamin E. Avoid mixing with very high concentrations of acids (over 10% glycolic acid) as this may reduce bisabolol's soothing effects.

### Can I use bisabolol products during [pregnancy](/conditions/pregnancy) or [breastfeeding](/conditions/breastfeeding)?

Topical bisabolol is considered safe during pregnancy and breastfeeding since it has minimal systemic absorption. It's been used safely in baby products for decades. However, always consult your healthcare provider before starting any new skincare regimen during pregnancy or while nursing.
