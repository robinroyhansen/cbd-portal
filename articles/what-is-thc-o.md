# What is THC-O (THC-O-Acetate)?

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Answer

THC-O (THC-O-Acetate) is a synthetic cannabinoid derived from hemp-extracted THC. It's reportedly 2-3 times more potent than delta-9-THC and produces strong psychoactive effects. Unlike natural cannabinoids, THC-O is created through chemical synthesis and carries significant safety concerns. The DEA has indicated that THC-O is a controlled substance regardless of its source.

---

## What is THC-O?

THC-O-Acetate is an acetate ester of THC, meaning it's THC with an acetate group attached. This modification significantly increases its potency and changes how it interacts with the body.

**Key characteristics:**
- Synthetic derivative (not naturally occurring)
- 2-3x more potent than delta-9-THC
- Requires chemical synthesis to produce
- Classified as a controlled substance by DEA
- Carries significant safety concerns

---

## How THC-O is Made

THC-O production involves:

1. Extracting CBD from hemp
2. Converting CBD to delta-8 or delta-9-THC
3. Adding acetic anhydride (a highly flammable chemical)
4. Creating THC-O-Acetate

This process requires dangerous chemicals and should only be performed by trained chemists in proper laboratories.

---

## Effects of THC-O

Users report THC-O produces:

- Strong psychoactive effects
- "Spiritual" or "psychedelic" experiences
- Intense euphoria
- Potentially longer onset time (especially when eaten)
- Effects lasting several hours

**Warning:** Due to limited research, the full effect profile and safety of THC-O are not established.

---

## Safety Concerns

**Major safety issues include:**

- **No safety research:** THC-O has not been studied for safety in humans
- **Production hazards:** Synthesis involves dangerous chemicals
- **Unknown contaminants:** Poorly made products may contain harmful byproducts
- **Thermal degradation:** When vaped, THC-O may produce ketene, a toxic gas
- **Potency variability:** Products may be inconsistent

---

## Legal Status

**United States:** In February 2023, the DEA clarified that THC-O is a controlled substance because it's synthetically derived, not naturally occurring in hemp. This applies regardless of whether the starting material was hemp-derived.

**Recommendation:** Avoid THC-O products due to legal risks and safety concerns.
