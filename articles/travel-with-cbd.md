# How to Travel with CBD

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Travelling with CBD requires understanding laws at your destination, following airline liquid rules for carry-on, keeping products in original packaging with lab reports accessible, and using THC-free products to minimise complications. CBD legality varies significantly by country, so research before you travelâ€”what's legal at home may be prohibited elsewhere.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Research destination laws first
- Follow TSA/airline liquid rules
- Keep original packaging
- Bring lab reports
- Use THC-free products when travelling

---

## Before You Travel

### Research Destination Laws

**Check:**
- Is CBD legal there?
- What THC limits apply?
- Are there import restrictions?
- Documentation requirements?

**Resources:**
- Government websites
- Embassy information
- Recent traveller reports
- Legal databases

### Countries Where CBD Is Problematic

**Strict/Banned:**
- Many Asian countries
- Some Middle Eastern countries
- Parts of Africa
- Varies significantly

**Even Trace THC Issues:**
- Countries with zero-tolerance policies
- Places where all cannabis is illegal
- Variable enforcement

---

## Domestic Travel (UK/EU)

### Within the UK

**Generally:**
- Legal to travel with legal CBD
- Keep in original packaging
- Have COA accessible
- No special permits needed

### Within the EU

**Considerations:**
- Laws vary by country
- THC limits may differ
- Novel Food compliance varies
- Keep documentation

### Best Practices

- Original packaging
- Lab reports accessible
- Reasonable quantities
- Avoid checked luggage if possible

---

## Air Travel Guidelines

### Carry-On Rules

**Liquids (Oils):**
- Follow 100ml rule (3.4oz)
- Place in liquids bag
- Declare if asked
- Original container preferred

**Solid Products:**
- Capsules, gummies okay
- Original packaging best
- Reasonable quantities
- May face questions

### Checked Luggage

**Considerations:**
- Temperature fluctuations
- Less accessible if questions
- Risk of loss/damage
- Generally allowed for legal CBD

### TSA/Security

**What to Expect:**
- May be flagged for testing
- Explain it's legal CBD
- Show lab reports if asked
- Remain calm and cooperative

---

## Documentation to Bring

### Essential Documents

**Recommended:**
- Lab report (COA)
- Product packaging
- Proof of purchase
- Letter from doctor (if applicable)

### Why Documentation Helps

- Proves it's legal CBD
- Shows THC content
- Demonstrates legitimacy
- May speed up any questions

## International Travel

### High-Risk Destinations

**Exercise Extreme Caution:**

| Region | Risk Level |
|--------|------------|
| Middle East | Very High |
| Asia (most) | Very High |
| Russia | Very High |
| Africa (varies) | High |
| South America (varies) | Moderate |

### Moderate-Risk Destinations

**Research Carefully:**
- Australia (prescription only)
- New Zealand (restrictions)
- Some European countries
- Canada (legal, different rules)

### Lower-Risk Destinations

**Generally More Tolerant:**
- Most of Western Europe
- Some US states (domestic)
- Switzerland
- But always verify current laws

---

## Tips for International Travel

### Safest Approach

**Consider:**
- Not bringing CBD at all
- Purchasing at destination (if legal)
- Using THC-free products only
- Carrying minimal amounts

### If You Must Bring CBD

**Preparation:**
- Research extensively
- Use isolate (no THC)
- Bring full documentation
- Be prepared to discard if needed

### At Customs

**If Questioned:**
- Be honest
- Show documentation
- Remain calm
- Accept local decisions

---

## Product Recommendations for Travel

### Best for Travel

**THC-Free Options:**
- CBD isolate products
- Verified zero THC
- Broad-spectrum (with COA confirming)

**Travel-Friendly Formats:**
- Capsules (no liquid concerns)
- Small oil bottles (<100ml)
- Gummies (less obvious)

### Avoid for International Travel

**Higher Risk:**
- Full-spectrum products
- Products without clear COA
- Large quantities
- Vape products (often more scrutinised)

**Considerations:**

## My Take

Travel with CBD requires more preparation than most people expect. My honest advice: if travelling internationally, seriously consider leaving CBD at home unless you've thoroughly verified it's legal at your destination. The risk of customs issues, confiscation, or worse simply isn't worth it in many cases. For domestic travel, keeping original packaging, having your COA accessible, and following liquid rules usually makes things straightforward. When in doubt, don't bring it.
