# Low-Quality CBD: How to Identify and Avoid

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Low-quality CBD products may contain less CBD than labelled, harmful contaminants, or be derived from poorly sourced hemp. Identifying these products requires checking third-party testing, researching brands, and understanding red flags that indicate quality problems.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Studies show many CBD products mislabelled
- Contaminants common in untested products
- Price alone doesn't indicate quality
- Third-party testing is essential
- Research brands before purchasing

---

## The Quality Problem

### Industry Studies Show

Research has consistently found:
- 26-43% of products have less CBD than claimed
- Some contain more THC than legal
- Contaminants found in many products
- Synthetic cannabinoids occasionally detected
- Label accuracy varies widely

### Why It Happens

- Unregulated supplement market
- Low barriers to entry
- Cost-cutting measures
- Lack of standardisation
- Insufficient oversight

---

## Signs of Low-Quality CBD

### Red Flags

**No Third-Party Testing**
- COA unavailable
- In-house testing only
- Refuses to provide documentation
- Test results don't match product

**Label Issues**
- No CBD content listed
- Vague "hemp" claims
- Missing batch numbers
- Incomplete ingredient lists

**Price Concerns**
- Significantly cheaper than competitors
- "Too good to be true" deals
- Bulk discounts with unknown origin
- No price explanation for low cost

**Company Red Flags**
- No physical address
- Unresponsive customer service
- No company history
- Anonymous ownership
- Excessive health claims

---

## Quality Problems to Watch For

### Potency Issues

**Mislabelling:**
- Less CBD than claimed (most common)
- More THC than legal limit
- Wrong spectrum type
- Inconsistent between batches

**Impact:**
- Ineffective product
- Legal concerns
- Dosing difficulties
- Wasted money

### Contaminant Problems

**What May Be Present:**
- Pesticide residues
- Heavy metals
- Residual solvents
- Microbial contamination
- Synthetic additives

**Impact:**
- Health risks
- Chronic exposure harm
- Unexpected effects
- No therapeutic benefit

## How to Verify Quality

### Essential Checks

1. **Request COA** — Recent, batch-specific
2. **Verify lab** — Accredited, independent
3. **Match numbers** — COA to product batch
4. **Check potency** — Within 10% of label
5. **Review contaminants** — Full panel, all pass

### Research the Company

- How long in business?
- Physical location verifiable?
- Customer reviews authentic?
- Transparent about sourcing?
- Responsive to questions?

### Product Evaluation

- Professional packaging?
- Complete labelling?
- Clear dosing instructions?
- Batch number present?
- Expiration date included?

## Price vs Quality

### Why Cheap Can Be Expensive

Low prices often mean:
- Inferior hemp source
- Skipped testing
- Poor extraction methods
- Diluted products
- Quality shortcuts

### Fair Price Indicators

Quality CBD typically costs:
- Oils: £0.05-0.15 per mg CBD
- Significantly cheaper warrants scrutiny
- Premium brands charge more
- Testing adds cost

## Where to Buy Quality CBD

### Safer Sources

- Established brands with history
- Companies with transparent testing
- Retailers who verify suppliers
- Direct from verified manufacturers

### Higher Risk Sources

- Unknown online sellers
- Convenience stores
- Unverified marketplaces
- Social media sellers
- "Too good to be true" deals

## My Take

The CBD market has real quality problems. However, quality products absolutely exist—you just need to verify. Spend ten minutes checking a COA and researching a company before purchase. The difference between quality and low-quality CBD is significant for both safety and effectiveness.
