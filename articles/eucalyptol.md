## Quick Summary

Eucalyptol (1,8-cineole) is a minty, camphor-scented terpene found in eucalyptus, rosemary, and cannabis. Known for respiratory benefits and anti-inflammatory properties, eucalyptol has extensive research supporting its use for breathing support and cognitive enhancement.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Primary component of eucalyptus oil (up to 90%)
- Well-researched for respiratory support
- Cooling, minty aroma with camphor notes
- Anti-inflammatory and potentially cognitive-enhancing
- Found in many over-the-counter respiratory products

---

## What Is Eucalyptol?

Eucalyptol (also called 1,8-cineole) is a cyclic monoterpene that comprises up to 90% of eucalyptus oil. It's one of the most thoroughly researched terpenes due to its widespread medicinal use.

Natural sources:
- **Eucalyptus** — Primary source
- **Rosemary** — Significant content
- **Bay leaves** — Culinary source
- **Tea tree** — Contributing to aroma
- **Cannabis** — Minor component in some strains

---

## Eucalyptol's Effects and Benefits

### Respiratory Support

Extensive research shows:
- **Bronchodilator effects** — Opens airways
- **Mucolytic properties** — Thins mucus
- **Anti-inflammatory in airways**
- Used in cough medicines and inhalants globally

### Cognitive Enhancement

Emerging research suggests:
- May improve attention and cognitive performance
- Potential neuroprotective effects
- Studied for dementia-related research

### Anti-Inflammatory Effects

Research demonstrates:
- Reduction of inflammatory markers
- Potential pain-relieving properties
- May enhance effects of other anti-inflammatories
