# How to Spot Fake CBD

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Fake or fraudulent CBD products include those with no actual CBD, significantly less CBD than claimed, dangerous contaminants, or synthetic cannabinoids. Spot fakes by verifying third-party lab reports, researching the brand, checking for unrealistic claims, and being wary of unusually low prices. The CBD market has improved but counterfeit and mislabeled products still exist.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Fake CBD is still a problem
- No lab report = high risk
- Unrealistic claims are red flags
- Too cheap often means fake
- Verify before you buy

---

## Types of Fake CBD

### No CBD at All

**Hemp Seed Oil Scams:**
- Labelled as "CBD oil"
- Contains hemp seed oil only
- Hemp seed has no CBD
- Legally misleading

### Significantly Under-Dosed

**Less Than Claimed:**
- Label says 1000mg
- Actually contains 200mg
- Most common fraud type
- Technically has CBD, just not enough

### Contaminated Products

**Dangerous Additions:**
- Pesticides
- Heavy metals
- Residual solvents
- Microbial contamination

### Synthetic Cannabinoids

**Fake Cannabis Compounds:**
- Lab-made synthetics
- Can be dangerous
- Not actual CBD
- Serious health risks

## Warning Signs

### No Lab Reports

**Major Red Flag:**
- Can't find COA
- Company won't provide
- Only "internal testing"
- No third-party verification

**Why It Matters:**
No legitimate CBD company sells without third-party testing.

### Unrealistic Claims

**Medical Claims:**
- "Cures cancer"
- "Treats disease"
- "Guaranteed results"
- "FDA approved" (it's not)

**Why It's a Problem:**
Legitimate brands don't make disease claims—it's illegal and unethical.

### Suspiciously Low Prices

**Price Comparison:**
- Significantly below market
- Too good to be true
- "Wholesale" to consumers
- Deep discounts on high amounts

**Reality:**
Quality CBD has minimum production costs. Extremely cheap = suspicious.

### Poor Packaging/Labelling

**Signs of Fakes:**
- Unprofessional appearance
- Spelling errors
- Vague ingredient lists
- No batch numbers
- Missing company information

---

## Verification Process

### Step 1: Check for COA

**Must Have:**
- Third-party lab report
- Accessible on website or product
- Current date
- Batch-specific

**If Missing:**
Don't buy.

### Step 2: Research the Company

**Verify:**
- Physical address exists
- Contact information works
- Company history
- Online presence

### Step 3: Compare Claims to Reality

**Check:**
- CBD content vs. COA
- Claims vs. legal allowances
- Testimonials vs. verified reviews
- Price vs. market rates

### Step 4: Inspect the Product

**When Received:**
- Professional packaging
- Matches website images
- Batch number present
- Seal intact

## Common Fake CBD Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Hemp Seed Oil Mislabelling

**What Happens:**
- Product called "Hemp Oil"
- Implies CBD content
- Actually hemp seed oil
- No cannabinoids present

**How to Spot:**
- Check for actual "CBD" on label
- Look for mg of CBD stated
- Hemp seed oil ≠ CBD oil

### Scenario 2: Gas Station CBD

**What Happens:**
- Sold at convenience stores
- Unknown brands
- No verification possible
- Often mislabeled or fake

**How to Spot:**
- No reputable presence online
- Can't find lab reports
- Very low prices
- Generic packaging

### Scenario 3: Online Marketplace Fakes

**What Happens:**
- Third-party sellers on marketplaces
- Counterfeit products
- No accountability
- Variable quality

**How to Spot:**
- Buy direct from brands
- Verify seller legitimacy
- Check reviews carefully
- Compare to official products

## The Price Test

### What Quality CBD Costs

**Reasonable Range:**
- £0.05-0.15 per mg CBD
- Quality testing costs money
- Proper extraction costs money
- Compliance costs money

### Red Flag Pricing

**Be Suspicious If:**
- Under £0.03 per mg
- "75% off" regular prices
- Buy one get three free
- Unlimited supply deals

### Why Cheap = Risky

**Either:**
- Less CBD than claimed
- Poor quality sourcing
- No proper testing
- Cutting corners somewhere

---

## Lab Report Red Flags

### Signs of Fake COAs

**Concerning Elements:**
- Non-existent lab
- Template looks generic
- No contact information
- Dates don't match
- Results too perfect

### How to Verify

**Check:**
- Lab name is searchable
- Lab has accreditation
- Contact lab directly if unsure
- Results seem realistic

## Quick Checklist

### Before Buying

- [ ] Third-party COA exists
- [ ] Lab is accredited/verifiable
- [ ] Company has physical address
- [ ] Contact information works
- [ ] No disease cure claims
- [ ] Price is reasonable
- [ ] Reviews exist and are varied
- [ ] Product is clearly labelled

### If Buying Online

- [ ] Website is professional
- [ ] Secure checkout
- [ ] Return policy stated
- [ ] Company is established

## If You've Bought Fake CBD

### Steps to Take

1. **Stop using it** — Don't continue
2. **Document everything** — Photos, receipts
3. **Request refund** — From seller
4. **Report if needed** — To authorities
5. **Share your experience** — Warn others

### Where to Report

**UK:**
- Trading Standards
- MHRA (if health claims)

**US:**
- FDA
- FTC
- State attorney general

## My Take

The CBD market has improved significantly, but fakes and frauds still exist. Your best protection is simple: only buy from brands that provide third-party lab reports from accredited labs. If a company can't or won't show you testing, they're not worth your money or risk. The few extra minutes spent verifying can save you from wasting money on sugar water labeled as CBD, or worse, consuming something actually harmful.
