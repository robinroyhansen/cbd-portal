# CBD for Rabbits: Safety, Dosing, and What to Know

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Answer

CBD for rabbits is **not well-studied** and should be approached with extreme caution. Rabbits have unique digestive systems and metabolisms that differ significantly from dogs and cats. If considering CBD for your rabbit, consult an exotic animal veterinarian first. There is no established safe dosage, and potential risks may outweigh unproven benefits.

---

## The Current State of Knowledge

### Research Status
- **Zero clinical studies** on CBD in rabbits
- No established safety profile
- No dosing guidelines exist
- Extrapolating from other species is risky

### Why Rabbits Are Different
- Hindgut fermenters (unique digestive system)
- Very sensitive to dietary changes
- Different drug metabolism than carnivores
- May respond unpredictably to cannabinoids

---

## Potential Concerns

### Digestive System Sensitivity
Rabbits have extremely sensitive GI systems:
- CBD oils contain carrier oils
- Carrier oils may disrupt gut flora
- GI stasis is a serious, potentially fatal condition
- Any new supplement carries risk

### Unknown Metabolism
- Rabbit liver enzymes differ from dogs/cats
- CBD processing may be different
- Metabolites and effects unpredictable
- No data on safe dosing

### Lack of Product Testing
- No CBD products tested on rabbits
- Concentrations designed for larger animals
- Accurate micro-dosing difficult

---

## Why People Consider CBD for Rabbits

Common reasons owners inquire:
- Anxiety (during travel, vet visits)
- Arthritis in older rabbits
- Pain management
- Seizures
- End-of-life comfort care

**Important:** Effective, rabbit-safe alternatives exist for many of these conditions. Consult your exotic vet.

---

## If You're Considering CBD

### Veterinary Consultation First
- Find a rabbit-experienced (exotic) vet
- Discuss the specific condition
- Ask about evidence-based treatments
- Get professional guidance on any supplementation

### Questions for Your Vet
1. Is there evidence this could help my rabbit's condition?
2. Are there proven alternatives?
3. What are the potential risks?
4. If we try it, what signs should I watch for?

---

## Safer Alternatives to Consider

### For Anxiety
- Proper handling techniques
- Environmental enrichment
- Bonded companion rabbit
- Prescription anxiolytics if needed

### For Arthritis/Pain
- Weight management
- Joint supplements (glucosamine, etc.)
- Pain medications from vet
- Environmental modifications

### For GI Issues
- Proper diet (hay-based)
- Probiotics designed for rabbits
- Address underlying causes
- Veterinary care for GI stasis

---

## Warning Signs in Rabbits

If you give CBD (against advice), watch for:
- Decreased appetite (serious in rabbits)
- Reduced fecal output
- Lethargy
- GI stasis signs
- Behavioral changes

**Any appetite change in a rabbit is an emergency.** Seek veterinary care immediately.

---

## Product Considerations

If your vet approves a trial:

### What to Look For
- CBD isolate (no THC)
- Minimal carrier oil
- Third-party tested
- Designed for small animals

### What to Avoid
- Full-spectrum products (THC risk)
- Products with additives
- Products designed for dogs (wrong concentration)
- Anything with xylitol or artificial sweeteners

---

## Theoretical Dosing

**Note:** No safe dose is established. This is not a recommendation.

Some exotic vets who have experimented suggest:
- Starting at tiny fractions of small-dog doses
- Perhaps 0.5-1mg CBD
- Observing closely for any adverse effects
- Stopping immediately if problems occur

**This is not medical advice and may not be safe for your rabbit.**

## When Might CBD Be Considered?

Potentially reasonable scenarios (with vet approval only):
- Terminal illness comfort care
- When all conventional options exhausted
- Under close veterinary supervision
- As part of palliative care plan

Even then, the decision should be made with full awareness of unknowns.
