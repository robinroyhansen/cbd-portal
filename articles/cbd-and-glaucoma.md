By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | Studies reviewed: 0 in database, historical research referenced
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Short Answer

There is **insufficient evidence** to support using CBD for glaucoma treatment. In fact, preliminary research suggests CBD may actually **increase intraocular pressure** in some cases, potentially worsening the condition. While THC has shown some ability to reduce eye pressure, CBD alone does not appear to share this effect. People with glaucoma should consult their ophthalmologist before using any cannabinoid products.

## Understanding Glaucoma

[Glaucoma](/glossary/glaucoma) is a group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, often due to abnormally high intraocular pressure. Key points:

- Leading cause of irreversible blindness
- Often no symptoms until vision loss occurs
- Requires consistent pressure management
- Standard treatments: eye drops, medications, surgery

---

## The THC vs CBD Distinction

When people hear "cannabis helps glaucoma," they're usually referring to research on THC:

**THC:**
- Has shown ability to reduce IOP in some studies
- Effect lasts only 3-4 hours
- Would require dosing every few hours
- Psychoactive effects make this impractical
- American Academy of Ophthalmology does not recommend

**CBD:**
- Does not appear to reduce IOP
- May potentially increase IOP (concerning finding)
- Should not be assumed to work like THC
- Not recommended for glaucoma

---

## Concerning Research

A 2018 study from Indiana University found:

- CBD eye drops **increased** intraocular pressure by 18% in mice
- THC eye drops decreased IOP by up to 30%
- CBD combined with THC blocked THC's pressure-lowering effects

While mouse studies don't directly translate to humans, this raises significant concerns about CBD for glaucoma.

## My Take

I'll be direct: **I do not recommend CBD for glaucoma management**. This isn't a situation where the evidence is simply limited—there are actual warning signs that CBD might be counterproductive for this condition. 

Glaucoma is too serious for experimentation. Vision loss from glaucoma is irreversible. While I'm generally supportive of exploring CBD for various conditions, this is one where the risk-benefit analysis doesn't favor CBD use.

If you have glaucoma and are curious about cannabinoids, please discuss with your ophthalmologist. Don't substitute CBD products for prescribed glaucoma medications.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can CBD help with glaucoma?

Current evidence does not support CBD for glaucoma treatment. Unlike THC, CBD has not demonstrated ability to reduce intraocular pressure and may potentially increase it.

### I've heard cannabis helps glaucoma. Why not CBD?

Cannabis research on glaucoma typically studied THC, not CBD. These are different compounds with different effects. THC may reduce eye pressure temporarily, but CBD does not appear to share this property.

### Is it safe to use CBD if I have glaucoma?

The 2018 research suggesting CBD may increase intraocular pressure is concerning. If you have glaucoma, consult your ophthalmologist before using any CBD products.

### What should I use for glaucoma instead?

Follow your ophthalmologist's recommendations. Proven treatments include prescription eye drops, oral medications, laser treatments, and surgery. These have extensive research supporting their efficacy and safety.
