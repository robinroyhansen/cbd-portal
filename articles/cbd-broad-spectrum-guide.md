# Broad-Spectrum CBD: The THC-Free Full Plant Option

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Broad-spectrum CBD contains most hemp compounds—cannabinoids and terpenes—but with THC removed. It's the middle ground between full-spectrum's complete profile and isolate's pure CBD, offering entourage benefits without THC concerns.

---

## What Is Broad-Spectrum CBD?

### Definition

Contains:
- CBD (primary cannabinoid)
- Other cannabinoids (CBG, CBN, CBC)
- Terpenes
- Flavonoids
- NO detectable THC

### Compared to Other Types

Position:
- Full-spectrum: all compounds including THC
- Broad-spectrum: all compounds minus THC
- Isolate: pure CBD only

---

## How THC Is Removed

### Processing Methods

Techniques:
- Chromatography separation
- Additional distillation
- Selective compound removal
- Quality varies by method

### Resulting Product

What remains:
- CBD and other cannabinoids
- Most terpenes preserved
- Flavonoids maintained
- THC below detection (<0.01%)

## Product Quality Considerations

### THC Removal Quality

Varies:
- Some processes remove more than THC
- Terpenes may be affected
- Quality matters significantly
- Verify with testing

### What to Look For

Quality markers:
- Third-party tested
- THC "ND" (non-detectable)
- Cannabinoid profile listed
- Terpenes preserved
- Reputable brand

### Reading COAs

Verify:
- THC truly non-detectable
- Other cannabinoids present
- Terpenes listed
- Not just isolate marketed as broad

## My Take

Broad-spectrum is the smart choice for anyone who faces drug testing or simply wants to avoid THC entirely. You get most of the entourage effect benefits without the THC concern.

The key is choosing quality products—some "broad-spectrum" products are poorly processed with terpenes removed. Look for products with documented cannabinoid and terpene profiles, not just "THC-free" claims.
