# CBD Side Effects: What You Need to Know

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

CBD is generally well-tolerated with a good safety profile. Most side effects are **mild and dose-dependent**: dry mouth, drowsiness, diarrhea, and reduced appetite are most common. The most important concern is **drug interactions**. Serious side effects are rare at typical doses.

| **Drowsiness** | Common (dose-dependent) | Mild |
| **Diarrhea** | Occasional | Mild |
| **Reduced appetite** | Occasional | Mild |

## Side Effects in Detail

### Dry Mouth

- Most frequently reported
- Also called "cottonmouth"
- CBD may affect saliva production
- Mild and manageable

**What to do:**
- Stay hydrated
- Keep water nearby
- Usually diminishes with continued use

### Drowsiness/Fatigue

- Dose-dependent
- Higher doses more likely to cause
- Not everyone experiences this
- Can be beneficial for sleep use

**What to do:**
- Take in evening if drowsy
- Reduce dose if problematic during day
- Don't drive until you know how CBD affects you

### Diarrhea/GI Effects

- Usually temporary
- Often early in use
- May relate to carrier oils
- Dose-dependent

**What to do:**
- Start with low dose
- Take with food
- Switch products if persistent
- Usually resolves with continued use

### Reduced Appetite

- Opposite of THC's munchies
- Not everyone experiences
- Usually mild
- May be welcome or unwelcome

### Changes in Weight

- Can go either direction
- Usually minimal
- May relate to appetite changes
- Monitor if concerned

## Drug Interactions

### The Most Important Concern

CBD can interact with many medications:
- Affects liver enzyme system (CYP450)
- Same enzymes process many drugs
- Can increase or decrease medication levels
- Some interactions potentially serious

### High-Risk Medications

| Medication Type | Interaction Risk |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Blood thinners (Warfarin)** | High |
| **Anti-seizure drugs** | High |
| **Immunosuppressants** | High |
| **Sedatives/Benzodiazepines** | Moderate |
| **Antidepressants** | Moderate |
| **Blood pressure medications** | Moderate |

### The Grapefruit Rule

If your medication warns against grapefruit, CBD may also interact:
- Same enzyme system affected
- Useful screening tool
- Check with pharmacist
- Err on side of caution

### What to Do

1. **List all medications** you take
2. **Check for grapefruit warnings**
3. **Consult pharmacist** (they're excellent for interactions)
4. **Inform your doctor** about CBD use
5. **Start low** if approved to use with medications
6. **Monitor for changes** in how medications affect you

## Less Common Side Effects

### Liver Enzyme Changes

- Seen in high-dose studies (clinical trials)
- Monitored in Epidiolex patients
- Usually reversible
- Uncommon at typical wellness doses

**Who should be cautious:**
- Those with liver conditions
- Those taking liver-metabolized drugs
- Those using very high doses

### Low Blood Pressure

- CBD may lower blood pressure slightly
- Usually mild
- Could cause lightheadedness
- Relevant if already on BP medications

### Mood Changes

- Rarely reported
- Usually positive, but not always
- Individual variation exists
- Monitor your response

## What the Research Shows

### Clinical Trial Data

High-dose CBD (like Epidiolex) showed:
- Drowsiness/sedation (10-20% of patients)
- Decreased appetite (10-15%)
- Diarrhea (10-15%)
- Elevated liver enzymes (some patients)
- Most effects dose-dependent

### Real-World Use

At typical wellness doses (20-50mg):
- Side effects generally milder
- Many people experience none
- Individual variation significant
- Most tolerate well

## Who Should Be Careful

### Consult Doctor First If:

- **Pregnant or nursing** — CBD not recommended
- **Taking medications** — Interaction check needed
- **Liver conditions** — Possible liver effects
- **Scheduled for surgery** — May affect anesthesia/bleeding
- **Mental health conditions** — Monitor response
- **Children** — Medical supervision recommended

### Special Populations

| Group | Concern |
|-------|---------|
| **Pregnant/nursing** | Avoid—insufficient safety data |
| **Children** | Medical supervision only |
| **Elderly** | Start very low, more drug interaction risk |
| **Liver disease** | May affect liver, use caution |

## Minimizing Side Effects

### Best Practices

1. **Start low** — 10-20mg initially
2. **Increase gradually** — Give your body time
3. **Take with food** — May reduce GI effects
4. **Note timing** — Evening if drowsy
5. **Track response** — Keep simple log
6. **Use quality products** — Avoid contaminants

### If Side Effects Occur

- **Reduce dose** — Often resolves the issue
- **Change timing** — Morning vs. evening
- **Try different form** — Oil vs. capsule
- **Take with food** — Helps some GI issues
- **Consider break** — Then restart lower
- **Consult provider** — If persistent or concerning

## Long-Term Safety

### What We Know

- No evidence of serious long-term effects
- No addiction or dependence
- Tolerance doesn't seem to develop significantly
- Years of use in clinical populations

### What We Don't Know

- Very long-term effects (decades)
- Effects in specific populations
- Optimal long-term dosing
- Research is ongoing

## Key Takeaways

1. **CBD is generally safe** with mild side effects
2. **Drug interactions** are the main concern
3. **Drowsiness and dry mouth** most common
4. **Start low, go slow** minimizes issues
5. **Check medications** — especially blood thinners
6. **Consult healthcare provider** if concerned

## My Take

CBD has an excellent safety profile compared to most substances people use for similar purposes. The side effects are generally mild and manageable—dry mouth and drowsiness being the most common.

The real concern is drug interactions. If you take medications, especially blood thinners, anti-seizure drugs, or immunosuppressants, checking interactions is not optional—it's essential. Talk to your pharmacist; they're specialists in exactly this question.

For most people taking no significant medications, CBD side effects are a non-issue at typical doses. Start low, see how you respond, and adjust from there. Most people tolerate CBD very well.
