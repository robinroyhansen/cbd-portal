## Quick Summary

Camphene is a pungent, earthy terpene found in conifer trees, cannabis, and various herbs. Research suggests significant cardiovascular benefits, including potential to lower cholesterol and triglycerides. It also shows anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

## Key Takeaways

- Earthy, musky, camphor-like aroma
- Promising cardiovascular research (cholesterol, triglycerides)
- Strong antioxidant properties
- Anti-inflammatory effects
- Found in cypress, valerian, camphor, and cannabis

---

## What Is Camphene?

Camphene is a bicyclic monoterpene with a distinctive earthy, slightly pungent aroma. Historically used as lamp fuel before petroleum, it's now valued for potential therapeutic properties.

Natural sources:
- **Cypress oil** — Significant content
- **Camphor tree** — Part of complex profile
- **Valerian** — Contributing to sedative herb
- **Nutmeg** — Spice component
- **Cannabis** — Various strains

## Camphene's Effects and Benefits

### Cardiovascular Research

Promising studies show:
- May lower cholesterol levels
- Potential to reduce triglycerides
- Antioxidant effects protecting cardiovascular tissue
- Could offer statin-like benefits without side effects

### Antioxidant Properties

Research demonstrates:
- Strong free radical scavenging
- Cell protection from oxidative damage
- Potential anti-aging benefits

### Anti-Inflammatory Effects

Studies indicate:
- Reduction of inflammatory markers
- May complement other anti-inflammatory compounds
- Potential applications in inflammatory conditions
