# CBD Laws in Europe: Complete Guide by Country

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

CBD laws across Europe vary by country, but most EU member states allow hemp-derived CBD with THC below 0.2-0.3%. The Novel Food Regulation affects CBD product sales, requiring authorisation for most CBD foods and supplements. Switzerland has more liberal laws allowing up to 1% THC.

---

## Key Takeaways

- EU generally permits hemp-derived CBD with <0.2% THC
- Novel Food Regulation impacts CBD food/supplement sales
- Individual countries may have additional requirements
- Switzerland allows higher THC (1%)
- UK follows similar framework post-Brexit

---

## EU Regulatory Framework

### Novel Food Regulation

Key requirements:
- CBD extracts classified as Novel Foods
- Products need authorisation for legal sale
- Many applications currently pending
- Enforcement varies by country

### THC Limits

- Current EU standard: 0.2%
- Moving toward 0.3% harmonisation
- Applies to final products
- Hemp cultivation limits separate

## Travelling with CBD in Europe

### Within Schengen

- Generally permitted
- Keep documentation
- Stay within THC limits
- Check airline policies

### Cross-Border Considerations

- Products should comply with destination laws
- Carry original packaging and COA
- Be prepared for questions

---

## My Take

Europe is generally CBD-friendly, but the patchwork of regulations requires attention to detail. Stick with reputable brands that comply with Novel Food requirements and verify THC content. When travelling, bring documentation and be conservative.
