## Quick Summary

Nerolidol is a sesquiterpene with a woody, floral, citrus aroma found in ginger, jasmine, and cannabis. Known for sedative properties, skin penetration enhancement, and potential antiparasitic effects, nerolidol is particularly valuable in CBD products designed for sleep and topical applications.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Woody, floral aroma with fresh bark and citrus notes
- Significant sedative and relaxing properties
- Enhances skin penetration of other compounds
- Potential antiparasitic and antimicrobial effects
- Found in ginger, jasmine, tea tree, and cannabis

---

## What Is Nerolidol?

Nerolidol (also called peruviol) is a sesquiterpene alcohol found in many flowers and plants. It exists in two forms (cis and trans) with slightly different properties.

Natural sources:
- **Neroli (orange blossoms)** — Named for this source
- **Ginger** — Contributing to complex aroma
- **Jasmine** — Floral component
- **Tea tree** — Part of medicinal profile
- **Cannabis** — Present in various strains

## Nerolidol's Effects and Benefits

### Sedative Properties

Research indicates:
- Significant sedative effects in animal studies
- May promote relaxation and sleep
- Potential anxiolytic properties
- Useful for evening/nighttime products

### Penetration Enhancement

Important for topicals:
- Increases skin absorption of other compounds
- May enhance bioavailability of CBD in topicals
- Valued in transdermal formulations

### Antimicrobial and Antiparasitic

Studies show:
- Activity against various pathogens
- Antiparasitic properties (malaria research)
- Potential antifungal effects
