# CBD Winterization: Removing Fats and Waxes

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Winterization is a purification process that removes fats, waxes, and lipids from crude CBD extract by dissolving it in alcohol and freezing. The unwanted materials solidify and are filtered out, resulting in a cleaner, more concentrated extract.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Removes fats, waxes, lipids
- Uses cold and filtration
- Improves extract quality
- Essential for many products
- Precedes distillation

---

## Why Winterization Is Needed

### What's in Crude Extract

Hemp extracts contain:
- Cannabinoids (desired)
- Terpenes (often desired)
- Fats and lipids
- Waxes
- Plant chlorophyll

### Problems with Fats/Waxes

- Cloudy appearance
- Harsh taste
- Reduces purity
- Clogs vape cartridges
- Inconsistent texture

---

## The Winterization Process

### Steps

1. **Dissolve** — Crude extract in high-proof ethanol
2. **Freeze** — Solution placed in freezer (24-48 hours)
3. **Solidify** — Fats and waxes precipitate
4. **Filter** — Remove solid materials
5. **Evaporate** — Remove ethanol
6. **Result** — Cleaner extract

### Temperature Requirements

- At least -20°C (-4°F)
- Colder is better
- Some use -40°C
- Duration affects results

---

## When Winterization Is Used

### Typically Required For

- CO2 extraction (captures lipids)
- Ethanol extraction
- Any full-spectrum product
- Pre-distillation processing

### Sometimes Skipped For

- Some hydrocarbon extracts
- Products where waxes acceptable
- Crude for further processing

---

## Impact on Final Product

### Benefits

- Cleaner appearance
- Smoother taste
- Better for vaping
- More stable products
- Higher concentration

### Potential Drawbacks

- Removes some terpenes
- Additional processing cost
- Ethanol residue possible
- Slight yield loss

---

## Quality Indicators

### Well-Winterized Extract

- Clear appearance
- Smooth consistency
- No waxy residue
- Good flow at room temperature

### Poorly Winterized

- Cloudy or hazy
- Waxy texture
- Harsh taste
- Separation in products

---

## My Take

Winterization is a behind-the-scenes process most consumers don't think about, but it significantly affects product quality. Well-winterized extracts are cleaner, taste better, and work better in final products. It's one of many steps that separate quality manufacturers from shortcuts.
