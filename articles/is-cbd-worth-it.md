# Is CBD Worth It? Cost-Benefit Analysis

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Whether CBD is "worth it" depends on your goals, response, product quality, and budget. For conditions with good evidence (like anxiety), many find value. For others, it may be expensive placebo. Start small, track results objectively, and let your personal experience guide the decision.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Value depends on personal response
- Strong evidence for some uses
- Quality products cost more
- Track effects to evaluate
- Individual decision based on results

## The Cost Reality

### What CBD Costs

**Typical Prices:**
- Entry level: £0.05-0.08/mg
- Mid-range: £0.08-0.12/mg
- Premium: £0.12-0.20/mg

**Monthly Cost (30mg/day):**
- Entry: £45-72/month
- Mid-range: £72-108/month
- Premium: £108-180/month

### Compared to Alternatives

| Option | Monthly Cost |
|--------|--------------|
| CBD oil | £50-150 |
| Gym membership | £30-80 |
| Therapy session | £50-150 each |
| OTC sleep aids | £10-30 |
| Prescription meds | Varies |

## Where CBD Has Most Value

### Strongest Evidence

**Epilepsy:**
- FDA-approved (Epidiolex)
- Proven effective
- Prescription required
- Clear value for those who need it

### Good Evidence

**Anxiety:**
- Multiple positive studies
- Many user reports
- Worth trying
- Good value if works for you

### Developing Evidence

- Sleep (promising)
- Pain (mixed)
- Other conditions (early)

## How to Evaluate Value for You

### The Trial Approach

1. **Set specific goal** — What are you hoping for?
2. **Start small** — Don't overspend initially
3. **Track results** — Be objective
4. **Give adequate time** — 4+ weeks
5. **Assess honestly** — Did it help?

### Questions to Answer

- Did my target symptom improve?
- Is the improvement worth the cost?
- Could I achieve this another way?
- Do benefits justify continuing?

---

## Making CBD More Affordable

### Cost-Saving Strategies

- Buy larger bottles (lower per-mg cost)
- Subscribe for discounts
- Find minimum effective dose
- Compare brands on per-mg basis
- Look for sales/promotions

### What Not to Sacrifice

- Quality (cheap often ineffective)
- Third-party testing
- Reputable brands
- Don't chase lowest price blindly

- Not tracking or evaluating

## My Take

CBD is worth it for some people, not for others—and that's fine. The key is approaching it honestly: set specific goals, track results objectively, and make decisions based on your actual experience rather than marketing promises. If it helps you meaningfully and fits your budget, it's worth it. If not, that money might be better spent elsewhere.
