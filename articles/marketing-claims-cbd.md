# CBD Marketing Claims: Regulations and Reality

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

CBD marketing is heavily regulated, with strict rules against health claims, disease treatment assertions, and misleading advertising. Understanding these regulations helps consumers identify legitimate products and avoid companies making illegal claims that may indicate broader quality issues.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Health claims heavily restricted
- Disease treatment claims illegal
- "Structure/function" claims limited
- Regulations protect consumers
- Excessive claims = red flag

---

## Regulatory Framework

### Why Claims Are Regulated

**Consumer Protection:**
- Prevent misleading information
- Ensure accurate expectations
- Protect vulnerable consumers
- Maintain market integrity

### Key Regulators

**EU:**
- EFSA (health claims)
- National authorities
- Novel Food regulations
- Advertising standards

**UK:**
- ASA (advertising)
- FSA (food)
- MHRA (medicines)
- Trading Standards

**US:**
- FDA (primary)
- FTC (advertising)
- State regulators

---

## What's Not Allowed

### Disease Claims

**Prohibited Examples:**
- "Cures cancer"
- "Treats epilepsy"
- "Prevents diabetes"
- "Heals arthritis"

**Why Prohibited:**
- Makes CBD a drug legally
- Requires medical approval
- Misleads consumers
- Potentially dangerous

### Unsubstantiated Health Claims

**Examples:**
- "Clinically proven to reduce pain"
- "Scientifically verified anxiety relief"
- "Doctor recommended for sleep"

**Problems:**
- May not be true
- Misleads consumers
- Often exaggerated
- Rarely verified

---

## What's Permitted (Generally)

### General Wellness Language

**May Be Acceptable:**
- "Supports overall wellness"
- "Part of a healthy lifestyle"
- "Natural plant compound"
- Ingredient/composition facts

### Truthful Information

- Cannabinoid content
- Ingredients
- Usage instructions
- Product descriptions

### Consumer Testimonials

**With Caveats:**
- Must be genuine
- Cannot make health claims
- Must include disclaimers
- Varies by jurisdiction

---

## Country-Specific Rules

### EU/UK

**Health Claims Regulation:**
- Specific approved claims only
- No CBD health claims authorised
- Novel Food considerations
- Strict enforcement

### US

**FDA Position:**
- CBD in food/supplements unclear
- Disease claims prohibited
- Warning letters issued
- Enforcement active

**FTC Requirements:**
- Advertising must be truthful
- Substantiation required
- Testimonials regulated
- Penalties for violations

---

## Red Flags for Consumers

### Concerning Claims

**Watch For:**
- "Cures" or "treats" language
- Specific disease mentions
- Miracle/breakthrough claims
- "FDA approved" (generally false)
- Celebrity health endorsements

### What It Indicates

**Companies Making Illegal Claims:**
- May ignore other regulations
- Quality possibly questionable
- Regulatory risk
- Desperate marketing

---

## Evaluating Marketing

### Credibility Checklist

**Trustworthy Marketing:**
- [ ] No disease claims
- [ ] Focuses on product quality
- [ ] Provides COAs
- [ ] Transparent about limitations
- [ ] Compliant with regulations

**Questionable Marketing:**
- [ ] Miracle language
- [ ] Disease treatment claims
- [ ] Before/after photos (health)
- [ ] Unverified statistics
- [ ] Urgency/fear tactics

---

## Understanding "Structure/Function" Claims

### What They Are

**Permitted (with caveats):**
- Describes supplement's role in body
- Not disease treatment
- General function support

**Examples:**
- "Supports calm"
- "Promotes relaxation"
- "Assists with recovery"

### Requirements

- Must be truthful
- Not misleading
- Substantiation needed
- Disclaimer often required

---

## Research References

### Legitimate Use

**Acceptable:**
- Referencing published studies
- Not claiming product does same
- Proper context
- Accurate representation

**Problematic:**
- Cherry-picking results
- Implying product equivalence
- Misrepresenting conclusions
- Overstating findings

## Consumer Protection Tips

### Be Sceptical Of

- Guaranteed results
- Quick fixes
- "The only product that..."
- Celebrity medical endorsements
- Pressure tactics

### Look For

- Quality focus
- Transparency
- Testing documentation
- Honest limitations
- Regulatory compliance
