# Understanding CBD Certificates of Analysis (COAs)

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

A Certificate of Analysis (COA) is a lab report that verifies what's in a CBD product. Learning to read COAs protects you from mislabeled products and ensures you're getting what you pay for.

---

## What Is a COA?

### Definition

Certificate of Analysis:
- Third-party lab test results
- Verifies product contents
- Checks for contaminants
- Industry standard document

### Why It Matters

Importance:
- CBD industry inconsistently regulated
- Many products mislabeled
- COA provides verification
- Protects consumers

---

## Key Elements of a COA

### Cannabinoid Profile

What it shows:
- CBD content (should match label)
- THC content (should be <0.3%)
- Other cannabinoids present
- Amounts in mg or percentage

### Contaminant Testing

Safety checks:
- Pesticides
- Heavy metals
- Residual solvents
- Microbial contamination

### Lab Information

Credibility markers:
- Accredited lab name
- Testing date
- Lab contact information
- Analyst signature

### Batch Information

Traceability:
- Product batch number
- Should match your product
- Manufacturing date
- Ensures you're reading correct COA

---

## How to Read Cannabinoid Results

### CBD Content

Verify:
- Look for "CBD" or "Cannabidiol"
- Compare mg amount to label claim
- Should be within 10% of label
- Higher is fine, lower is concerning

### THC Content

Check:
- Must be under 0.3% (legal limit)
- "ND" means not detected
- LOQ means below testing limit
- Any THC if concerned about drug tests

### Other Cannabinoids

Full-spectrum products:
- Should show multiple cannabinoids
- CBG, CBN, CBC common
- Minor cannabinoids listed
- Verifies full-spectrum claims

---

## Contaminant Testing

### Pesticides

What to look for:
- "Pass" or "ND" for all
- List of pesticides tested
- Should test common ones
- Any "Fail" is concerning

### Heavy Metals

Common tests:
- Arsenic
- Cadmium
- Lead
- Mercury
- All should be below limits

### Residual Solvents

From extraction:
- Ethanol, butane, etc.
- Should be minimal or none
- "Pass" is what you want
- Relevant to extraction method

### Microbial

Contamination:
- Mold and yeast
- E. coli
- Salmonella
- Should all pass

---

## Red Flags

### Missing Information

Concerns:
- No batch number
- No date
- Missing contaminant tests
- Partial results only

### Questionable Results

Watch for:
- CBD far from label claim
- THC above 0.3%
- Failed contaminant tests
- Unclear or confusing format

### Outdated Testing

Timing issues:
- Very old test dates
- Different batch than product
- No regular testing indicated
- Freshness concerns

---

## Where to Find COAs

### Company Website

Best practice:
- Most reputable companies post online
- Searchable by batch number
- Easy to access
- Transparency indicator

### QR Code on Product

Modern approach:
- Scan code on packaging
- Links directly to COA
- Convenient verification
- Real-time access

### Request from Company

If not available:
- Email customer service
- They should provide readily
- Refusal is red flag
- Takes more effort

---

## Accreditation Matters

### Lab Quality

Look for:
- ISO 17025 accreditation
- State licensing
- DEA registration (for THC testing)
- Reputable lab name

### Why It Matters

Quality assurance:
- Accredited labs follow standards
- More reliable results
- Proper equipment and methods
- Accountability

---

## Quick Verification Checklist

### Before Buying

Verify:
- [ ] COA available?
- [ ] Recent date?
- [ ] CBD matches label (+/- 10%)?
- [ ] THC under 0.3%?
- [ ] Contaminant tests pass?
- [ ] Accredited lab?

### Match to Product

Confirm:
- [ ] Batch number matches?
- [ ] Correct product tested?
- [ ] Current batch (not old)?

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Always check COA** before buying
2. **CBD should match** label claim
3. **THC must be** under 0.3%
4. **Contaminant tests** should pass
5. **Accredited labs** more reliable

---

## My Take

Reading COAs is the single most important thing you can do to ensure CBD product quality. The industry has too many mislabeled products, and COAs are your protection.

Don't buy from companies that don't provide readily accessible COAs. It only takes a few minutes to verify, and it's worth the effort for peace of mind about what you're putting in your body.
