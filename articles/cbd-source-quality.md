# CBD Source Quality: From Seed to Product

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

The quality of CBD products begins with the hemp source. Factors including genetics, growing conditions, farming practices, and supply chain transparency all impact the final product. Understanding source quality helps identify truly premium CBD products.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Hemp genetics determine cannabinoid potential
- Growing conditions affect quality significantly
- Organic and sustainable practices matter
- Supply chain transparency indicates quality
- European hemp often meets higher standards

---

## Hemp Genetics

### Why Genetics Matter

Starting material determines:
- Cannabinoid content potential
- Terpene profiles
- Plant health and yield
- Contaminant resistance

### Quality Genetic Indicators

**Certified Seeds:**
- EU certified varieties
- Known cannabinoid profiles
- Consistent results
- Legal compliance assured

**Breeding Quality:**
- High-CBD genetics
- Low THC (<0.2-0.3%)
- Good terpene expression
- Disease resistance

---

## Growing Conditions

### Soil Quality

**Importance:**
- Hemp is a bioaccumulator
- Absorbs what's in soil
- Clean soil = clean product
- Contaminated soil = contaminated product

**Quality Indicators:**
- Soil testing documentation
- No industrial history
- Agricultural certification
- Regular monitoring

### Climate Factors

**Optimal Conditions:**
- Adequate sunshine
- Proper rainfall/irrigation
- Moderate temperatures
- Appropriate growing season

**Impact on Quality:**
- Cannabinoid development
- Terpene expression
- Plant health
- Harvest timing

---

## Farming Practices

### Organic Cultivation

**Benefits:**
- No synthetic pesticides
- Natural fertilisers
- Environmental sustainability
- Cleaner end product

**Certification Levels:**
- Certified organic (USDA, EU)
- Transitional organic
- "Organic practices" (uncertified)
- Conventional

### Sustainable Methods

**Regenerative Farming:**
- Soil health focus
- Biodiversity support
- Carbon sequestration
- Long-term sustainability

**Quality Practices:**
- Crop rotation
- Natural pest management
- Water conservation
- Minimal environmental impact

---

## Geographic Considerations

### European Hemp

**Advantages:**
- Strict EU regulations
- Certified seed varieties
- Quality oversight
- Lower THC limits (0.2%)

**Key Regions:**
- Switzerland
- Netherlands
- Italy
- France
- Eastern Europe

### US Hemp

**Considerations:**
- State-by-state regulation
- 0.3% THC limit
- Variable quality oversight
- Large-scale production

**Quality Regions:**
- Colorado
- Oregon
- Kentucky
- Vermont

### Other Regions

**Emerging Sources:**
- Canada
- China (variable quality)
- Various developing markets

**Due Diligence Required:**
- Verify growing practices
- Confirm testing standards
- Check regulatory compliance

## Supply Chain Transparency

### Traceability

**Quality Companies Provide:**
- Seed source information
- Farm location/practices
- Extraction facility details
- Batch tracking throughout
- Complete documentation

**Why It Matters:**
- Accountability at each stage
- Problem identification
- Quality verification
- Consumer confidence

### Vertical Integration

**Advantages:**
- Control over entire process
- Consistent quality
- Direct accountability
- Better traceability

**Indicators:**
- Own or partner farms
- In-house extraction
- Complete oversight
- Transparent about process

## Questions to Ask Brands

### About Their Source

1. Where is your hemp grown?
2. What seed varieties do you use?
3. Is it certified organic?
4. Can you provide farm information?
5. How do you ensure soil quality?

### About Their Process

1. Who does your extraction?
2. What method is used?
3. How do you maintain traceability?
4. What certifications do you hold?
5. Can I visit or see documentation?

---

## Red Flags

### Concerning Indicators

- Won't disclose source location
- No farming practice information
- Unknown supply chain
- No certifications
- Suspiciously low prices
- Vague "premium hemp" claims
