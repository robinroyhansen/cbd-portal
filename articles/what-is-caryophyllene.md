# What is Caryophyllene (Beta-Caryophyllene)?

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Answer

Beta-caryophyllene (BCP) is a unique [terpene](/knowledge/what-are-terpenes) with a spicy, peppery aroma. What makes it extraordinary: it's the only terpene known to directly activate [CB2 receptors](/knowledge/cb2-receptors), making it both a terpene and a dietary cannabinoid. This dual action gives caryophyllene significant potential for anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving effects.

---

## Caryophyllene Profile

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Chemical formula** | C₁₅H₂₄ |
| **Aroma** | Spicy, peppery, woody, clove-like |
| **Boiling point** | 130°C (266°F) |
| **Prevalence in cannabis** | Very common |
| **Also found in** | Black pepper, cloves, cinnamon, hops, rosemary |

---

## What Makes Caryophyllene Special

Unlike other terpenes, beta-caryophyllene directly binds to and activates CB2 receptors—the same receptors that cannabinoids interact with.

**This means:**
- Acts as a dietary cannabinoid
- May produce cannabinoid-like effects
- Unique among all terpenes
- Adds another dimension to the entourage effect

The FDA recognizes caryophyllene as GRAS (Generally Recognized as Safe) as a food additive.

## Potential Effects and Benefits

Research on caryophyllene shows:

**Anti-inflammatory**
- Strong anti-inflammatory activity via CB2
- May reduce inflammation markers
- Studied for various inflammatory conditions

**Pain Relief**
- Analgesic properties documented
- May enhance CBD's pain-relieving effects
- Studied for chronic pain applications

**Anxiety Reduction**
- Anxiolytic effects in animal studies
- May contribute to calming experiences
- CB2 activation linked to mood

**Gastroprotective**
- May protect stomach lining
- Studied for digestive health
- Potential for ulcer prevention

**Neuroprotective**
- Brain health research ongoing
- CB2 receptor involvement in neuroprotection
- Potential for neurodegenerative conditions

## CB2 Receptor Activation

The CB2 receptor activation is what makes caryophyllene exceptional:

**What CB2 Does:**
- Modulates immune response
- Reduces inflammation
- Involves no psychoactive effects
- Present throughout the body

**Implications:**
- Caryophyllene may provide cannabinoid benefits
- No "high" (CB2 doesn't cause psychoactivity)
- Legal as a food ingredient
- Synergy with other cannabinoids

## The Black Pepper Connection

Ever heard that black pepper can calm cannabis-induced anxiety? Black pepper is rich in caryophyllene, and its CB2 activation may explain this effect. While scientific evidence for this specific use is limited, the mechanism is plausible.
