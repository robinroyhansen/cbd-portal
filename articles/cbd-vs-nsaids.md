# CBD vs NSAIDs: Comparing Anti-Inflammatory Options

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Comparison

| Factor | CBD | NSAIDs |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Strength** | Mild-Moderate | Moderate-Strong |
| **Onset** | Slower (hours/days) | Fast (30-60 min) |
| **GI Side Effects** | Minimal | Common (ulcers, bleeding) |
| **Cardiovascular** | Minimal concern | Concerns with long-term use |
| **Long-term Safety** | Good | Concerns |
| **OTC Available** | Yes | Yes |
| **Proven Efficacy** | Moderate evidence | Strong evidence |

---

## How They Work

### CBD's Anti-Inflammatory Mechanism

- Endocannabinoid system modulation
- Cytokine reduction
- Indirect anti-inflammatory effects
- Multiple pathways

### NSAID Mechanism

- COX enzyme inhibition (COX-1 and COX-2)
- Direct prostaglandin reduction
- Blocks inflammatory mediators
- Well-understood mechanism

---

## Effectiveness

### NSAIDs

Strong, proven efficacy:
- Decades of research
- Clear anti-inflammatory effects
- Rapid pain relief
- Established dosing

### CBD

Growing evidence:
- Anti-inflammatory properties documented
- Generally milder than NSAIDs
- May take longer to notice
- Individual response varies

**For acute inflammation:** NSAIDs typically more effective
**For chronic management:** CBD may be safer long-term

---

## Side Effects

### NSAID Side Effects

**Gastrointestinal (significant concern):**
- Stomach ulcers
- GI bleeding
- Heartburn
- Nausea

**Cardiovascular:**
- Increased heart attack risk (long-term)
- Stroke risk (long-term)
- Blood pressure elevation

**Kidney:**
- Kidney damage with long-term use
- Fluid retention

### CBD Side Effects

**Generally mild:**
- Dry mouth
- Drowsiness (dose-dependent)
- Diarrhea (some people)
- Drug interactions

**No known:**
- GI ulceration
- Cardiovascular risks
- Kidney damage

---

## Long-Term Safety

### NSAIDs Long-Term

Significant concerns:
- GI complications increase with duration
- Cardiovascular risk accumulates
- Not recommended for chronic daily use
- Monitoring recommended

### CBD Long-Term

Appears safer:
- No organ damage documented
- Non-addictive
- Well-tolerated over time
- May be suitable for chronic use

---

## When to Use Each

### Choose NSAIDs When:

- Need fast relief
- Acute inflammation/injury
- Short-term use (days to weeks)
- Stronger effect needed
- Proven approach preferred

### Choose CBD When:

- Long-term management needed
- GI sensitivity to NSAIDs
- Want to avoid NSAID side effects
- Milder effect acceptable
- Part of overall wellness approach

### Consider Both When:

- Using NSAIDs short-term for acute phase
- Transitioning to CBD for maintenance
- Under healthcare guidance

## For Specific Conditions

### Arthritis

**NSAIDs:** Fast relief, proven efficacy
**CBD:** Chronic management, topical + oral
**Combination:** NSAIDs for flares, CBD ongoing

### Back Pain

**NSAIDs:** Acute episodes
**CBD:** Chronic management
**Note:** Neither addresses underlying cause

### Headaches

**NSAIDs:** Generally effective
**CBD:** Limited evidence for headaches
**Recommendation:** NSAIDs usually better choice

### Sports Injuries

**NSAIDs:** Acute injury phase
**CBD:** Recovery phase
**Note:** Some prefer CBD to preserve healing (NSAIDs may impair)

---

## Drug Interactions

### NSAIDs Interact With:

- Blood thinners (increased bleeding)
- Blood pressure medications
- Certain antidepressants
- Other NSAIDs

### CBD Interacts With:

- Blood thinners (especially warfarin)
- Certain seizure medications
- Various medications via CYP450
- Some overlap with NSAIDs

## Making the Decision

### Questions to Ask

1. Is this acute or chronic?
2. Do I have GI sensitivity?
3. How fast do I need relief?
4. How long will I need treatment?
5. What other medications am I taking?
6. What's my budget?

### Decision Flow

**Acute, need fast relief:** NSAIDs
**Chronic, long-term:** Consider CBD
**GI issues:** Avoid or limit NSAIDs
**Already on blood thinners:** Caution with both

## My Take

This isn't an either/or choice for most people. NSAIDs and CBD serve different purposes and can complement each other.

For acute inflammation—a sprained ankle, sudden back pain, injury—NSAIDs work faster and more powerfully. For short-term use, the GI risks are manageable.

For ongoing inflammation management—chronic arthritis, general aches, long-term comfort—CBD makes more sense. The GI and cardiovascular concerns with long-term NSAID use are real, while CBD appears much safer for extended periods.

My practical suggestion: use NSAIDs when you need rapid, strong relief, but don't rely on them daily for months. For chronic inflammation, CBD (alongside other approaches like exercise and anti-inflammatory diet) is a safer long-term strategy.
