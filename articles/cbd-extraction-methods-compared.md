# CBD Extraction Methods Compared

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

CBD must be extracted from hemp plants to create products. Different extraction methods affect purity, quality, and price. Understanding these methods helps you evaluate CBD products.

---

## Why Extraction Method Matters

### Impact on Product

Affects:
- Purity of CBD
- Presence of other compounds
- Potential contaminants
- Overall quality
- Price point

### Main Methods

Primary approaches:
- CO2 extraction (most common for quality)
- Ethanol extraction
- Hydrocarbon extraction
- Olive oil extraction (DIY/small scale)

---

## CO2 Extraction

### How It Works

Process:
- Uses pressurized carbon dioxide
- CO2 becomes "supercritical" state
- Pulls cannabinoids from plant
- CO2 evaporates, leaving extract

### Advantages

Benefits:
- Very clean extraction
- No residual solvents
- Precise targeting possible
- Industry gold standard
- Preserves terpenes well

### Disadvantages

Drawbacks:
- Expensive equipment
- Technical expertise required
- Higher product cost
- Slower process

### Best For

Ideal when:
- Quality is priority
- Willing to pay more
- Want clean product
- Full-spectrum or broad-spectrum

---

## Ethanol Extraction

### How It Works

Process:
- Plant material soaked in ethanol
- Ethanol dissolves cannabinoids
- Alcohol evaporated off
- Extract remains

### Advantages

Benefits:
- Effective extraction
- Lower cost than CO2
- Scalable process
- GRAS solvent (Generally Recognized As Safe)

### Disadvantages

Drawbacks:
- May extract chlorophyll (affects taste)
- Requires thorough solvent removal
- Can destroy some terpenes
- Less selective than CO2

### Best For

Used when:
- Balancing cost and quality
- Large-scale production
- Isolate production
- Cost-sensitive products

---

## Hydrocarbon Extraction

### How It Works

Process:
- Uses butane, propane, or hexane
- Solvents strip cannabinoids
- Evaporation removes solvent
- Extract remains

### Advantages

Benefits:
- Efficient extraction
- Preserves terpenes well
- Good for full-spectrum
- Lower equipment cost

### Disadvantages

Concerns:
- Residual solvent risk
- Requires rigorous purging
- Safety concerns during extraction
- Quality depends on purging

### Best For

Used when:
- Terpene preservation important
- Cost matters
- Proper testing verifies purity
- Experienced manufacturers

---

## Olive Oil Extraction

### How It Works

Process:
- Heat plant material in olive oil
- Cannabinoids dissolve into oil
- Filter plant material
- Oil contains CBD

### Advantages

Benefits:
- Simple process
- No residual solvents
- Safe for home use
- Low cost

### Disadvantages

Significant drawbacks:
- Low CBD concentration
- Perishable product
- Not commercially practical
- Inconsistent potency

### Best For

Appropriate for:
- DIY extraction
- Personal use
- Not commercial products
- Experimentation

---

## Comparing Methods

### Quality Ranking

General assessment:
1. CO2 (highest quality potential)
2. Ethanol (good quality, lower cost)
3. Hydrocarbon (quality depends on purging)
4. Olive oil (lowest concentration)

### Cost Ranking

Price impact:
1. Olive oil (cheapest)
2. Ethanol (moderate)
3. Hydrocarbon (moderate)
4. CO2 (most expensive)

### Best Overall

For consumers:
- CO2 for premium products
- Ethanol for value products
- Both can produce quality with proper testing
- Method matters less than final testing

---

## What to Look For

### Product Labels

May indicate:
- "CO2 extracted" common claim
- Extraction method sometimes listed
- More common on premium products
- Not always disclosed

### More Important

Focus on:
- Third-party testing results
- Residual solvent testing
- Cannabinoid verification
- Final product quality matters most

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **CO2 extraction** is gold standard
2. **Ethanol** produces good quality at lower cost
3. **Final testing** matters more than method
4. **Residual solvents** should be tested
5. **Quality products** use good extraction + testing

---

## My Take

Extraction method matters, but testing matters more. A well-executed ethanol extraction with thorough residual solvent testing can produce excellent products. A poorly done CO2 extraction without quality control can produce inferior products.

When evaluating CBD products, extraction method is one factor, but third-party testing results showing what's actually in the final product are more important. Look for products that test clean regardless of extraction method.
