# Pesticides in CBD Products: What You Need to Know

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Pesticides used during hemp cultivation can end up in CBD products, with extraction potentially concentrating these chemicals. Testing for pesticide residues is essential for product safety, as chronic exposure to these agricultural chemicals can pose health risks.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Pesticides can concentrate during CBD extraction
- Dozens of pesticides should be screened
- "Organic" doesn't guarantee pesticide-free
- Quality products test for comprehensive panels
- Look for "ND" (not detected) results

---

## Why Pesticides Are a Concern

### From Farm to Product

Pathway to contamination:
1. Hemp treated with pesticides
2. Chemicals remain on/in plant
3. Extraction concentrates residues
4. End product contains pesticides
5. Consumer exposure occurs

### Health Implications

Chronic pesticide exposure linked to:
- Neurological effects
- Endocrine disruption
- Carcinogenic potential
- Reproductive effects
- Immune system effects

## Common Pesticide Categories

### Organophosphates

**Examples:** Chlorpyrifos, malathion

**Concerns:**
- Neurotoxic (acetylcholinesterase inhibition)
- Linked to developmental issues
- Some banned in many regions
- Can persist in products

### Pyrethroids

**Examples:** Bifenthrin, permethrin

**Concerns:**
- Insecticides from natural source
- Synthetic versions more persistent
- Neurological effects possible
- Common in agriculture

### Carbamates

**Examples:** Carbaryl, methomyl

**Concerns:**
- Similar mechanism to organophosphates
- Neurological effects
- Generally shorter persistence
- Still require testing

### Fungicides

**Examples:** Myclobutanil, paclobutrazol

**Concerns:**
- Control mould on plants
- Some converted to toxic compounds when heated
- Particularly concerning for inhalable products
- Often detected in cannabis testing

### Growth Regulators

**Examples:** Paclobutrazol, daminozide

**Concerns:**
- Used to control plant growth
- Some have health concerns
- May be inappropriately used
- Should not be present

## Testing Standards

### How Many Pesticides?

Testing panels vary:
- Basic: 20-40 pesticides
- Comprehensive: 60-100+ pesticides
- California standard: 66 pesticides
- Oregon: 59 pesticides

### Regulatory Frameworks

| Region | Pesticides Tested |
|--------|-------------------|
| California | 66 |
| Oregon | 59 |
| Colorado | 13+ |
| EU | Varies by country |

### Detection Methods

**LC-MS/MS (Liquid Chromatography)**
- Standard method
- Highly sensitive
- Detects most pesticides
- Quantifies residue levels

**GC-MS (Gas Chromatography)**
- Complementary method
- Some pesticides better detected
- Often used alongside LC-MS
- Comprehensive coverage

## Reading Pesticide Results

### What to Look For

**Ideal Results:**
- "ND" (not detected) for all
- "Pass" with no detections
- Comprehensive panel tested
- Recent test dates

**Concerning Results:**
- Any "Fail" indication
- Detections near limits
- Limited panel tested
- Missing common pesticides

### Understanding Limits

- Action limits vary by pesticide
- Some have zero tolerance
- Others allow trace amounts
- Limits based on safety assessments

---

## Organic CBD and Pesticides

### What "Organic" Means

- Synthetic pesticides not used
- Organic pesticides may be used
- Certification requirements vary
- Still should be tested

### Testing Still Important

- Cross-contamination possible
- Organic pesticides still exist
- Verification ensures claims
- Quality brands test anyway

---

## Protecting Yourself

### Verification Steps

1. Request full COA
2. Check pesticide panel is included
3. Verify comprehensive testing (40+)
4. Confirm "ND" or "Pass" results
5. Note lab accreditation

### Quality Markers

**Trustworthy:**
- Comprehensive pesticide testing
- All "ND" results
- Third-party verified
- Organic certification (bonus)

**Questionable:**
- No pesticide testing
- Limited panel only
- Detections present
- In-house testing only
