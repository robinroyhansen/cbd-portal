# How to Report CBD Side Effects

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

If you experience side effects from CBD, you can report them to regulatory authorities (like the MHRA in the UK or FDA in the US), to the product manufacturer, and to your healthcare provider. Reporting helps build safety data, may trigger investigations, and protects other consumers. Even minor side effects are worth reporting.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Reporting helps everyone
- Multiple channels available
- Both serious and minor matter
- Document thoroughly
- Keep the product

### Building Safety Data

**Each Report:**
- Adds to knowledge base
- May identify patterns
- Triggers investigations
- Informs regulation

### Protecting Others

**Your Report Could:**
- Lead to product recall
- Warn other consumers
- Improve industry standards
- Save someone from harm

## Where to Report

### UK Reporting

**MHRA Yellow Card Scheme:**
- Official UK reporting system
- Online: yellowcard.mhra.gov.uk
- Accepts supplement reports
- Free and anonymous option available

**How to Report:**
1. Visit Yellow Card website
2. Select "Report a side effect"
3. Choose appropriate category
4. Complete the form
5. Submit

### EU Reporting

**National Systems:**
- Each country has authority
- Check local health ministry
- EudraVigilance for cross-border

**Examples:**
- Germany: BfArM
- France: ANSM
- Netherlands: Lareb

### US Reporting

**FDA MedWatch:**
- www.fda.gov/medwatch
- Online form available
- Phone: 1-800-FDA-1088
- Accepts supplement reports

**Consumer Complaint:**
- FDA Safety Reporting Portal
- Documents adverse events
- May trigger investigation

---

## What Information to Include

### Essential Details

**About You:**
- Age and sex
- General health status
- Medications you take
- Health conditions

**About the Product:**
- Brand name
- Product type
- Batch/lot number
- Where purchased
- CBD concentration

**About the Side Effect:**
- What happened
- When it started
- How severe
- How long it lasted
- What you did about it

---

## Documenting Your Experience

### Keep Records

**Write Down:**
- Timeline of events
- Symptoms in detail
- Any treatment needed
- Recovery process

**Preserve:**
- The product itself
- Packaging and labels
- Purchase receipt
- Any lab results (COA)

### Photos Help

- Product appearance
- Any visible reactions
- Labels and batch codes
- Packaging details

---

## Reporting to the Manufacturer

### Contact the Brand

**Steps:**
1. Find contact information
2. Describe what happened
3. Provide product details
4. Request response
5. Document communication

### What to Expect

**Good Brands Will:**
- Respond promptly
- Take concerns seriously
- May offer refund
- May investigate batch

**If No Response:**
- Report to authorities anyway
- Share experience publicly (reviews)
- Warn others

---

## Telling Your Healthcare Provider

### Always Inform

**Your Doctor Should Know:**
- What you experienced
- What product you used
- Your response to stopping
- Any treatment needed

### Medical Documentation

- Creates official record
- May trigger formal report
- Helps your care
- Adds to medical knowledge

---

## What Counts as a Side Effect?

### Report All Concerns

**Obvious Issues:**
- Allergic reactions
- Severe symptoms
- Required medical attention
- Hospitalisation

**Also Report:**
- Unexpected effects
- Interactions suspected
- Effects not on label
- Anything concerning

### Even "Minor" Matters

- Persistent drowsiness
- Digestive issues
- Mood changes
- Any unusual symptoms

---

## Timeline for Reporting

### Report Promptly

**Ideally:**
- As soon as practical
- While details fresh
- Before discarding product
- While you can document

### Better Late Than Never

Old reports still valuable if you remember details.

---

## What Happens After Reporting

### Regulatory Process

**Your Report May:**
- Be reviewed by officials
- Join a database
- Contribute to patterns
- Trigger investigation

### You May Be Contacted

- For more information
- To clarify details
- Rarely, but possible
- Cooperation helps

---

## Anonymous Reporting

### If Concerned About Privacy

**Options:**
- Many systems allow anonymous reports
- Still valuable
- Include as much detail as possible
- Less follow-up possible
