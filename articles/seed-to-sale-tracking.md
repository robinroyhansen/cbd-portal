# Seed-to-Sale Tracking in CBD: Supply Chain Transparency

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Seed-to-sale tracking follows CBD products from hemp seed through cultivation, extraction, manufacturing, and final sale. This traceability ensures quality control, regulatory compliance, and consumer safety by documenting every step of the production process.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Complete supply chain documentation
- Enables quality issue identification
- Required in some jurisdictions
- Indicates professional operations
- Provides consumer confidence

---

## What Is Seed-to-Sale Tracking?

### Definition

A system that documents:
- Seed source and genetics
- Cultivation practices
- Harvesting and processing
- Extraction and manufacturing
- Distribution and retail

### Purpose

**Quality Assurance:**
- Track problems to source
- Verify claims
- Ensure consistency
- Enable recalls if needed

**Regulatory Compliance:**
- Meet legal requirements
- Demonstrate due diligence
- Support audits
- Maintain licenses

---

## Tracking Points

### Stage 1: Seeds/Genetics

**Documented:**
- Seed source/supplier
- Variety/strain
- Certification status
- Purchase records
- Genetic testing (if applicable)

### Stage 2: Cultivation

**Documented:**
- Farm location
- Planting dates
- Growing practices
- Inputs used (fertilizers, etc.)
- THC testing results
- Harvest dates

### Stage 3: Drying and Processing

**Documented:**
- Processing facility
- Drying methods
- Storage conditions
- Initial processing
- Biomass testing

### Stage 4: Extraction

**Documented:**
- Extraction facility
- Method used
- Batch numbers
- Input material amounts
- Output yields
- Testing results

### Stage 5: Manufacturing

**Documented:**
- Product formulation
- Manufacturing facility
- Batch/lot numbers
- Quality control tests
- Packaging dates

### Stage 6: Distribution and Sale

**Documented:**
- Distribution chain
- Storage conditions
- Retail partners
- Final sale records
- Consumer information

## For Consumers

### What to Look For

**Transparency Indicators:**
- Batch numbers on products
- QR codes linking to info
- COAs with batch matching
- Farm/source information
- Company transparency pages

### Questions to Ask

1. Can you trace my product to the farm?
2. What information is tracked?
3. How do I access product history?
4. What happens if there's a recall?

---

## Verification Steps

### Consumer Verification

1. Find batch/lot number
2. Scan QR code (if present)
3. Check company website
4. Match to COA
5. Review available documentation

### What Good Tracking Looks Like

- Batch links to specific COA
- Farm/source information available
- Extraction method documented
- Manufacturing details accessible
- Complete chain visible
