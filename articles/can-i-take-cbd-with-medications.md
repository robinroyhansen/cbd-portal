# Can I Take CBD with My Medications? Drug Interaction Guide

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Important Disclaimer

**This article is educational only—not medical advice.** Always consult your doctor or pharmacist before combining CBD with any medications. Drug interactions can be serious.

---

## Quick Answer

CBD can interact with many medications because it affects how your liver processes drugs. The main concern is **CYP450 enzyme inhibition**—CBD can make some medications stronger or longer-lasting, which may be dangerous. Always check with a healthcare provider.

---

## How CBD Causes Interactions

### The CYP450 System

Your liver uses CYP450 enzymes to process many drugs:
- CBD inhibits some of these enzymes (especially CYP3A4, CYP2D6)
- When enzymes are inhibited, medications aren't broken down as fast
- This can increase medication levels in your blood
- Higher levels may cause side effects or toxicity

### The Grapefruit Test

A helpful rule of thumb:
- **If your medication warns against grapefruit, CBD may also interact**
- Grapefruit affects the same liver enzymes as CBD
- Not perfect but a useful indicator
- Examples: many statins, some blood pressure meds, some anxiety meds

## Medications with Known/Likely Interactions

### Blood Thinners

| Medication | Interaction Risk |
|------------|-----------------|
| Warfarin (Coumadin) | **High** — CBD increases warfarin levels |
| Heparin | Moderate |
| Clopidogrel (Plavix) | Moderate |
| Rivaroxaban (Xarelto) | Moderate |

**What happens:** Increased bleeding risk. If you take warfarin, CBD can significantly increase INR levels.

**Recommendation:** Do not combine without medical supervision and monitoring.

### Anti-Epileptic Drugs

| Medication | Interaction Risk |
|------------|-----------------|
| Clobazam | **High** — well-documented |
| Valproate | Moderate to high |
| Phenytoin | Moderate |
| Carbamazepine | Moderate |

**What happens:** CBD can increase levels of clobazam significantly. This is actually studied because prescription CBD (Epidiolex) is used for epilepsy.

**Recommendation:** Only under medical supervision with regular monitoring.

### Sedatives and Benzodiazepines

| Medication | Interaction Risk |
|------------|-----------------|
| Diazepam (Valium) | Moderate |
| Lorazepam (Ativan) | Moderate |
| Alprazolam (Xanax) | Moderate |
| Zolpidem (Ambien) | Moderate |

**What happens:** Additive sedation; CBD may also slow metabolism of some benzos.

**Recommendation:** Increased sedation possible—be cautious. Don't drive or operate machinery.

### Antidepressants

| Medication | Interaction Risk |
|------------|-----------------|
| SSRIs (Prozac, Zoloft, etc.) | Moderate |
| SNRIs (Effexor, Cymbalta) | Moderate |
| Tricyclics | Moderate |
| MAOIs | Lower but caution |

**What happens:** CBD may increase antidepressant levels. Serotonin syndrome risk is low but theoretical.

**Recommendation:** Monitor for increased side effects. Inform your prescriber.

### Blood Pressure Medications

| Medication | Interaction Risk |
|------------|-----------------|
| Calcium channel blockers | Moderate |
| Beta blockers | Lower to moderate |
| ACE inhibitors | Lower |

**What happens:** CBD may lower blood pressure additionally. Some medications may have levels affected.

**Recommendation:** Monitor blood pressure. Watch for dizziness.

### Immunosuppressants

| Medication | Interaction Risk |
|------------|-----------------|
| Cyclosporine | **High** |
| Tacrolimus | High |
| Sirolimus | Moderate to high |

**What happens:** CBD can significantly increase levels of these drugs, risking toxicity.

**Recommendation:** Do not combine without medical supervision and regular blood level monitoring.

### Chemotherapy Drugs

| Interaction Risk |
|-----------------|
| Variable but potentially serious |

**What happens:** CBD may affect metabolism of some chemotherapy drugs.

**Recommendation:** Always discuss with oncologist. Do not self-add CBD during chemotherapy.

### Opioid Pain Medications

| Medication | Interaction Risk |
|------------|-----------------|
| Oxycodone | Moderate |
| Morphine | Lower |
| Fentanyl | Moderate |

**What happens:** Possible additive sedation. Some metabolic interactions.

**Recommendation:** Inform your prescriber. Never use recreationally.

---

## Medications with Lower Interaction Risk

These generally have fewer concerns (but still check):
- Over-the-counter pain relievers (ibuprofen, acetaminophen)
- Most antibiotics
- Thyroid medications
- Proton pump inhibitors

**Still inform your healthcare provider** even with lower-risk medications.

---

## Signs of Drug Interactions

Watch for:
- Unusual drowsiness or sedation
- Nausea or GI issues
- Dizziness or lightheadedness
- Increased side effects of your medication
- Changes in how medication usually affects you

If you notice these, contact your healthcare provider.

## How to Approach CBD with Medications

### Step 1: Check Your Medications

- Look for grapefruit warnings
- Research specific interactions
- Make a list of all medications and supplements

### Step 2: Consult Healthcare Providers

**Talk to:**
- Your prescribing doctor
- Your pharmacist (they're excellent for interaction checks)
- Any specialists involved in your care

**Tell them:**
- Specific medications you take
- Dose of CBD you're considering
- Why you want to try CBD

### Step 3: If Approved, Start Carefully

- Start with low CBD dose
- Time CBD and medications apart (a few hours)
- Monitor for any changes
- Keep records
- Follow up with healthcare provider

### Step 4: Ongoing Monitoring

- Some medications require blood level monitoring
- Report any side effects
- Regular check-ins with prescriber
- Adjust as needed under guidance

## Special Populations

### Elderly

Higher risk because:
- Often on multiple medications
- Slower metabolism
- More sensitive to interactions
- Extra caution required

### Those with Liver Issues

- CBD processed by liver
- Compromised liver affects both CBD and medications
- Medical supervision essential

### Pregnancy/Nursing

- CBD not recommended during pregnancy/nursing
- Medications have own considerations
- Do not add CBD

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **CBD affects liver enzymes** that process many drugs
2. **Grapefruit warning = potential CBD interaction**
3. **Blood thinners, epilepsy drugs, immunosuppressants** — high risk
4. **Always consult healthcare provider** before combining
5. **Start low and monitor** if approved
6. **Report side effects** immediately

---

## My Take

Drug interactions are the most serious consideration with CBD use. While CBD itself is generally safe, combining it with medications that are affected by CYP450 enzymes can have real consequences.

I strongly recommend talking to your pharmacist—they're specifically trained in drug interactions and often more accessible than doctors for this type of question. They can quickly check your specific medications against CBD.

If you take blood thinners, anti-seizure medications, or immunosuppressants, be especially careful. These have well-documented interactions that require medical supervision.

Don't let this scare you away from CBD if it might help you—just approach it responsibly. Get professional guidance, start carefully, and monitor your response.
