# CBD Contraindications: When to Avoid CBD

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

While CBD is generally well-tolerated, certain conditions and situations may make CBD use inadvisable. Contraindications include pregnancy, severe liver disease, certain medication combinations, and specific allergies. Understanding these helps ensure safe CBD use.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Some conditions make CBD inadvisable
- Pregnancy and breastfeeding are contraindications
- Severe liver disease requires avoidance
- Certain medications create significant risks
- Always disclose CBD use to healthcare providers

---

## Absolute Contraindications

### Pregnancy

- Crosses placental barrier
- Potential developmental effects
- Insufficient safety data
- Risk outweighs any benefit

### Breastfeeding

- Transfers to breast milk
- Effects on infant unknown
- Developing systems vulnerable
- Avoid during nursing

### Severe Liver Disease

- CBD metabolised in liver
- Impaired clearance
- Increased toxicity risk
- Avoid without specialist guidance

### Known CBD Allergy

- Rare but possible
- Previous allergic reaction
- Cross-reactivity concerns
- Choose alternatives

---

## Relative Contraindications

### Conditions Requiring Caution

**Liver Conditions**
- Hepatitis (active)
- Cirrhosis
- Elevated liver enzymes
- Requires medical supervision

**Parkinson's Disease**
- Mixed evidence
- May worsen tremor in some
- Medical guidance essential
- Individual response varies

**Mental Health Conditions**
- Bipolar disorder (potential mood effects)
- Psychosis history
- Severe depression
- Requires psychiatric oversight

---

## Medication Contraindications

### High-Risk Combinations

**Blood Thinners**
- Warfarin (significant interaction)
- Increased bleeding risk
- INR monitoring essential
- May need to avoid CBD

**Anti-Epileptics**
- Clobazam (strong interaction)
- Valproate
- Requires neurologist guidance
- Dose adjustments needed

**Immunosuppressants**
- Cyclosporine
- Tacrolimus
- Critical drug levels
- Generally avoid

---

## Situational Contraindications

### When to Avoid

- **Before surgery** — Stop 2 weeks prior (bleeding risk)
- **During acute illness** — Adds variables
- **Starting new medications** — Establish baseline first
- **Unexplained symptoms** — Diagnose first

---

## Age Considerations

### Children

- Not recommended without medical supervision
- Developing systems
- Limited safety data
- Medical guidance essential

### Elderly

- Not contraindicated but requires caution
- More medications typically
- Slower metabolism
- Start low, go slow

---

## Risk Assessment Questions

### Ask Yourself

1. Do I have liver disease?
2. Am I pregnant or nursing?
3. What medications do I take?
4. Have I had allergic reactions?
5. Do I have a history of mental health conditions?
6. Am I scheduled for surgery?

### If You Answer Yes

- Consult healthcare provider
- Discuss specific risks
- Consider alternatives
- Proceed only with guidance

---

## Alternatives When Contraindicated

### Consider Instead

- Non-CBD cannabinoids (where appropriate)
- Other natural approaches
- Conventional treatments
- Lifestyle modifications

### Discussing with Healthcare Provider

- Explain your goals
- Ask about alternatives
- Understand the specific risks
- Make informed decisions together

---

## My Take

Contraindications exist for good reasons—they protect you from harm. If you fall into a contraindicated category, respect that boundary. The potential benefits of CBD don't outweigh significant risks in these situations. Work with healthcare providers to find safe alternatives.
