# What is Eucalyptol (1,8-Cineole)?

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Answer

Eucalyptol, also known as 1,8-cineole, is a [terpene](/knowledge/what-are-terpenes) with a distinctive cooling, minty, eucalyptus aroma. As the primary compound in eucalyptus oil, it's widely used for respiratory support. Research supports anti-inflammatory, decongestant, and antimicrobial properties. It's less common in cannabis but adds refreshing qualities when present.

---

## Eucalyptol Profile

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Chemical formula** | C₁₀H₁₈O |
| **Also called** | 1,8-Cineole, cineole |
| **Aroma** | Cooling, minty, eucalyptus, camphoraceous |
| **Boiling point** | 176°C (349°F) |
| **Prevalence in cannabis** | Less common |
| **Also found in** | Eucalyptus (primary), rosemary, tea tree, sage, bay leaves |

---

## Respiratory Benefits

Eucalyptol is most famous for respiratory applications:

**Medicinal Uses:**
- Active ingredient in chest rubs (Vicks VapoRub)
- Used in cough suppressants
- Found in throat lozenges
- Prescribed for respiratory conditions in some countries

**Mechanisms:**
- Opens airways (bronchodilation)
- Clears mucus (mucolytic)
- Reduces inflammation
- Antimicrobial action

---

## Potential Effects and Benefits

Research on eucalyptol shows:

**Anti-inflammatory**
- Well-documented effects
- Respiratory inflammation studied
- Systemic anti-inflammatory activity

**Decongestant**
- Clears nasal passages
- Opens bronchial airways
- The "cooling" sensation reflects this

**Antimicrobial**
- Antibacterial properties
- Antiviral activity
- Antifungal effects

**Pain Relief**
- Analgesic properties
- Used in topical preparations
- May complement CBD for pain

**Cognitive Enhancement**
- May improve mental clarity
- Stimulating rather than sedating
- Traditional use for alertness

---

## Eucalyptol in Medicine

Unlike most terpenes, eucalyptol has pharmaceutical applications:

**Approved Uses (in some countries):**
- Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)
- Asthma (adjunct therapy)
- Sinusitis
- Bronchitis

This reflects the strength of its research base.

## Eucalyptol in CBD Products

Products with eucalyptol may be marketed as:
- Respiratory support formulas
- Invigorating or refreshing blends
- Topicals for muscle relief (cooling effect)
- Products with minty/fresh profiles

---

## Safety Considerations

Eucalyptol is generally safe but:
- High concentrations can be irritating
- Keep away from face/eyes in pure form
- Not for internal use in concentrated forms
- Follow product directions

At levels found in cannabis and CBD products, it's well-tolerated.

---

## The Cooling Sensation

Eucalyptol's characteristic cooling effect comes from:
- Activation of cold-sensing receptors (TRPM8)
- Similar mechanism to menthol
- Creates perception of coolness without actual temperature change
- Pleasant in aromatherapy and topicals

---

## Research Status

Eucalyptol is well-researched:
- Clinical trials for respiratory conditions
- Established mechanisms of action
- Safety data from pharmaceutical use
- Extensive aromatherapy research

It's one of the most scientifically validated terpenes.
