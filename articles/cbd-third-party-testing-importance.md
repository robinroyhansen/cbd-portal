# Why Third-Party Testing Matters for CBD

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Third-party testing is the most important quality indicator for CBD products. Without independent lab verification, you can't know what's actually in a product—and studies show many CBD products are mislabeled.

---

## The Problem

### Mislabeling Is Common

Studies show:
- Many CBD products don't match labels
- Some contain less CBD than claimed
- Some contain more THC than legal
- Contaminants found in some products

### Why It Happens

Contributing factors:
- Limited regulation
- No mandatory testing (in most places)
- Unscrupulous manufacturers
- Quality control failures
- Rapid industry growth

---

## What Is Third-Party Testing?

### Definition

Independent verification:
- Testing by external laboratory
- No financial connection to brand
- Objective analysis
- Results in Certificate of Analysis (COA)

### How It Works

Process:
- Brand sends samples to lab
- Lab tests for various compounds
- Results documented in COA
- COA should be publicly available

---

## What Labs Test For

### Cannabinoid Profile

Primary test:
- CBD content (should match label)
- THC content (should be legal)
- Other cannabinoids present
- Verifies label accuracy

### Contaminants

Safety tests:
- Pesticides
- Heavy metals (lead, mercury, etc.)
- Residual solvents
- Microbial contamination (mold, bacteria)

### Potency Verification

Accuracy:
- Total mg of CBD
- Percentage of CBD
- Per-serving amounts
- Consistency with claims

---

## Why Independent Testing Matters

### Objectivity

Importance:
- In-house testing has conflict of interest
- Third-party labs are objective
- No incentive to fudge results
- Trustworthy verification

### Credibility

Trust factor:
- Reputable brands invest in testing
- Transparency shows confidence
- Builds consumer trust
- Industry standard for quality

### Safety

Protection:
- Identifies contaminants
- Ensures legal THC levels
- Verifies actual contents
- Protects your health

---

## Reading a COA

### Key Elements

Look for:
- Lab name and accreditation
- Date of testing
- Batch number (should match product)
- Cannabinoid results
- Contaminant results

### What to Verify

Checklist:
- CBD matches label (+/- 10%)
- THC below 0.3% (legal limit)
- Contaminant tests pass
- Recent test date
- Batch matches your product

### Red Flags

Warning signs:
- No batch number
- Very old date
- Missing contaminant tests
- Results don't match label

---

## Finding COAs

### Where to Look

Sources:
- Brand website (common location)
- QR code on product
- Email request to company
- Should be readily available

### If Not Available

Concerns:
- Major red flag
- Don't buy without COA
- Company should provide easily
- Lack of transparency is telling

---

## Lab Quality Matters

### Accreditation

Look for:
- ISO 17025 certification
- State licensing
- DEA registration (for THC testing)
- Known, reputable lab name

### Why Accreditation Matters

Quality assurance:
- Follows standardized methods
- Proper equipment calibration
- Quality control procedures
- Accountable results

---

## Cost of Testing

### Industry Investment

Reality:
- Testing costs money
- Legitimate brands invest
- Part of quality products
- Cheap products often skip

### Reflected in Price

Consideration:
- Very cheap products may lack testing
- Quality costs more
- Value includes safety
- Worth the investment

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Third-party testing** is non-negotiable
2. **Many products** are mislabeled without it
3. **COA should be** readily available
4. **Verify cannabinoid** and contaminant results
5. **No COA = don't buy**

---

## My Take

Third-party testing is the single most important thing to look for in CBD products. Without it, you're trusting the manufacturer's claims completely—and studies show those claims are often inaccurate.

Never buy CBD without accessible third-party testing results. It only takes a few minutes to verify, and it's your assurance that you're getting what you pay for and that it's safe to use.
