By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

## Quick Comparison

| Factor | CBD | Valerian |
|--------|-----|----------|
| **Source** | Hemp plant | Valeriana officinalis root |
| **Type** | Cannabinoid | Herbal extract |
| **Primary Use** | Anxiety, pain, sleep | Sleep, mild anxiety |

---

## How They Work

### CBD's Mechanism

CBD works through multiple pathways:
- Endocannabinoid system modulation
- Serotonin receptor interaction
- GABA enhancement
- Anxiolytic effects support sleep

CBD addresses underlying anxiety that prevents sleep rather than directly inducing drowsiness.

### Valerian's Mechanism

Valerian works primarily through:
- GABA receptor modulation
- Increased GABA availability
- Mild sedative effect
- Adenosine receptor interaction

Valerian has a more directly sedating approach.

## For Sleep

### CBD for Sleep

**How it helps:**
- Reduces anxiety that prevents sleep
- Promotes relaxation
- May extend sleep duration
- Non-sedating relaxation

**Best for:**
- Anxiety-related insomnia
- Racing mind at bedtime
- Those who don't want sedation
- Sleep issues with other symptoms

### Valerian for Sleep

**How it helps:**
- Mild sedative effect
- Increases GABA
- Traditional sleep remedy
- Promotes drowsiness

**Best for:**
- Simple sleep initiation issues
- Those who respond to herbs
- Wanting direct sleep support
- Traditional herbal approach

## Research Comparison

### CBD Sleep Research

- Growing evidence base
- Promising but not conclusive
- May work through anxiety reduction
- Mechanism increasingly understood

### Valerian Sleep Research

- Decades of studies
- Results are mixed
- Some show benefit, others don't
- Meta-analyses inconclusive
- "May help" is the general conclusion

**Reality:** Neither has overwhelming evidence, but both have enough support to be worth trying.

## For Anxiety

### CBD for Anxiety

**Strengths:**
- Multiple mechanisms of action
- Research specifically for anxiety
- Versatile—works for different types
- Can be used as needed

### Valerian for Anxiety

**Limited use:**
- Primarily a sleep herb
- Some mild anxiolytic effect
- Less research for anxiety specifically
- Sedation may not be wanted

**Winner for Anxiety:** CBD has more support for anxiety as a primary concern.

## Side Effects

### CBD Side Effects

Generally mild:
- Dry mouth
- Drowsiness (dose-dependent)
- Potential drug interactions
- Mild GI effects sometimes

### Valerian Side Effects

Generally mild:
- Morning grogginess
- Headache (some people)
- GI upset
- Vivid dreams
- Strong smell/taste

### The Smell/Taste Factor

Valerian has a notoriously unpleasant smell:
- Often described as "dirty socks"
- Can make capsules preferable to tea
- Some find it hard to tolerate
- CBD is much more palatable

## Onset and Duration

### CBD

- **Onset:** 30-60 minutes (sublingual faster)
- **Duration:** 4-6 hours
- **Best timing:** 1-2 hours before bed

### Valerian

- **Onset:** 30-60 minutes
- **Duration:** 4-6 hours
- **Best timing:** 30-60 minutes before bed

Similar timing profiles for both.

---

## Drug Interactions

### CBD Interactions

- Affects CYP450 enzymes
- Multiple potential interactions
- Check with pharmacist
- More interaction potential

### Valerian Interactions

- Fewer documented interactions
- May enhance sedatives
- Avoid with other CNS depressants
- Generally milder interaction profile

## Using Together

### Can You Combine Them?

Yes, with considerations:
- Both affect GABA system
- May enhance sedation
- Start with lower doses of each
- Monitor for excessive drowsiness

### When Combination Makes Sense

- Single ingredient doesn't work well enough
- Different mechanisms may help
- As part of sleep stack
- Under guidance if on medications

## Which to Try First?

### Try Valerian First If:

- Budget is a primary concern
- You want to try the traditional approach
- Sleep is your only issue
- You tolerate herbs well
- Simplicity appeals

### Try CBD First If:

- Anxiety contributes to sleep issues
- You have pain or other symptoms
- You dislike valerian's smell/taste
- You want more versatility
- You've tried valerian without success

### Consider Both If:

- Neither alone is sufficient
- You want comprehensive support
- Different nights need different approaches
- Budget allows

## My Take

For pure sleep support without other concerns, valerian is worth trying first—it's cheap, it's been used for centuries, and it works for some people. The main downside is the awful smell and taste, which capsules solve.

But if your sleep issues stem from anxiety, racing thoughts, or stress, CBD is likely the better choice. Valerian is more of a "knock you out" approach, while CBD is more "calm you down so you can sleep naturally."

The research on both is honestly not great—lots of "may help" and mixed results. But both are safe to try, and individual responses vary enough that personal experimentation is warranted.

My practical suggestion: try valerian first (it's cheaper). If it works, great. If not, or if you need something more versatile, try CBD. Some people end up using both—valerian occasionally when they just need sleep, CBD more regularly for anxiety and stress management.
