## What Is CBG?

### The "Mother Cannabinoid"

CBG is unique:
- CBGA (acidic form) is the precursor to other cannabinoids
- CBD, THC, and CBC all derive from CBGA
- As plant matures, CBGA converts to other cannabinoids
- Only ~1% CBG remains in mature hemp
- Must harvest early or use special strains for higher CBG

### Why It's Called Mother

Biosynthesis pathway:
1. Plant produces CBGA
2. Enzymes convert CBGA to THCA, CBDA, or CBCA
3. Heat converts these to THC, CBD, CBC
4. Little CBG remains

## How They Work

### CBD's Mechanism

CBD works indirectly:
- Modulates endocannabinoid system (doesn't bind directly)
- Interacts with serotonin receptors (5-HT1A)
- Affects GABA system
- Multiple receptor interactions
- Broad, diffuse effects

### CBG's Mechanism

CBG works more directly:
- Partial agonist at CB1 and CB2 receptors
- More direct endocannabinoid binding
- Also interacts with alpha-2 adrenergic receptors
- May affect GABA reuptake
- Different receptor profile than CBD

## Research Status

### CBD Research

Extensive body of research:
- Thousands of studies
- FDA-approved medication (Epidiolex)
- Human clinical trials for multiple conditions
- Well-understood safety profile
- Established evidence base

### CBG Research

Growing but earlier stage:
- Fewer studies
- Mostly preclinical (animal/cell)
- Limited human trials
- Promising but preliminary
- Less understood safety profile (long-term)

**Reality:** CBG shows promise but lacks CBD's research depth.

## Key Differences in Effects

### CBD Effects (User Reports)

- Calming, relaxing
- Can be mildly sedating at higher doses
- Anxiety reduction
- General wellness support
- Subtle effects

### CBG Effects (User Reports)

- More focused, alert feeling
- Less sedating than CBD
- "Clear-headed" description common
- May have more noticeable effect
- Different qualitative experience

Some users prefer CBG for daytime use due to non-sedating profile.

## Cost Comparison

### Why CBG Costs More

| Factor | Impact on Price |
|--------|-----------------|
| Low natural abundance (~1%) | Higher extraction cost |
| Special cultivation needed | More expensive farming |
| Early harvest required | Lower overall yield |
| Smaller market scale | Less economy of scale |

**Typical prices:**
- CBD oil: $0.05-0.10/mg
- CBG oil: $0.10-0.20/mg

CBG often costs 2x or more compared to CBD.

## Using CBD and CBG Together

### The Entourage Effect

Cannabinoids may work better together:
- Different receptor profiles complement
- May enhance each other's effects
- Some products combine both
- "Broad-spectrum" may include some CBG

### Combination Products

- CBG:CBD ratio products available
- Common ratios: 1:1, 1:2, 1:4
- May provide balanced effects
- Worth trying if single cannabinoid isn't sufficient

## Which to Choose?

### Choose CBD If:

- You want established research backing
- Cost is a consideration
- Anxiety or sleep is primary concern
- You want proven safety profile
- New to cannabinoids

### Choose CBG If:

- You want to try something different
- CBD alone hasn't been sufficient
- Focus/alertness is preferred over sedation
- Willing to pay premium
- Interested in gut health applications

### Consider Both If:

- You want comprehensive support
- Single cannabinoid isn't enough
- Interested in entourage effect
- Budget allows

## Safety Comparison

### CBD Safety

Well-established:
- Generally well-tolerated
- Known side effects (mild)
- Drug interaction profile understood
- Long track record

### CBG Safety

Less established:
- Appears well-tolerated (limited data)
- Side effects not fully characterized
- Drug interactions less studied
- Shorter use history

CBG appears safe but has less safety data than CBD.

## Dosing Comparison

### CBD Dosing

- 20-50mg typical for wellness
- 50-100mg+ for specific issues
- Well-documented ranges

### CBG Dosing

- 10-30mg typical starting point
- Optimal doses less established
- Start low due to limited research
- Often used in combination

## Key Takeaways

1. **CBD is well-researched**, CBG is promising but earlier stage
2. **CBG costs more** due to low natural abundance
3. **Different effects** — CBG may be less sedating
4. **CBG called "mother cannabinoid"** — precursor to others
5. **Can use together** for potential entourage effect
6. **Start with CBD** if new to cannabinoids

## My Take

CBD is the sensible starting point for most people—it has far more research, a proven safety profile, and costs less. If CBD works for you, there's no need to change.

CBG is interesting if you want to experiment or if CBD alone hasn't met your needs. The less-sedating, more "focused" profile some people report makes it appealing for daytime use. But recognize you're in more experimental territory with less research backing.

If trying CBG, start with small amounts and see how you respond. Or try a combination product that includes both cannabinoids for potential synergy.

The main reasons to consider CBG: CBD isn't working well enough for you, you prefer a non-sedating option, or you're interested in its potential gut health applications. Otherwise, stick with the better-researched, more affordable CBD.
