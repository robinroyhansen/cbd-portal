## Choosing Product Type

### Oils/Tinctures

Best for:
- Flexible dosing
- Sublingual use
- Fast onset
- Experienced users

### Capsules/Softgels

Best for:
- Precise dosing
- Convenience
- No taste preference
- Travel-friendly

### Gummies/Edibles

Best for:
- Pleasant taste
- Discreet use
- Beginners
- Consistent doses

### Topicals

Best for:
- Localized issues
- No systemic effects
- Skin application
- Targeted relief

## Quality Verification

### Non-Negotiables

Must have:
- Third-party COA available
- CBD content verified
- Contaminant testing passed
- Recent test dates

### How to Verify

Steps:
1. Find COA (QR code, website)
2. Match batch number
3. Check cannabinoid content
4. Review contaminant results

---

## Spectrum Selection

### Full-Spectrum

Choose if:
- Not drug tested
- Want maximum benefit
- Okay with trace THC
- Believe in entourage effect

### Broad-Spectrum

Choose if:
- Drug tested
- Want multiple cannabinoids
- Need zero THC
- Best of both worlds

### Isolate

Choose if:
- Strictly drug tested
- Want pure CBD only
- Sensitive to other compounds
- Precise CBD only

## Dosing Considerations

### Starting Doses

Recommendations:
- Beginners: 20-25mg daily
- Increase gradually
- Give time to evaluate
- Individual calibration

### Strength Selection

Choosing potency:
- Lower for new users
- Higher for experienced
- Calculate per-serving cost
- Match to expected use

## My Take

The CBD market has improved, but you still need to be a discerning shopper. The good news is that quality products are more available than ever; the challenge is identifying them among products that still cut corners.

Focus on verification: if a company provides clear, recent third-party testing that matches their claims, they're likely legitimate. If they can't or won't provide this, move on. There are plenty of transparent companies making quality products.
