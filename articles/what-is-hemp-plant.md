# What Is Hemp? Understanding the Plant

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Hemp is a variety of Cannabis sativa L. cultivated for industrial and wellness purposes. Unlike marijuana, hemp contains very low THC (below 0.2-0.3%) and is the source of legal CBD products. The plant has been used for thousands of years for fibre, food, and medicine.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Hemp is Cannabis sativa with low THC (<0.2-0.3%)
- Legal distinction from marijuana based on THC content
- Source of CBD, fibre, seeds, and more
- Thousands of years of human use
- One of the most versatile crops

---

## Botanical Classification

### Scientific Name

**Cannabis sativa L.**
- Same species as marijuana
- Different varieties/cultivars
- Legal distinction based on THC
- Not a separate species

### Hemp vs Marijuana

| Factor | Hemp | Marijuana |
|--------|------|-----------|
| THC content | <0.2-0.3% | 5-30%+ |
| CBD content | Often high | Variable |
| Primary use | CBD, fibre, seeds | Intoxication |
| Legal status | Generally legal | Mostly controlled |

## Plant Anatomy

### Key Parts

**Flowers (Buds)**
- Highest cannabinoid concentration
- Source of CBD extracts
- Trichome-covered
- Female plants most valuable

**Leaves**
- Lower cannabinoid content
- Used in some extracts
- Fan leaves vs sugar leaves
- Iconic cannabis shape

**Stalks**
- Source of hemp fibre
- Outer bast fibre (strong)
- Inner hurd (absorbent)
- Industrial applications

**Seeds**
- Nutritious food source
- Hemp seed oil
- No cannabinoids (in seed itself)
- High in omega fatty acids

**Roots**
- Deep tap root system
- Soil remediation potential
- Traditional medicine use
- Not commonly used today

## Cannabinoid Production

### How Hemp Makes CBD

1. Plant produces CBGa (precursor)
2. Enzymes convert to CBDa
3. Heat/time converts CBDa to CBD
4. Concentration highest in flowers
5. Trichomes contain most cannabinoids

### Other Compounds

**Cannabinoids:**
- CBD (primary)
- CBG, CBC, CBN
- Trace THC (below legal limit)
- 100+ identified cannabinoids

**Terpenes:**
- Aromatic compounds
- Contribute to effects
- Myrcene, limonene, etc.
- Create unique profiles

**Flavonoids:**
- Antioxidant properties
- Colour pigments
- Potential health benefits
- Research ongoing

## Historical Use

### Ancient History

**Thousands of Years:**
- Chinese medicine (2737 BCE documented)
- Fibre for textiles and rope
- Seeds for food
- Spiritual and medicinal uses

### Modern History

**20th Century Decline:**
- Marijuana prohibition affected hemp
- 1937 US Marihuana Tax Act
- Industrial use diminished
- Confusion with marijuana

**21st Century Revival:**
- 2018 US Farm Bill
- EU cultivation permits
- CBD industry growth
- Industrial renaissance

## Hemp Cultivation

### Growing Requirements

**Climate:**
- Temperate regions ideal
- 60-80 days of frost-free weather
- Moderate rainfall
- Good sunlight

**Soil:**
- Well-draining
- Moderate fertility
- pH 6.0-7.0
- Clean (no contaminants)

### Growing Practices

**For CBD Production:**
- Female plants only
- Prevent pollination
- Harvest at peak cannabinoid content
- Careful drying and curing

**For Fibre/Seed:**
- Dense planting
- Different harvest timing
- Male and female plants
- Different varieties selected

- Low water requirements
- Natural pest resistance
- Improves soil health
- Carbon negative potential

**Compared to Other Crops:**

## Legal Framework

### EU Regulations

- Approved varieties list
- THC limit 0.2% (moving to 0.3%)
- Cultivation permits required
- Novel Food regulations for CBD

### US Regulations

- 2018 Farm Bill legalised
- THC limit 0.3%
- State-by-state rules vary
- FDA still developing CBD rules
