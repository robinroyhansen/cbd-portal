# What Are Terpenes? The Complete Guide to Cannabis Aromatics

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

## Quick Summary

Terpenes are aromatic compounds found in cannabis and thousands of other plants, responsible for distinctive scents and potentially significant therapeutic effects. In CBD products, terpenes contribute to the "entourage effect"—the theory that cannabis compounds work better together than in isolation.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Terpenes are aromatic compounds that give plants their scents and flavours
- Cannabis contains over 200 different terpenes
- Each terpene has unique potential therapeutic properties
- Terpenes may enhance CBD's effects through the entourage effect
- Full-spectrum CBD products contain natural terpenes; isolates do not

## What Are Terpenes?

Terpenes are organic compounds produced by plants, primarily for defence against predators and attraction of pollinators. They're responsible for the distinctive smells of pine forests, lavender fields, citrus fruits, and cannabis.

### Basic Chemistry

Terpenes are built from isoprene units (C₅H₈):

- **Monoterpenes** — Two isoprene units (C₁₀H₁₆): limonene, pinene, myrcene
- **Sesquiterpenes** — Three isoprene units (C₁₅H₂₄): caryophyllene, humulene
- **Diterpenes** — Four isoprene units: less common in cannabis

### Terpenes vs Terpenoids

These terms are often used interchangeably, but technically:

- **Terpenes** — Pure hydrocarbons
- **Terpenoids** — Terpenes modified by oxidation or other processes
- In practice, the distinction rarely matters for CBD consumers

## Why Terpenes Matter for CBD

### The Entourage Effect

The entourage effect theory suggests that cannabis compounds—cannabinoids, terpenes, and flavonoids—work synergistically. Research indicates:

- Terpenes may modulate cannabinoid effects
- Certain terpenes may enhance CBD absorption
- The combination may be more effective than isolated compounds
- Different terpene profiles create different experiences

### Beyond Aroma

Terpenes aren't just about smell. Research suggests they have independent therapeutic properties:

| Terpene | Associated Effects |
|---------|-------------------|
| Myrcene | Relaxation, sedation |
| Limonene | Mood elevation, stress relief |
| Linalool | Anti-anxiety, calming |
| Pinene | Alertness, focus |
| Caryophyllene | Anti-inflammatory, pain relief |
| Humulene | Anti-inflammatory, appetite suppression |

## Major Cannabis Terpenes

### Myrcene

The most abundant cannabis terpene:
- **Aroma:** Earthy, musky, fruity
- **Effects:** Relaxing, potentially sedating
- **Also found in:** Mangoes, hops, lemongrass

### Limonene

The citrus terpene:
- **Aroma:** Lemon, orange, citrus
- **Effects:** Uplifting, mood-enhancing
- **Also found in:** Citrus fruit peels

### Linalool

The lavender terpene:
- **Aroma:** Floral, lavender, slightly spicy
- **Effects:** Calming, anti-anxiety
- **Also found in:** Lavender, mint, cinnamon

### Pinene

The forest terpene:
- **Aroma:** Pine, fresh, earthy
- **Effects:** Alert, focused, clear-headed
- **Also found in:** Pine trees, rosemary

### Caryophyllene

The spicy terpene (unique CB2 activator):
- **Aroma:** Peppery, spicy, woody
- **Effects:** Anti-inflammatory, pain relief
- **Also found in:** Black pepper, cloves

### Humulene

The hoppy terpene:
- **Aroma:** Earthy, woody, slightly spicy
- **Effects:** Anti-inflammatory, appetite suppression
- **Also found in:** Hops, sage

## Minor Cannabis Terpenes

Beyond the major terpenes, cannabis contains many minor terpenes that contribute to overall effects:

- **Terpinolene** — Floral, herbal; potentially sedating
- **Ocimene** — Sweet, herbaceous; possibly uplifting
- **Bisabolol** — Floral, nutty; anti-inflammatory
- **Eucalyptol** — Minty, camphor; respiratory support
- **Geraniol** — Rose-like; antioxidant properties
- **Nerolidol** — Woody, floral; potentially sedating

## Terpenes in CBD Products

### Full-Spectrum CBD

Contains the full range of hemp compounds:
- Natural terpene profile preserved
- Entourage effect potential
- May have trace THC (under 0.2% in EU)
- Flavour reflects natural terpenes

### Broad-Spectrum CBD

THC removed, other compounds retained:
- Terpenes usually preserved
- Entourage effect possible (without THC)
- Processing may reduce some terpenes
- Variable quality between products

### CBD Isolate

Pure CBD only:
- No natural terpenes
- Some products have terpenes re-added
- No entourage effect from natural compounds
- Consistent, predictable CBD content

### Terpene-Enhanced Products

Some products add terpenes:
- May use cannabis-derived terpenes
- Or botanically-derived (from other plants)
- Can create targeted effects
- Quality varies significantly

## How to Identify Terpene Content

### Reading Lab Reports (COAs)

Quality CBD products include terpene testing:

1. **Look for "Terpene Profile" section**
2. **Note percentages of each terpene**
3. **Check for major terpenes present**
4. **Compare to product claims**

### What to Look For

- **Total terpene content** — Usually 1-5% in quality full-spectrum
- **Dominant terpenes** — Match to your desired effects
- **Diversity** — More terpenes often means more complex effects
- **Recent testing** — Terpenes degrade over time

## Matching Terpenes to Your Needs

### For Sleep

Prioritise:
- Myrcene (sedating)
- Linalool (calming)
- Terpinolene (potentially sedating)

Avoid or limit:
- Pinene (alerting)
- Limonene (energising)

### For Anxiety

Prioritise:
- Linalool (anti-anxiety)
- Limonene (mood elevation)
- Caryophyllene (calming)

### For Pain

Prioritise:
- Caryophyllene (CB2 anti-inflammatory)
- Humulene (anti-inflammatory)
- Myrcene (analgesic potential)

### For Focus

Prioritise:
- Pinene (alertness)
- Limonene (clarity)

Avoid:
- Myrcene (sedating)
- Linalool (calming)

## Research on Terpenes

### Current State of Evidence

Terpene research is promising but limited:

**What We Know:**
- Individual terpenes have documented biological activities
- Aromatherapy research supports some effects
- Animal studies show various therapeutic properties
- The entourage effect has theoretical support

**What We Don't Know:**
- Optimal doses for specific effects
- Exact mechanisms in humans
- Long-term effects of concentrated terpenes
- How terpene combinations interact precisely

### Research Limitations

- Most studies use isolated terpenes
- Human clinical trials are limited
- Effects may differ in whole-plant extracts
- Individual variation is significant

## Safety Considerations

### Generally Safe

Terpenes have excellent safety profiles:
- Present in foods consumed daily
- Long history of aromatherapy use
- Approved as food flavourings
- Natural plant compounds

### Considerations

- **Concentration matters** — Isolated terpenes may have different effects than natural levels
- **Allergies possible** — Rare but can occur
- **Medication interactions** — Possible with high doses
- **Quality varies** — Choose tested products

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do terpenes get you high?

No. Terpenes are not psychoactive in the way THC is. However, they may influence your experience—for example, myrcene may make you feel more relaxed, while limonene may feel uplifting. These are mood effects, not intoxication.

### Are cannabis terpenes different from other plant terpenes?

Chemically, no. Limonene from a lemon is identical to limonene from cannabis. However, cannabis contains unique combinations and ratios of terpenes that create its distinctive profiles. Some products use botanically-derived (non-cannabis) terpenes to recreate cannabis profiles.

### How do I know which terpenes are in my CBD product?

Check the Certificate of Analysis (COA). Quality brands test for terpene content and list results. If no terpene testing is available, the product may be isolate-based or the brand may not prioritise transparency.

### Can I add terpenes to CBD isolate?

Yes, terpene concentrates are available for this purpose. However, proper dilution is essential—pure terpenes are potent. Pre-made full-spectrum or broad-spectrum products are simpler alternatives.

### Why do different CBD products smell different?

Different hemp strains have different terpene profiles, just like different apple varieties taste different. Additionally, processing methods affect terpene preservation, and some products add terpenes for specific effects or flavours.
