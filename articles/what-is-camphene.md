# What is Camphene?

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Answer

Camphene is a [terpene](/knowledge/what-are-terpenes) with a pungent, herbal, fir-needle aroma often mistaken for myrcene. Found in essential oils used for coughs and colds, camphene research suggests cardiovascular benefits, anti-inflammatory properties, and antimicrobial activity. It shows particular promise for cholesterol and heart health.

---

## Camphene Profile

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Chemical formula** | C₁₀H₁₆ |
| **Aroma** | Pungent, herbal, fir-needle, damp woodland |
| **Boiling point** | 159°C (318°F) |
| **Prevalence in cannabis** | Moderate |
| **Also found in** | Camphor oil, cypress, valerian, ginger, nutmeg |

---

## The Heart Health Terpene

What makes camphene particularly interesting is cardiovascular research:

**Cholesterol Studies:**
- May lower LDL ("bad") cholesterol
- May lower triglycerides
- Potential cardioprotective effects
- Alternative to statin-like effects

This makes camphene unique among commonly discussed cannabis terpenes.

---

## Potential Effects and Benefits

Research on camphene suggests:

**Cardiovascular**
- May reduce cholesterol and triglycerides
- Potential heart-protective effects
- Lipid metabolism research ongoing
- Animal studies show promise

**Antioxidant**
- Protects against oxidative stress
- May prevent cellular damage
- Complements cardiovascular effects

**Anti-inflammatory**
- Reduces inflammation markers
- May complement CBD's effects
- Studied in various models

**Antimicrobial**
- Antibacterial properties
- Antifungal activity
- Used in traditional remedies

**Pain Relief**
- Analgesic properties documented
- Part of traditional topical formulas
- May contribute to overall effects

**Respiratory**
- Used in chest rubs
- May help with congestion
- Traditional cold remedy ingredient

## Historical Uses

Camphene was historically important:
- Used as lamp fuel before kerosene (dangerous, highly flammable)
- Traditional medicine applications
- Precursor in camphor synthesis
- Part of herbal remedy traditions

---

## Research Status

Camphene research includes:
- Cholesterol/triglyceride studies
- Antioxidant mechanism research
- Traditional medicine validation
- Antimicrobial testing

Human clinical trials specific to camphene are limited but the cardiovascular research is compelling.

---

## Safety

Camphene has a reasonable safety profile:
- Used in fragrances and flavoring
- Part of many essential oils
- Generally well-tolerated
- Follow product guidelines

Note: Highly concentrated forms are flammable.

## My Take

The cardiovascular research on camphene sets it apart from other terpenes. While most terpene discussions focus on anxiety, pain, or sleep, camphene opens a different conversation about heart health. I find this research direction promising, though it's early days. For those interested in the full potential of terpenes beyond the usual benefits, camphene deserves attention.
