# Flying with CBD: Airport Security and Airline Policies

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Flying with CBD is possible in many jurisdictions but requires careful consideration of both departure and arrival location laws, airline policies, and security procedures. Domestic flights in CBD-legal countries are generally straightforward; international flights require more research.

---

## Key Takeaways

- CBD legality depends on departure AND arrival locations
- Check airline-specific policies
- Keep products in original packaging with COA
- Liquids subject to standard restrictions
- When uncertain, don't risk it

---

## Before You Fly

### Research Checklist

1. Is CBD legal at your destination?
2. What are the THC limits?
3. Does the airline allow CBD?
4. Any transit country restrictions?

### Documentation

Bring:
- Original product packaging
- Certificate of Analysis (COA)
- Proof of THC content
- Proof of purchase (receipt)

---

## Airport Security

### TSA (United States)

- Allows hemp-derived CBD (<0.3% THC)
- May refer to local law enforcement if concerned
- Keep accessible for inspection

### EU Airports

- Generally permitted for legal products
- May be questioned
- Documentation helpful

### International Transit

- Research each country's laws
- Transit country rules apply
- Conservative approach recommended

---

## Packing CBD

### Carry-On

- Liquids: 100ml limit applies
- Keep accessible for inspection
- Original packaging preferred

### Checked Luggage

- Less likely to be inspected
- No liquid limits
- Temperature changes may affect product

---

## High-Risk Scenarios

### Avoid Flying with CBD To

- Countries where CBD is illegal
- Conservative countries (Middle East, Asia)
- Countries with severe drug penalties
- Places you're uncertain about

---

## My Take

I fly with CBD domestically and within CBD-legal regions, but I'm very conservative about international travel. The inconvenience of leaving CBD at home is nothing compared to potential legal issues abroad. When in doubt, don't bring it.
