# What is Borneol?

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Answer

Borneol is a [terpene](/knowledge/what-are-terpenes) with a sharp, camphor-like, minty aroma with earthy undertones. Used for centuries in traditional Chinese and Ayurvedic medicine, borneol research suggests analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective properties. Notably, it may enhance the absorption of other compounds, including cannabinoids.

---

## Borneol Profile

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Chemical formula** | C₁₀H₁₈O |
| **Aroma** | Camphor, minty, herbal, earthy |
| **Boiling point** | 210°C (410°F) |
| **Prevalence in cannabis** | Less common |
| **Also found in** | Camphor tree, rosemary, mint, ginger, thyme |

## Traditional Medicine History

Borneol has an extensive history in traditional medicine:

**Traditional Chinese Medicine:**
- Called "bingpian" (冰片)
- Used for thousands of years
- Valued for cardiovascular and pain conditions
- Considered a "guiding" herb (enhances other compounds)

**Ayurvedic Medicine:**
- Used in various preparations
- Digestive and respiratory applications
- Part of traditional formulations

---

## Potential Effects and Benefits

Research on borneol suggests:

**Analgesic (Pain Relief)**
- Demonstrated pain-relieving effects
- May enhance cannabinoid analgesia
- Traditional use supports this

**Anti-inflammatory**
- Reduces inflammation in studies
- May complement CBD's effects
- Multiple mechanisms involved

**Neuroprotective**
- Brain health research shows promise
- May protect against stroke damage
- Potential for neurodegenerative conditions

**Enhanced Absorption**
- May improve drug penetration into brain
- Traditional "guiding" property validated
- Could enhance CBD efficacy

**Antimicrobial**
- Antibacterial activity documented
- Antifungal properties
- Used in oral care products

**Sedative (Mild)**
- May contribute to relaxation
- Calming aromatic effects
- Part of traditional sleep remedies

## The "Guiding" Effect

Traditional Chinese Medicine considers borneol a "guiding" (引药/messenger) compound:
- Helps other ingredients reach their targets
- May enhance blood-brain barrier penetration
- Modern research partially validates this
- Potential implications for CBD formulations
