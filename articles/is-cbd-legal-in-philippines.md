# Is CBD Legal in the Philippines? Complete 2026 Guide

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Answer

CBD is **illegal in the Philippines**. The Philippines has strict anti-drug laws, and all cannabis-derived products including CBD are prohibited. The country's drug enforcement is aggressive. **Do not bring CBD to the Philippines.**

---

## Philippines Drug Laws

### Comprehensive Dangerous Drugs Act

The legal framework:
- Republic Act 9165
- All cannabis products prohibited
- CBD not distinguished
- Severe penalties

### Zero Tolerance

**Policy:**
- Aggressive drug enforcement
- No exemptions for CBD
- Hemp-derived not different
- Strict prosecution

---

## Penalties

### Severe Consequences

Philippines penalties:
- Possession: Up to 12 years prison
- Use: Mandatory rehabilitation or prison
- Trafficking: Life imprisonment to death
- No CBD distinction

### Enforcement

**Reality:**
- Active drug enforcement
- Foreigners not exempt
- Laws applied strictly
- Serious consequences

---

## What's Banned

### All CBD Products

Prohibited:
- CBD oils
- CBD capsules/edibles
- CBD topicals
- Any cannabinoid products
- Hemp products with CBD

### No Exceptions

**Total prohibition:**
- Source doesn't matter
- THC content irrelevant
- Medical need not recognized
- All cannabinoids controlled

## Medical Situation

### Limited Medical Cannabis

**Developments:**
- Compassionate use bill passed (limited)
- Medical cannabis for specific conditions
- Highly restricted
- CBD products still not available commercially

### For Visitors

**The reality:**
- No tourist access to medical cannabis
- Foreign prescriptions not recognized
- Don't bring CBD
- Find alternatives

## My Take

The Philippines has strict drug laws and aggressive enforcement. While there's been some movement on medical cannabis, this doesn't extend to over-the-counter CBD products. Don't bring CBD to the Philippines and don't try to acquire it there. The risk isn't worth it.
