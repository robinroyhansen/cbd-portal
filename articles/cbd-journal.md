# CBD Journal: How to Keep One

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

A CBD journal is a simple daily log where you record your CBD dose, timing, and how you feel. It helps you find your optimal dose, identify what works, and make informed decisions about your CBD use. Even a basic journal dramatically improves your ability to assess whether CBD helps you.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Journals reveal patterns you'd miss
- Keep it simple and consistent
- Track dose, timing, and effects
- Review weekly for insights
- Even basic tracking helps

---

## What Is a CBD Journal?

### Purpose

A daily record of:
- What CBD you took
- When you took it
- How you felt

### Benefits

- Find optimal dose
- Identify patterns
- Assess effectiveness
- Support healthcare discussions
- Remember what works

---

## What to Include

### Essential Information

**Every Entry:**
- Date
- CBD product used
- Dose (mg)
- Time taken
- Primary effect rating (1-10)

### Useful Additions

**If Relevant:**
- Food status (empty/full stomach)
- Other supplements taken
- Stress level that day
- Sleep quality
- Notable events
- Side effects

---

## Journal Formats

### Paper Notebook

**Advantages:**
- No battery needed
- Private
- Tactile experience
- Easy to customise

**Example Format:**
```
Date: Jan 21, 2026
CBD: 25mg full-spectrum oil
Time: 9:00pm (bedtime)
Stomach: Light dinner 2 hours before
Sleep: 4/10 (fell asleep in 15 min)
Morning energy: 3/10 (good)
Notes: Stressful day but slept well
```

### Digital Options

**Spreadsheet:**
- Calculates averages automatically
- Easy to create charts
- Shareable
- Searchable

**Phone Notes:**
- Always with you
- Quick entries
- Timestamped
- Backed up

**Dedicated Apps:**
- CBD-specific features
- Reminders
- Built-in analytics
- May include databases

## Simple Daily Template

### Quick Version (1 Minute)

```
Date: [date]
CBD: [mg] [product type]
Time: [when]
Feel: [1-10 rating]
Notes: [anything notable]
```

### Detailed Version (3-5 Minutes)

```
Date: [date]
Product: [brand, type, spectrum]
Dose: [mg]
Time taken: [time]
With food: [yes/no, what]

RATINGS (1=best, 10=worst):
Sleep quality: [  ]
Stress level: [  ]
Discomfort: [  ]
Energy: [  ]
Mood: [  ]

Notes:
[Any observations, events, side effects]
```

## Weekly Review Process

### What to Do

**Every Week:**
1. Calculate average ratings
2. Compare to previous weeks
3. Note patterns
4. Identify questions
5. Plan adjustments

### Questions to Consider

- Are metrics improving?
- Any patterns by day of week?
- Does timing matter?
- Optimal dose emerging?
- Side effects appearing?

---

## What Patterns to Look For

### Positive Signs

- Improving weekly averages
- More consistent ratings
- Faster effects over time
- Better specific outcomes

### Adjustment Signals

- No improvement after 4+ weeks
- Inconsistent effects
- Side effects present
- Diminishing returns

### Interesting Discoveries

- Time of day matters
- Food affects absorption
- Stress days need different dose
- Certain activities help

---

## How Long to Journal

### Minimum Periods

**Initial Trial:**
- 4 weeks minimum
- Daily entries essential
- Weekly reviews

**Establishing Routine:**
- 2-3 months ideal
- Captures variations
- Confirms patterns

### Ongoing

**Once Stable:**
- Less frequent logging
- Note changes or issues
- Resume detailed tracking when adjusting

---

## Tips for Consistency

### Make It Easy

- Same time each day
- Keep journal accessible
- Use phone reminders
- Quick format preferred

### Build the Habit

- Link to existing routine
- Evening review works well
- 2 minutes is enough
- Don't skip days

### Stay Honest

- Record accurately
- Bad days count too
- Don't edit later
- Objective observations

## Using Your Journal Data

### Finding Your Dose

**Your journal can show:**
- What doses work best
- Whether to increase/decrease
- Optimal timing
- When effects plateau

### Healthcare Discussions

**Bring journal to appointments:**
- Objective record
- Shows your approach
- Identifies patterns
- Supports informed discussion

### Product Decisions

**Journal reveals:**
- Which products work
- Whether to continue
- Value for money
- When to try alternatives

---

## Common Journal Mistakes

### What to Avoid

**Inconsistency:**
- Skipping days
- Changing metrics
- Random times
- Vague entries

**Overthinking:**
- Too many metrics
- Paragraph entries
- Excessive detail
- Analysis paralysis

**Impatience:**
- Judging too soon
- Daily conclusions
- Expecting instant patterns
- Giving up early

## Sample Week

### Example Journal Entries

**Monday:**
CBD: 20mg oil, 9pm, light dinner
Sleep: 5/10, Stress: 6/10
Notes: Busy day, took 45 min to fall asleep

**Tuesday:**
CBD: 20mg oil, 8pm, full dinner
Sleep: 4/10, Stress: 5/10
Notes: Earlier dose, fell asleep faster

**Wednesday:**
CBD: 25mg oil, 8pm, light dinner
Sleep: 3/10, Stress: 5/10
Notes: Higher dose, great sleep

**Thursday:**
CBD: 25mg oil, 8pm, no dinner
Sleep: 4/10, Stress: 4/10
Notes: Empty stomach, slight dry mouth

**Friday:**
CBD: 25mg oil, 8pm, light dinner
Sleep: 3/10, Stress: 5/10
Notes: Weekend coming, relaxed evening

**Weekly Review:**
- 25mg seems better than 20mg
- 8pm timing better than 9pm
- Light dinner best (empty stomach = dry mouth)
- Average sleep improved from 5.5 to 4.2
