# CBD for Truckers: Navigating DOT Regulations and Road Life

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Trucking presents unique challenges—long hours, sedentary work, sleep disruption, and strict DOT drug testing. Truckers interested in CBD must navigate complex regulations while managing the physical and mental demands of life on the road.

---

## Critical: DOT Drug Testing

### Understanding the Rules

Current situation:
- DOT prohibits marijuana use
- CBD legality is complex for CDL holders
- THC on a drug test = career consequences
- Zero tolerance policy

### The Risk

Reality:
- Even legal CBD can contain trace THC
- THC accumulates with regular use
- Drug tests don't distinguish source
- Failed test = serious consequences

### Safest Approach

If choosing to use:
- Only THC-free isolate products
- Third-party verified (COA showing ND)
- Extra caution warranted
- Understand the risk remains

Isolation effects:
- Long hours alone
- Away from family
- Monotony
- Stress of deadlines

## If You Choose to Use CBD

### Product Selection Critical

Must use:
- CBD isolate only
- Verified THC-free
- Reputable brands
- Batch-specific COA

### Verification Steps

Essential:
- Check COA shows "ND" for THC
- Match batch numbers
- Recent testing
- Accredited lab

### Documentation

Smart practice:
- Keep product packaging
- Save COA copies
- Purchase receipts
- Record keeping

## Key Takeaways

1. **DOT testing** creates real risk for CDL holders
2. **Only THC-free isolate** if using any CBD
3. **Verify with COA**—batch-specific, ND for THC
4. **Off-duty only**—never before/during driving
5. **Understand** career stakes before using

---

## My Take

Truckers are in a difficult position—the job creates significant physical and mental health challenges, but DOT regulations create career risk with CBD use. The safest approach is avoiding CBD entirely, but I understand that's not the answer everyone wants.

If you choose to use CBD, use only verified THC-free isolate products, use off-duty only, and understand that some risk remains. Failed drug tests have serious career consequences. Make an informed decision based on your testing frequency, career stage, and personal circumstances.
