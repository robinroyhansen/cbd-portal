# Does CBD Make You Hungry?

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Unlike THC, which famously causes "the munchies," CBD typically doesn't increase appetite and may even have appetite-suppressing effects in some people. However, by reducing nausea or stress, CBD might indirectly help people eat more normally.

---

## Key Takeaways

- CBD doesn't cause "munchies" like THC
- May actually suppress appetite in some
- Can help normalize eating via stress reduction
- Individual responses vary
- Different mechanism than THC

---

## CBD vs THC: Appetite Effects

### THC (Increases Hunger)

- Binds directly to CB1 receptors
- Triggers hunger signals
- Famous "munchies" effect
- Well-documented

### CBD (Usually Doesn't)

- Doesn't bind CB1 the same way
- May suppress appetite in some
- Different receptor profile
- No "munchies" effect

---

## What Research Shows

### Appetite Studies

**Findings Suggest:**
- CBD doesn't increase appetite directly
- Some studies show appetite reduction
- Effects are mild either direction
- Not a strong appetite driver

### Clinical Trial Data

In Epidiolex trials:
- Decreased appetite was a side effect
- Not increased appetite
- Supports non-hunger-inducing profile

---

## How CBD Might Affect Eating

### Indirect Effects

**Could Increase Eating By:**
- Reducing nausea
- Decreasing stress/anxiety
- Improving mood
- Reducing pain (can suppress appetite)

**Could Decrease Eating By:**
- Direct appetite suppression (some people)
- Medication interaction effects
- Individual response

## For Different Goals

### If You Want to Eat More

**CBD Might Help If:**
- Nausea prevents eating
- Anxiety suppresses appetite
- Stress affects eating
- Not direct hunger stimulation

### If You Want to Eat Less

**CBD Is Not:**
- A weight loss supplement
- A proven appetite suppressant
- Reliable for weight goals
- Substitute for proper approach
