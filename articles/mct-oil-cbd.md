# MCT Oil and CBD: Why It's the Most Popular Carrier

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

MCT (Medium-Chain Triglyceride) oil is the most popular carrier oil for CBD products due to its high bioavailability, neutral taste, and long shelf life. Derived from coconut or palm oil, MCT oil is processed differently by the body than other fats, allowing for faster absorption of CBD. Most CBD oils on the market use MCT as their base.

---

## Key Takeaways

- MCT = Medium-Chain Triglycerides
- Derived from coconut or palm
- Best bioavailability of common carriers
- Neutral taste
- Industry standard for CBD

---

## What Is MCT Oil?

### Definition

MCT oil consists of medium-chain triglycerides—fatty acids with 6-12 carbon atoms. These are extracted and concentrated from coconut oil or palm kernel oil.

### Types of MCTs

**C6 (Caproic Acid):**
- Shortest chain
- Fastest absorption
- Can cause digestive upset
- Usually removed

**C8 (Caprylic Acid):**
- Premium MCT
- Very fast absorption
- Best for energy
- Often in high-quality products

**C10 (Capric Acid):**
- Good absorption
- Gentler on stomach
- Common in MCT oils

**C12 (Lauric Acid):**
- Longest "medium" chain
- Slower absorption
- Often excluded from pure MCT

## Why MCT Oil for CBD?

### Bioavailability Advantage

**How MCT Differs:**
- Doesn't require bile for digestion
- Absorbed directly from intestine
- Goes straight to liver
- Faster processing than long-chain fats

**Result:**
More CBD reaches your bloodstream more quickly.

### Other Benefits

**Neutral Taste:**
- Doesn't compete with CBD
- Clean, mild flavour
- Easy to take
- Works with flavoured products

**Long Shelf Life:**
- Resistant to oxidation
- Stays fresh longer
- Stable at room temperature
- Won't go rancid quickly

## How MCT Improves CBD Absorption

### The Science

**Traditional Fats:**
1. Eaten
2. Require bile to emulsify
3. Broken down in small intestine
4. Absorbed into lymphatic system
5. Eventually reach bloodstream

**MCT Oil:**
1. Eaten
2. Directly absorbed
3. Go straight to liver via portal vein
4. Rapidly metabolised
5. Faster availability

### What This Means for CBD

- CBD in MCT may absorb faster
- Potentially higher absorption rate
- More efficient use of CBD
- Quicker onset possible

## Potential Concerns

### Digestive Sensitivity

**Some People Experience:**
- Stomach upset initially
- Loose stools
- Nausea if taken on empty stomach

**Solutions:**
- Start with lower amounts
- Take with food
- Body often adapts
- Try different carrier if persistent

### Coconut Allergy

**Rare But Possible:**
- MCT is highly processed
- Most allergens removed
- But caution if allergic
- Alternatives available

## Using MCT-Based CBD

### Best Practices

**Sublingual Use:**
- Hold under tongue 60-90 seconds
- Allow absorption through membranes
- Then swallow remainder
- MCT doesn't irritate tissues

**With Food:**
- Can take with meals
- Fatty foods may help
- Won't significantly change absorption
- Makes routine easier

**Timing:**
- Works on empty or full stomach
- Empty may be slightly faster
- Full may be gentler
- Find what works for you

## My Take

MCT oil has become the industry standard carrier for good reason—it offers the best bioavailability of common options, has virtually no taste, and stays fresh for a long time. For most people choosing a CBD oil, MCT is the right carrier. The only reasons to choose something else are coconut sensitivity, digestive issues with MCT specifically, or a strong preference for a different oil (like hemp seed for its omega content). When in doubt, MCT is the safe, effective choice.
