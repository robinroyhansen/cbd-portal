# Can I Use CBD Oil on Skin?

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Yes, you can apply CBD oil directly to skin. CBD has shown benefits for skin health in research, including anti-inflammatory and sebum-regulating properties. However, CBD topicals are specifically formulated for skin use and may work better than repurposing oral CBD oil.

---

## Key Takeaways

- CBD oil can be applied topically
- Dedicated topicals often work better
- Skin has cannabinoid receptors
- Good for localised issues
- Won't produce systemic effects

---

## Using Oral CBD Oil on Skin

### It Works, But...

**Can Be Done:**
- CBD absorbs through skin
- Will provide local effects
- Some people do this
- Not harmful

**Considerations:**
- Not optimised for skin
- Carrier oil may not suit skin
- May be greasy
- Cost-ineffective

## When to Apply CBD Topically

### Good Uses

- Localised muscle tension
- Joint discomfort
- Skin inflammation
- Acne-prone areas
- Dry or irritated skin
- Post-exercise recovery

### Not Ideal For

- Systemic effects (use oral)
- Anxiety or sleep (need systemic)
- Deep internal issues

---

## How to Apply

### If Using CBD Oil

1. Clean the area
2. Apply small amount
3. Massage gently
4. Allow to absorb
5. May feel oily

### If Using Topical

1. Clean the area
2. Apply as directed
3. Massage in
4. Absorbs better usually
5. Less residue

## Absorption Through Skin

### What Happens

**Transdermal CBD:**
- Absorbs into local tissue
- Reaches receptors in skin
- Minimal systemic absorption
- Localised effects

### Not the Same as Oral

- Won't get into bloodstream well
- Local effects only
- No "whole body" benefit
- Targeted application
