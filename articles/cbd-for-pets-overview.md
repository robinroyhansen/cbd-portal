# CBD for Pets: Essential Overview for Pet Owners

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

CBD products formulated for pets are increasingly popular for managing anxiety, pain, and other conditions in dogs, cats, and other animals. Research is limited but growing. **Always use pet-specific CBD products** (human products may contain ingredients toxic to pets) and consult your veterinarian.

## The Endocannabinoid System in Animals

### Pets Have an ECS

Dogs, cats, and many animals have:
- CB1 and CB2 receptors
- Endocannabinoid production
- Similar ECS function to humans
- Respond to cannabinoids

### Why This Matters

- CBD works through the same pathways
- Animals may benefit similarly to humans
- Different sensitivities exist
- Proper dosing crucial

## Safety Considerations

### Critical: Use Pet-Specific Products

**Why human CBD isn't always safe:**
- May contain xylitol (toxic to dogs)
- May have excessive THC
- Flavorings may be harmful
- Dosing not appropriate

**Pet CBD products:**
- Formulated for animal safety
- Appropriate dosing
- Safe flavorings
- THC levels verified

### THC Toxicity

THC is toxic to pets:
- Dogs especially sensitive
- Can cause serious symptoms
- Use THC-free products only
- Verify with lab reports

### Signs of Adverse Reaction

Watch for:
- Excessive sedation
- Vomiting or diarrhea
- Loss of coordination
- Disorientation
- Changes in behavior

**If symptoms occur:** Stop CBD and contact veterinarian

---

## Dosing for Pets

### General Guidelines

**Dogs:**
- Start: 0.5-1mg per 10lbs body weight
- Frequency: 1-2 times daily
- Increase gradually if needed
- Lower doses often effective

**Cats:**
- Start: 0.5mg per 10lbs (lower than dogs)
- Extra caution warranted
- Monitor closely
- Cats are more sensitive

### Titration

1. Start with lowest suggested dose
2. Observe for 1-2 weeks
3. Increase only if needed
4. Find minimum effective dose
5. Monitor ongoing

## Pet CBD Products

### Common Forms

- Most flexible dosing

- Pre-measured doses

- Consistent dosing

## Key Takeaways

1. **Always use pet-specific CBD** — human products may be harmful
2. **Consult veterinarian** — especially if pet is on medications
3. **Start with low doses** — pets often need less
4. **THC is toxic** to pets — use THC-free only
5. **Monitor your pet** — watch for effects and side effects

## My Take

CBD for pets holds promise, particularly for anxiety and arthritis in dogs where some research exists. I've heard countless positive reports from pet owners about calmer, more comfortable animals.

The most important point: use pet-specific products. Human CBD products may contain xylitol or other ingredients that are toxic to dogs, and dosing is completely different.

Start low, go slow, and observe your pet. Animals can't tell us how they feel, so watch their behavior carefully. If something seems off, stop and consult your vet.

Work with your veterinarian, especially if your pet is on other medications. They may be more knowledgeable about pet CBD than you expect—it's a growing area of veterinary interest.
