# CBD for Martial Arts: Recovery and Performance Support

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Martial arts training is physically demanding, involving striking, grappling, and high-impact contact. CBD may help martial artists with recovery, inflammation management, and the unique demands of combat sports training.

---

## Why Martial Artists Use CBD

### Recovery Demands

Primary benefits:
- Post-training recovery
- Impact-related inflammation
- Joint stress management
- Sleep optimization

### Combat Sports Challenges

CBD addresses:
- Bruising and soft tissue trauma
- Joint manipulation stress (BJJ, wrestling)
- Striking impact (boxing, Muay Thai)
- Nervous system recovery

---

## How CBD Helps Martial Artists

### Inflammation from Impact

Unique needs:
- Striking creates tissue inflammation
- Grappling stresses joints
- CBD's anti-inflammatory effects
- Supports healing process

### Joint Health

Important for grapplers:
- Submissions stress joints
- BJJ, judo, wrestling demands
- CBD supports joint recovery
- Long-term joint health

### Recovery Between Sessions

Multiple training types:
- Technique work
- Sparring
- Conditioning
- Need rapid recovery

## Using CBD for Martial Arts

### Post-Training Protocol

Approach:
- Take after training sessions
- Higher doses after sparring
- Topical for specific impact areas
- Evening dose for sleep

### By Training Type

Adjust accordingly:
- Light technique: lower dose (15-25mg)
- Hard sparring: higher dose (40-60mg)
- Competition camp: consistent use
- Recovery days: maintain

## Discipline-Specific Uses

### BJJ and Grappling

Focus areas:
- Joint recovery (fingers, knees, shoulders)
- Neck stress from chokes
- Pressure-related bruising
- High training volume recovery

### Striking Arts

Focus areas:
- Shin conditioning recovery
- Hand and wrist inflammation
- Core and rib bruising
- Head impact recovery (cautiously)

### MMA

Combined demands:
- All of the above
- Highest recovery needs
- Multiple energy systems
- Consistent daily use recommended

## Competition Considerations

### USADA/WADA Status

Current rules:
- CBD not prohibited
- THC prohibited
- Product purity critical
- Use verified THC-free

### Combat Sports Testing

Be aware:
- UFC tests under USADA
- Many promotions test
- Use only verified products
- Stop before competition if concerned

---

## Pain Management Considerations

### Chronic Training Pain

Reality of martial arts:
- Some discomfort is constant
- CBD may help manage
- Not a substitute for rest
- Address injuries properly

### What CBD Won't Fix

Limitations:
- Can't replace proper recovery
- Injuries need treatment
- Overtraining needs rest
- Support, not solution
