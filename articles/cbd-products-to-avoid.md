# CBD Products to Avoid: Red Flags and Warning Signs

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Not all CBD products are created equal. Some are ineffective, mislabeled, or even unsafe. Learning to identify red flags helps you avoid wasting money and potentially exposing yourself to contaminants or inadequate products.

---

## Why Quality Varies

### Unregulated Market

Current situation:
- Limited FDA oversight
- No standard requirements
- Quality varies wildly
- Consumer beware necessary

### Consequences of Poor Products

Risks:
- No actual CBD
- Contaminants present
- Mislabeled amounts
- Ineffective products
- Wasted money

---

## Major Red Flags

### No Third-Party Testing

Critical warning:
- COA not available
- Won't provide test results
- Lab name not identified
- Testing not current/relevant

### Health Claims

Illegal claims:
- "Cures" any disease
- "Treats" medical conditions
- FDA-drug-like claims
- Guaranteed results

### Too Cheap

Suspicious pricing:
- Significantly below market
- Impossible economics
- "Clearance" unknown brands
- Free product offers

### No Ingredient List

Basic requirement missing:
- Can't see what's inside
- Vague "proprietary blend"
- Missing carrier oil info
- Undisclosed additives

---

## Label Warning Signs

### Vague CBD Claims

Watch for:
- "Hemp extract" without CBD mg
- "Full plant" without amounts
- No mg specified
- Inflated total numbers

### Missing Information

Should be on label:
- CBD amount per serving
- Total CBD content
- Spectrum type
- Batch/lot number
- Serving size

### Inconsistent Information

Problems:
- Label doesn't match COA
- Different amounts claimed
- Contradictory spectrum claims
- Math doesn't add up

---

## COA Problems

### No COA Available

Major red flag:
- Can't verify claims
- No quality assurance
- Hiding something
- Move on

### COA Issues

Problems to spot:
- Old/outdated testing
- Different batch tested
- Unknown lab
- Missing tests
- Results don't match label

### What COA Should Show

Required:
- Cannabinoid profile
- Contaminant testing
- Recent date
- Batch number match
- Accredited lab

---

## Specific Product Types to Avoid

### Gas Station CBD

Generally avoid:
- Unknown brands
- No quality control
- Often very low quality
- Convenience premium
- No COA access

### Extremely Cheap Online Products

Suspicious:
- Below-market pricing
- Unknown manufacturers
- No reviews
- Flashy marketing, no substance
- Too-good-to-be-true

### Products with Outrageous Claims

Marketing red flags:
- "Miracle cure"
- Guaranteed results
- Treats all conditions
- Doctor endorsements
- Celebrity associations

---

## Company Red Flags

### No Contact Information

Warning sign:
- No address
- No phone number
- Only contact form
- Can't reach anyone

### No Company History

Concerns:
- Brand new company
- No track record
- Unknown ownership
- Sudden appearance

### Pressure Tactics

Sales red flags:
- Limited time offers
- Aggressive upselling
- Hard to cancel subscriptions
- MLM/pyramid structure

---

## Ingredient Red Flags

### Harmful Additives

Avoid:
- Synthetic cannabinoids
- Unapproved additives
- Artificial colors (some products)
- Unknown chemicals

### Allergen Concerns

Check for:
- Undisclosed allergens
- Cross-contamination risks
- Carrier oil sensitivities
- Added ingredients

### Contaminants

Should be tested for:
- Heavy metals
- Pesticides
- Residual solvents
- Microbial contamination

---

## What Good Products Look Like

### Quality Indicators

Positive signs:
- Easily accessible COA
- Clear labeling
- Reasonable pricing
- Company transparency
- Customer reviews

### Minimum Standards

Should have:
- Third-party testing
- Clear CBD content
- Identified spectrum
- Proper labeling
- Contactable company

---

## If You've Bought a Bad Product

### What to Do

Steps:
- Stop using immediately
- Document the product
- Request refund
- Report to company
- Consider reporting to authorities

### Where to Report

Options:
- FDA MedWatch
- State consumer protection
- Better Business Bureau
- Credit card dispute
- Online reviews

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **No COA** is the biggest red flag
2. **Health claims** are illegal and suspicious
3. **Too cheap** usually means poor quality
4. **Vague labeling** hides poor products
5. **Trust your instincts** about sketchy products
