# CBD Skin Research: Dermatological Evidence

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Research on CBD for skin conditions shows promising results, particularly for inflammatory conditions like acne, eczema, and psoriasis. CBD interacts with the endocannabinoid system in skin, with anti-inflammatory, sebum-regulating, and barrier-supporting effects identified in studies.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Skin has its own endocannabinoid system
- Anti-inflammatory effects documented
- Acne research most developed
- Multiple conditions studied
- Topical application growing

---

## Skin Endocannabinoid System

### Discovery

**Skin ECS Contains:**
- Cannabinoid receptors (CB1, CB2)
- Endocannabinoids
- Metabolic enzymes
- Involved in skin homeostasis

### Functions

- Inflammation regulation
- Sebum production
- Cell growth and death
- Barrier function
- Pain and itch signalling

## Human Studies

### Topical CBD Research

**Palmieri et al. (2019):**
- 20 patients with skin conditions
- CBD-enriched ointment
- Improvement in symptoms
- No adverse effects

### Limitations

- Small sample sizes
- Often uncontrolled
- Product variability
- More research needed

---

## Topical vs Systemic

### Topical Application

**Advantages:**
- Local effect
- Minimal systemic absorption
- Direct to affected area
- Fewer drug interactions

**Challenges:**
- Penetration variability
- Formulation matters
- Dosing uncertainty
- Stability issues

### Oral/Systemic

- May help inflammatory conditions
- Whole-body effects
- Different use case
- Research ongoing

## My Take

CBD's effects on skin are biologically plausible given the skin's endocannabinoid system. The acne research is particularly interesting, with clear mechanisms identified. For inflammatory skin conditions, CBD topicals may offer benefit with minimal risk. However, more rigorous human studies are needed to make confident recommendations.
