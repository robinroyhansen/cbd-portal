# Is CBD Legal in the Netherlands? Complete 2026 Guide

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Answer

CBD is **legal in the Netherlands** when derived from hemp and containing less than 0.05% THC—one of Europe's strictest limits. Despite the country's reputation for cannabis tolerance, CBD regulations are actually quite restrictive. Novel Food rules also apply, and CBD cannot be sold as a food supplement without authorization.

---

## Understanding Dutch Cannabis Policy

### The Contradiction

Many assume the Netherlands is permissive about CBD. Reality is different:

- **Coffeeshops sell THC cannabis** (tolerated but technically illegal)
- **CBD has strict regulations** (must be <0.05% THC)
- **More restrictive than most EU countries** for CBD

### Why This Matters

If you're expecting the same relaxed approach for CBD as for coffeeshop cannabis, you'll be surprised. CBD products face stricter scrutiny than in many neighboring countries.

---

## THC Limits: The 0.05% Rule

### Europe's Strictest Limit

The Netherlands applies a 0.05% THC limit:
- Much stricter than EU's 0.2% cultivation standard
- Among the lowest in Europe
- Full-spectrum products often don't qualify
- Many products use isolate or broad-spectrum

### Practical Impact

- Products must be essentially THC-free
- Many international products don't comply
- Dutch market favors isolate products
- Testing sensitivity requirements high

---

## What's Legal

### Clearly Legal

- CBD isolate products (<0.05% THC)
- CBD cosmetics (properly formulated)
- Hemp foods (seeds, etc.)
- Epidiolex (prescription)

### Restricted/Complex

- CBD as food (Novel Food applies)
- Full-spectrum products (usually exceed THC limit)
- CBD flowers (complicated status)

### Prohibited

- Products >0.05% THC
- Medical claims without authorization
- Sale as medicines without approval

---

## CBD Flowers (Hennepbloemen)

### Complicated Status

CBD flowers in the Netherlands face issues:
- THC content often exceeds 0.05%
- Difficult to distinguish from regular cannabis
- Some shops sell them; others don't
- Enforcement can be strict

### Compared to Coffeeshops

- Coffeeshops don't typically sell CBD-only products
- They operate under toleration policy
- CBD shops are separate businesses
- Different regulatory framework

---

## Novel Food Regulations

### Dutch Enforcement

The Netherlands applies EU Novel Food rules:
- CBD as food requires authorization
- NVWA (Dutch food authority) enforces
- Some products removed from market
- Stricter enforcement than some countries

### Current Status

- Many CBD food products unavailable
- Cosmetics less affected
- Market more limited than neighbors
- Enforcement actions have occurred

## Medical Cannabis vs CBD

### Dutch Medical Cannabis

The Netherlands has a medical cannabis program:
- Available through pharmacies
- Produced by Bedrocan
- Prescription required
- Insurance may cover
- Includes various THC/CBD ratios

### Prescription CBD

- Epidiolex available
- For specific conditions
- Through healthcare system
- Pharmaceutical grade

### Over-the-Counter CBD

- Limited options
- Must be <0.05% THC
- Cannot make health claims
- Regulated as food/cosmetic

## My Take

The Netherlands surprises many CBD users. Despite its famous tolerance for cannabis coffeeshops, CBD faces some of Europe's strictest regulations. The 0.05% THC limit effectively eliminates most full-spectrum products from the legal market.

If you rely on CBD and are visiting the Netherlands, consider bringing your own supply (within personal use limits) rather than expecting to find your usual products easily. The market is more limited than in Germany, Belgium, or France.

For Dutch residents, quality CBD is available, but you'll need to focus on isolate or broad-spectrum products that meet the strict THC threshold. The market exists, it's just more constrained than you might expect.

The irony of stricter CBD rules in a country famous for cannabis tolerance isn't lost on anyone—but that's the current regulatory reality.
