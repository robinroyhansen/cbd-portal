# What is CBT (Cannabitriol)?

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Answer

CBT (Cannabitriol) is a rare, non-psychoactive [cannabinoid](/glossary/cannabinoid) found in trace amounts in cannabis. It was first identified in the 1970s but remains one of the least studied cannabinoids. CBT doesn't produce a high and is not commonly discussed in CBD product contexts.

---

## What is CBT?

Cannabitriol (CBT) is a minor cannabinoid with a unique molecular structure. There are actually multiple forms of CBT (sometimes called "CBT-C" for different variants), and research on all forms is limited.

**Key characteristics:**
- Non-psychoactive
- Found in trace amounts in cannabis
- Multiple structural variants exist
- Minimal research conducted
- Not typically highlighted in CBD products

---

## CBT Research

The limited research on CBT includes:
- Initial identification and structural characterization (1970s)
- Basic detection methods development
- Occasional appearance in comprehensive cannabinoid screens

No significant therapeutic studies have been published specifically on CBT.

---

## CBT in CBD Products

CBT is rarely mentioned in CBD product testing because:
- It occurs in very low concentrations
- It's not routinely tested for
- Its effects are unknown
- Consumer interest is minimal

If you see CBT in lab results, it simply indicates the presence of this trace cannabinoid.

---

## Note on "CBT" Terminology

Don't confuse CBT (Cannabitriol) with:
- **CBT therapy** (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy)
- Other cannabinoid abbreviations

In the cannabis context, CBT specifically refers to Cannabitriol.
