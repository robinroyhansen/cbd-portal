# CBD and Blood Thinners: Critical Information

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

CBD can significantly interact with blood thinners, particularly warfarin, by inhibiting the enzymes that metabolise these medications. This interaction can increase bleeding risk. Medical supervision is essential if you take anticoagulants and want to use CBD.

---

## Key Takeaways

- CBD inhibits warfarin metabolism
- Can increase INR and bleeding risk
- Medical supervision is essential
- Dose timing and monitoring critical
- Never adjust medications without medical guidance

---

## Understanding the Interaction

### Why It Happens

CBD affects blood thinners through:
- CYP2C9 enzyme inhibition (warfarin)
- CYP3A4 enzyme inhibition (some newer anticoagulants)
- Increased drug levels in blood
- Enhanced anticoagulant effect

### The Risk

- Elevated INR values
- Increased bleeding risk
- Bruising more easily
- Potential for serious bleeding events

---

## Warfarin (Coumadin)

### Specific Concerns

- Well-documented interaction
- CBD increases warfarin levels
- INR can rise significantly
- Requires close monitoring

### Research Evidence

- Case reports of INR increases
- Mechanism well understood
- Effect can be significant
- Individual variation exists

### Management

- More frequent INR testing
- Potential warfarin dose reduction
- Close medical supervision
- Report any bleeding signs

---

## Other Anticoagulants

### DOACs (Direct Oral Anticoagulants)

- Rivaroxaban (Xarelto)
- Apixaban (Eliquis)
- Dabigatran (Pradaxa)
- Edoxaban (Savaysa)

### Interaction Risk

- CYP3A4 interactions possible
- Less studied than warfarin
- Still requires caution
- Medical guidance essential

### Antiplatelet Drugs

- Aspirin
- Clopidogrel (Plavix)
- Potential additive effects
- Monitor for bleeding

---

## Warning Signs

### Seek Medical Attention For

- Unusual bruising
- Prolonged bleeding from cuts
- Blood in urine or stool
- Nosebleeds that won't stop
- Coughing up blood
- Severe headache
- Vomiting blood

---

## If You Want to Use CBD

### Steps to Take

1. **Consult your doctor** — Don't start without medical guidance
2. **Disclose fully** — Tell them about CBD interest
3. **Plan monitoring** — More frequent INR tests initially
4. **Start very low** — Lower doses reduce interaction risk
5. **Watch carefully** — Report any symptoms immediately

### What Your Doctor May Do

- Increase monitoring frequency
- Adjust anticoagulant dose
- Recommend against CBD use
- Suggest safer alternatives

---

## Risk Reduction

### If Approved by Doctor

- Use lowest effective CBD dose
- Maintain consistent CBD dosing
- Keep all monitoring appointments
- Report any side effects
- Don't change either medication without guidance

### Timing Considerations

- Separating doses may help
- Consistency is key
- Follow medical advice exactly

---

## My Take

The CBD-blood thinner interaction is one of the most clinically significant. If you're on anticoagulants, this isn't an area for experimentation. The consequences of getting it wrong—excessive bleeding—can be life-threatening. Only proceed with explicit medical supervision and enhanced monitoring.
