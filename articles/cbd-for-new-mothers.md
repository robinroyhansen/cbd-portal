# CBD for New Mothers: Important Considerations

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

New motherhood brings exhaustion, anxiety, and physical recovery challenges. While CBD might seem helpful, there are important considerations for breastfeeding mothers. This guide provides honest information for making informed decisions.

---

## The New Mother Reality

### Common Challenges

New motherhood:
- Severe sleep deprivation
- Postpartum anxiety
- Physical recovery from birth
- Overwhelming adjustment
- Isolation and stress

### Why CBD Seems Appealing

Potential help:
- Sleep support
- Anxiety relief
- Stress management
- Natural option

---

## The Critical Question: Breastfeeding

### Current Research

What we know:
- CBD transfers into breast milk
- Amount transferred unclear
- Effects on infants unknown
- Research is limited

### Professional Guidance

Medical position:
- Most medical organizations advise caution
- FDA advises avoiding during breastfeeding
- Pediatricians generally recommend against
- Limited safety data

### The Honest Assessment

Reality:
- We don't know if it's safe
- No evidence it's safe for breastfed infants
- Potential unknown effects on development
- Erring on caution makes sense

---

## If You're Breastfeeding

### Conservative Recommendation

Approach:
- Avoid CBD while breastfeeding
- Discuss with healthcare provider
- Other options may be safer
- Wait until weaning

### If You Choose to Use

If proceeding:
- Discuss with doctor and pediatrician
- Use lowest possible dose
- THC-free products only
- Monitor infant for any changes

### Alternatives

Safer options:
- Talk therapy for anxiety
- Sleep when baby sleeps
- Support system utilization
- Consult doctor for safe medications

---

## If You're Formula Feeding

### Different Calculation

Considerations:
- No transfer to infant concern
- Your own health decisions
- Still postpartum considerations
- More freedom but still caution

### Reasonable Use

If appropriate:
- Discuss with doctor
- Standard precautions apply
- May help with recovery
- Personal choice

---

## Postpartum-Specific Issues

### Postpartum Anxiety

Common:
- More common than depression
- Racing thoughts
- Excessive worry
- CBD may help (but see breastfeeding concerns)

### Sleep Deprivation

Reality:
- Extreme exhaustion
- CBD helps sleep—when you can sleep
- Doesn't replace actual sleep opportunity
- Managing what you can

### Physical Recovery

Body healing:
- Topical CBD may be option
- No breastfeeding concern for topicals
- Pain and inflammation
- Discuss with provider

---

## Topical CBD: Different Considerations

### Lower Concern

Why:
- Minimal systemic absorption
- Less likely to transfer to milk
- Localized effects
- May be reasonable option

### Uses

Potential applications:
- Perineal healing (discuss with provider)
- Back pain from carrying baby
- Muscle tension
- Physical recovery

### Still Discuss

Caution:
- Mention to healthcare provider
- Not studied specifically
- Lower risk than oral
- Individual decision

---

## When to Seek Professional Help

### Beyond CBD's Scope

Get help if:
- Postpartum depression symptoms
- Can't care for baby
- Thoughts of harm
- Severe anxiety

### CBD Isn't Treatment

Important:
- Postpartum mental health needs treatment
- CBD is not sufficient for serious issues
- Professional support essential
- Don't delay getting help

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Breastfeeding**: most advise avoiding CBD
2. **Safety unknown** for breastfed infants
3. **Topicals** may be lower risk
4. **Formula feeding** different calculation
5. **Seek professional help** for serious symptoms

---

## My Take

This is one area where I advise real caution. New motherhood is hard, and CBD might help with some symptoms—but we don't know enough about its effects on breastfed infants. That uncertainty tips me toward recommending avoidance during breastfeeding.

Topicals may be a reasonable middle ground for physical recovery, and formula-feeding mothers have more flexibility. But serious postpartum mental health issues need professional treatment, not supplements. Don't let CBD delay getting real help.
