# CBD and Alcohol: What You Should Know

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Answer

Combining CBD and alcohol is **generally considered safe** at moderate levels, but they can **amplify each other's effects**. Both are sedating, so together they may increase drowsiness and impairment. There's no evidence of dangerous interactions, but moderation is wise.

---

## What Happens When Combined

### Amplified Effects

Both CBD and alcohol are:
- Sedating/relaxing
- Processed by liver
- Potentially impairment-causing

Together:
- Effects may be intensified
- More sedation likely
- Reaction time possibly more affected
- Enhanced relaxation

### What Users Report

**Common experiences:**
- Increased relaxation
- More drowsiness than either alone
- Some report feeling alcohol less
- Enhanced calming effect

---

## Safety Considerations

### Is It Dangerous?

**No known dangerous interactions:**
- No documented serious adverse events
- Both processed similarly
- Combination is common
- Not contraindicated

**But caution warranted:**
- Additive sedation
- Potential for increased impairment
- Individual response varies
- Moderation important

### The Sedation Factor

| Substance | Sedating? | Combined Effect |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| CBD | Mildly (dose-dependent) | Enhanced sedation |
| Alcohol | Yes | Enhanced sedation |
| Together | — | More than either alone |

---

## What Research Shows

### Limited Studies

Research on CBD + alcohol is limited:
- Few direct studies
- Some animal research
- One older human study
- More research needed

### Key Findings

**One study (1979):**
- CBD + alcohol together
- Similar motor/cognitive impairment
- Some suggestion CBD may reduce blood alcohol levels
- Study was small, needs replication

**Animal research:**
- Some protective effects suggested
- CBD may reduce alcohol-induced liver damage
- Not proven in humans

---

## Potential Interactions

### Liver Processing

Both affect liver:
- Both metabolized by CYP450 enzymes
- Could theoretically compete
- Practical impact unclear
- No significant interaction documented

### Enhanced Impairment

The real concern:
- Don't drive or operate machinery
- Be aware of increased drowsiness
- Reaction time may be affected
- Err on side of caution

---

## How to Approach Combining

### If You Choose to Combine

**Start cautiously:**
- Use lower doses of both
- Note how you feel
- Don't drive regardless
- Stay in safe environment

**Timing options:**
- Same time (most interaction)
- CBD before drinking (may reduce anxiety)
- CBD after drinking (recovery support?)
- Experiment carefully

### Dose Considerations

| Scenario | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| **Light drinking** | Normal CBD dose likely fine |
| **Heavy drinking** | Reduce CBD dose or skip |
| **CBD-focused, one drink** | Normal CBD, minimal alcohol |
| **Alcohol-focused** | Consider skipping CBD |

## Potential Benefits

### Some Suggest CBD May:

- Reduce some alcohol-related anxiety
- Support next-day recovery (anecdotal)
- Provide harm reduction aspects
- Help with relaxation without drinking more

**Caution:** These aren't proven benefits—just reported experiences.

### For Reducing Alcohol Intake

Some use CBD to:
- Drink less (CBD provides relaxation)
- Manage social anxiety without alcohol
- Support alcohol reduction goals
- Not proven, but reported

---

## Potential Concerns

### Overconsumption Risk

If CBD reduces "feeling drunk":
- May drink more than intended
- False sense of sobriety
- Impairment still present
- Dangerous for driving decisions

### Blood Pressure

Both may lower blood pressure:
- Alcohol causes vasodilation
- CBD may reduce BP
- Could cause dizziness
- Caution if BP issues exist

### Next Day

Combined use may affect:
- Sleep quality
- Next-day grogginess
- Hydration (alcohol dehydrates)
- Overall recovery

---

## Who Should Avoid Combining

### Don't Combine If:

- On medications that interact with alcohol or CBD
- Have liver disease
- Have low blood pressure
- Are driving or operating machinery
- Are pregnant or nursing
- Have substance use concerns

### Special Caution

| Situation | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Medications** | Check interactions |
| **Liver issues** | Avoid both together |
| **Blood pressure meds** | Monitor and use caution |
| **Recovery** | May not be appropriate |

## CBD for Alcohol Recovery

### Different Topic

Some research suggests CBD may help with:
- Alcohol withdrawal (very preliminary)
- Cravings (animal studies)
- Alcohol-induced damage (early research)

This is separate from recreational combining and requires medical guidance.

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **No dangerous interactions** documented
2. **Additive sedation** — expect more drowsiness
3. **Don't drive** — impairment may be increased
4. **Moderation** is key for both
5. **Individual response varies** — be cautious
6. **Skip if** on medications or have health concerns

---

## My Take

CBD and alcohol together isn't inherently dangerous, but it's not the ideal combination either. Both are relaxing/sedating, so together you may feel more impaired than expected.

If you occasionally have a drink or two and also use CBD, it's not a major concern. But be aware of the additive sedation, absolutely don't drive, and pay attention to how you feel.

One thing I'd caution against: assuming CBD will protect you from alcohol's effects. While some research hints at protective properties, it's not proven, and you shouldn't drink more thinking CBD is a safeguard.

The safest approach? Moderate both, or enjoy them at different times. There's no need to combine them routinely.
