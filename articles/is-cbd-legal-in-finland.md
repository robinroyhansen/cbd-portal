# Is CBD Legal in Finland? Complete 2026 Guide

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Answer

CBD legality in Finland is **complex and restrictive**. Finland classifies most CBD products as medicines, requiring marketing authorization. The practical result is limited availability compared to other EU countries. While not outright banned, the regulatory framework makes accessing CBD products more difficult.

---

## Legal Framework

### Finnish Classification

Finland's approach:
- CBD classified as a medicine (generally)
- Requires marketing authorization for sale
- No approved CBD medicines in normal commerce
- Stricter than most EU countries
- Not following typical EU CBD interpretation

### Why It's Different

Finland interprets regulations differently:
- Medicine classification is broad
- Novel Food rules secondary to medicine classification
- Conservative regulatory approach
- Limited market consequences

## What This Means Practically

### Limited Availability

- Few domestic CBD retailers
- Products technically require approval
- Pharmacies main legitimate channel
- Online options exist but legally complex

### What You Might Find

- Some cosmetics with CBD
- Pharmacy products (limited)
- Online imports (buyer risk)
- Very limited selection

### What's Difficult

- CBD oils for internal use
- Supplements positioned as foods
- Wide product variety
- Competitive pricing

## Traveling with CBD to Finland

### Important Caution

Bringing CBD into Finland:
- Products legal elsewhere may face scrutiny
- Medicine classification complicates things
- Customs may question
- Consider leaving CBD at home

### If You Must Bring CBD

- Original packaging
- Documentation of THC content
- Be prepared for questions

## My Take

Finland is one of Europe's most challenging CBD markets, similar to Sweden and Norway. The medicine classification effectively limits what's available without going through pharmaceutical approval processes.

If you're in Finland and want CBD, pharmacy options are safest but extremely limited. Online ordering from EU countries is possible but carries regulatory uncertainty.

For visitors, I'd suggest not bringing CBD to Finland. The regulatory environment simply isn't welcoming, and it's not worth the hassle for customs questions.

This isn't about safety—CBD is safe—it's about Finnish regulatory interpretation that hasn't adapted to how most of Europe treats CBD products.
