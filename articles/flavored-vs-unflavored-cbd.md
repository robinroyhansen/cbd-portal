# Flavoured vs. Unflavoured CBD: Which Is Better?

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Flavoured CBD oils mask the natural hemp taste with mint, citrus, berry, or other flavours, making them more palatable for many users. Unflavoured (natural) CBD oil has an earthy, herbaceous taste that some find unpleasant but others appreciate. Neither is objectively "better"—it's a matter of personal preference and priorities.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Flavoured masks natural hemp taste
- Unflavoured has earthy hemp flavour
- Both can be equally effective
- Choice is personal preference
- Check flavouring ingredients

---

## The Natural CBD Taste

### What Unflavoured Tastes Like

**Common Descriptions:**
- Earthy
- Grassy
- Herbaceous
- Slightly bitter
- Nutty (especially hemp seed carrier)
- "Hempy"

### Why It Tastes This Way

**Contributing Factors:**
- Chlorophyll
- Terpenes
- Plant compounds
- Carrier oil (hemp seed has stronger taste)
- Spectrum type (full-spectrum is stronger)

## Common CBD Flavours

### Popular Options

**Mint/Peppermint:**
- Most popular
- Fresh, clean taste
- Masks hemp effectively
- Familiar flavour

**Citrus (Orange, Lemon):**
- Bright, pleasant taste
- Works well with hemp
- Common choice
- Natural pairing

**Berry:**
- Sweet option
- Multiple varieties
- Good taste masking
- Popular choice

**Vanilla:**
- Mild, sweet
- Subtle flavour
- Good for mixing
- Versatile

**Natural/Unflavoured:**
- Hemp taste present
- "Pure" experience
- For those who don't mind
- Often less processed

## Flavoured CBD

### How Flavouring Works

**Methods:**
- Natural flavour extracts added
- Essential oils used
- Flavour compounds mixed in
- After CBD is in carrier oil

### Pros of Flavoured

**Advantages:**
- Easier to take
- More pleasant experience
- Better compliance (you'll use it)
- Options for preferences
- Masks bitter compounds

### Cons of Flavoured

**Considerations:**
- Additional ingredients
- May contain artificial flavours
- Could affect purity
- Some may be sensitive
- Less "natural" feel

---

## Unflavoured CBD

### The Natural Experience

**What You Get:**
- CBD + carrier oil only
- True hemp taste
- Minimal additives
- "Pure" product

### Pros of Unflavoured

**Advantages:**
- Fewer ingredients
- Cleaner formulation
- Know exactly what's in it
- No potential flavour sensitivities
- Often less expensive

### Cons of Unflavoured

**Considerations:**
- Taste can be challenging
- May reduce compliance
- Not pleasant for many
- Harder to take consistently

## Effectiveness Comparison

### Does Flavour Affect CBD Potency?

**Short Answer:** No (if formulated properly)

**Both Should Deliver:**
- Same CBD content
- Same absorption
- Same effects
- Same quality

**What Matters More:**
- Actual CBD content
- Product quality
- Proper formulation
- Your consistency of use

## Spectrum Type and Taste

### How Spectrum Affects Taste

| Type | Taste Intensity |
|------|-----------------|
| Full-spectrum | Strongest |
| Broad-spectrum | Moderate |
| Isolate | Mildest |

### Isolate + Flavour

CBD isolate with MCT oil and flavouring can taste almost nothing like hemp—just the flavour.

### Full-Spectrum + Flavour

Even flavoured full-spectrum products retain some hemp character, but it's significantly reduced.

**Good Products Use:**
- Natural flavour extracts

## Adapting to Natural Taste

### If You Want to Try Unflavoured

**Tips:**
- Start with isolate (mildest)
- Chase with juice or water
- Mix into food/drink
- Hold breath while taking
- Give it time—many adapt

### Why Some Prefer Natural

- Fewer additives
- "Pure" experience
- Association with effectiveness
- Acquired taste develops
- Prefer knowing exact contents

## Making Your Choice

### Choose Flavoured If

- Hemp taste bothers you
- You'll be more consistent with pleasant taste
- You want enjoyable experience
- New to CBD
- Taking sublingually regularly

### Choose Unflavoured If

- You don't mind or like hemp taste
- You want minimal ingredients
- You have flavour sensitivities
- You prefer "pure" products
- You're mixing into food anyway

## Carrier Oil and Taste

### How Carrier Affects Overall Taste

| Carrier | Taste Profile |
|---------|---------------|
| MCT | Neutral, mild |
| Hemp seed | Nutty, earthy |
| Olive | Distinctive, strong |
| Sunflower | Light, mild |

### Best Combinations

**For Mildest Taste:**
- Isolate + MCT + natural flavour

**For Strongest Natural:**
- Full-spectrum + hemp seed carrier

## Format Alternatives

### If Taste Is a Major Issue

**Consider:**
- Capsules (no taste)
- Gummies (candy taste)
- Topicals (not consumed)
- Softgels (swallowed whole)

These bypass the taste issue entirely.

---

## My Take

Neither flavoured nor unflavoured is inherently better—what matters is that you actually take your CBD consistently. If natural hemp taste makes you dread taking CBD, you won't use it regularly, and it won't work. A quality flavoured product solves that problem. But if you can handle or even enjoy the natural taste, unflavoured offers a simpler, cleaner formulation. Choose what you'll actually use, and make sure the flavouring (if present) comes from natural sources without unnecessary additives.
