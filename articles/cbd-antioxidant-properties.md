# CBD's Antioxidant Properties: Scientific Overview

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

## Quick Summary

CBD has demonstrated antioxidant properties in laboratory studies, with the US government even holding a patent (US6630507) on cannabinoids as antioxidants. CBD can neutralise free radicals, reduce oxidative stress, and may protect cells from damage, though human clinical evidence remains limited.

---

## Key Takeaways

- CBD shows antioxidant activity in studies
- Can neutralise free radicals
- US patent recognises antioxidant properties
- May protect against oxidative damage
- Human research still developing

## Understanding Oxidative Stress

### What It Is

**The Process:**
- Imbalance between free radicals and antioxidants
- Free radicals damage cells
- Natural process, but excess is harmful
- Linked to aging and disease

### Free Radicals

**Reactive Oxygen Species (ROS):**
- Unstable molecules
- Seek electrons from other molecules
- Damage DNA, proteins, lipids
- Produced during metabolism

### Why Antioxidants Matter

- Neutralise free radicals
- Prevent cellular damage
- Support cellular health
- Balance oxidative processes

## CBD as an Antioxidant

### Chemical Structure

**What Makes CBD Antioxidant:**
- Phenolic ring structure
- Electron donation capability
- Radical scavenging ability
- Similar to other plant antioxidants

### US Patent 6630507

**Recognition:**
- Held by US Department of Health
- "Cannabinoids as antioxidants and neuroprotectants"
- Filed 1999, granted 2003
- Acknowledges antioxidant properties

**Findings:**

### Animal Studies

**Demonstrated:**

### Human Studies

**Current Status:**
- Limited direct studies

- More research needed
- Mechanism translation uncertain
