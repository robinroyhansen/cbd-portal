# Sabinene: The Spicy, Piney Bicyclic Terpene

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

## Quick Summary

Sabinene is a bicyclic monoterpene with a spicy, piney, and citrus aroma. Found in black pepper, Norway spruce, and cannabis, sabinene shows potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It contributes to the complex spicy notes in some cannabis strains.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Spicy, piney, citrus aroma
- Significant anti-inflammatory potential
- Strong antioxidant properties
- Found in black pepper, cardamom, nutmeg, and cannabis
- Contributes to warming, spicy quality

---

## What Is Sabinene?

Sabinene is a bicyclic monoterpene contributing to the spicy, warming quality of many essential oils and spices. It's one of the main components of carrot seed oil and contributes to black pepper's aroma.

Natural sources:
- **Black pepper** — Contributing to spiciness
- **Cardamom** — Part of complex spice aroma
- **Nutmeg** — Spice component
- **Norway spruce** — Significant content
- **Cannabis** — Various strains
