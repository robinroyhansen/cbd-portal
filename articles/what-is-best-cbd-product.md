## Product Types Compared

### CBD Oil/Tincture

**Best For:**
- Flexible dosing
- Faster absorption
- Dose customisation
- Sublingual use

**Pros:**
- Quick onset (15-30 min)
- Easy to adjust dose
- Versatile use
- Good value

**Cons:**
- Taste (some dislike)
- Less discrete
- Requires dropper use

### CBD Gummies

**Best For:**
- Convenience
- Taste preference
- Discrete use
- Consistent dosing

**Pros:**
- Tasty and enjoyable
- Pre-measured doses
- Easy to take
- Very discrete

**Cons:**
- Slower onset
- Fixed doses
- Added sugars
- More expensive per mg

### CBD Capsules

**Best For:**
- Precise dosing
- No taste concerns
- Supplement routine
- Travel convenience

**Pros:**
- Exact dose every time
- No taste
- Familiar format
- Easy to track

**Cons:**
- Fixed doses
- Slower absorption
- Can't adjust easily

### CBD Topicals

**Best For:**
- Local issues
- Skin conditions
- Muscle/joint discomfort
- Targeted application

**Pros:**
- Direct to area
- No systemic effects
- Easy to use
- Combine with massage

**Cons:**
- Limited absorption
- Local only
- Variable formulations

**Best Options:**

## Budget Considerations

### Best Value

- Oil typically cheapest per mg
- Larger bottles more economical
- Calculate cost/mg to compare
- Don't sacrifice quality

### Premium Worth It?

- Better testing often
- Cleaner products
- Not always necessary
- Research the brand

## My Take

The "best" CBD product is the one that fits your life and that you'll actually use consistently. A premium oil sitting unused is worth less than affordable gummies taken daily. Start with what appeals to you, ensure it's quality-tested, and adjust based on experience. Most people eventually find their preferred format.
