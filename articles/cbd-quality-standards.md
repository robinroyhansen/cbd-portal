# CBD Quality Standards: What Defines a Quality Product

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Quality CBD products meet standards including accurate potency labelling, absence of contaminants (pesticides, heavy metals, solvents), proper extraction methods, and transparent third-party testing. Understanding these standards helps you identify trustworthy products.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Quality requires third-party testing for potency and contaminants
- Accurate labelling (within 10% of claimed content)
- No harmful contaminants above safe limits
- Proper extraction and manufacturing practices
- Transparent companies provide easy COA access

---

## Potency Standards

### Accuracy Requirements

- CBD content within 10% of label claim
- THC within legal limits
- Consistent batch-to-batch results
- Clear per-serving calculations

### Testing Requirements

- Independent third-party lab
- Recent test dates
- Batch-specific testing
- ISO 17025 accredited lab preferred

---

## Contaminant Standards

### What Should Be Tested

- **Pesticides** — Agricultural chemicals
- **Heavy metals** — Lead, mercury, arsenic, cadmium
- **Residual solvents** — From extraction
- **Microbial** — Bacteria, mould, yeast

### Acceptable Levels

All contaminants should be:
- "ND" (not detected) ideally
- Below established safety limits
- "Pass" on COA

---

## Manufacturing Standards

### Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP)

- Controlled production environment
- Documented procedures
- Quality control at each stage
- Traceability throughout process

### EU-GMP

- European standard
- Higher requirements
- Relevant for pharmaceutical-grade products

---

## How to Identify Quality

### Checklist

✓ Third-party COA available
✓ CBD content matches label
✓ THC within legal limits
✓ Contaminant testing passed
✓ Recent test dates
✓ Reputable brand/company
✓ Clear product information

### Red Flags

✗ No COA available
✗ In-house testing only
✗ Old or missing test dates
✗ CBD significantly different from label
✗ Contaminant failures
✗ Unrealistic claims

---

## My Take

Quality standards exist to protect consumers. The CBD market has quality issues, making your due diligence essential. Ten minutes checking a COA can save you from ineffective or contaminated products. Prioritise transparency over price.
