# CBD and Antidepressants: Interactions and Safety

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

CBD can interact with many antidepressants due to liver enzyme inhibition, potentially increasing drug levels in your blood. SSRIs, SNRIs, tricyclics, and other antidepressants may be affected. While some people use CBD alongside antidepressants, this should only be done under medical supervision. Never stop or change antidepressant medication without consulting your doctor.

---

## Key Takeaways

- CBD interacts with many antidepressants
- Liver enzyme inhibition is the concern
- Can increase antidepressant levels
- Medical supervision essential
- Never change medications without doctor

---

## Why Interactions Occur

### The Liver Enzyme Issue

**How CBD Affects Drug Metabolism:**
- CBD inhibits CYP450 enzymes
- Particularly CYP2D6 and CYP3A4
- Many antidepressants use these enzymes
- Inhibition slows drug breakdown

**Result:**
Antidepressant levels may increase in your bloodstream.

---

## Types of Antidepressants and CBD

### SSRIs (Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors)

**Common SSRIs:**
- Sertraline (Zoloft)
- Fluoxetine (Prozac)
- Paroxetine (Paxil)
- Citalopram (Celexa)
- Escitalopram (Lexapro)

**Interaction Concern:**
- Many metabolised by CYP2D6
- CBD may increase levels
- Could intensify side effects
- Serotonin syndrome risk (theoretical)

### SNRIs (Serotonin-Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibitors)

**Common SNRIs:**
- Venlafaxine (Effexor)
- Duloxetine (Cymbalta)
- Desvenlafaxine (Pristiq)

**Interaction Concern:**
- CYP2D6 and CYP3A4 involved
- Similar concerns as SSRIs
- Blood pressure considerations

### Tricyclic Antidepressants

**Common Tricyclics:**
- Amitriptyline
- Nortriptyline
- Imipramine

**Interaction Concern:**
- Metabolised by CYP2D6
- Higher risk due to narrow therapeutic window
- Increased side effects possible
- More caution needed

### MAOIs (Monoamine Oxidase Inhibitors)

**Examples:**
- Phenelzine (Nardil)
- Tranylcypromine (Parnate)

**Interaction Concern:**
- Less well-studied with CBD
- Caution advised
- Complex interactions possible

### Atypical Antidepressants

**Examples:**
- Bupropion (Wellbutrin)
- Mirtazapine (Remeron)
- Trazodone

**Interaction Concern:**
- Varies by specific drug
- Bupropion: CYP2D6 involvement
- Mirtazapine: sedation additive
- Trazodone: enhanced sedation

## Potential Consequences

### Increased Antidepressant Levels

**May Cause:**
- Heightened side effects
- Drowsiness
- Nausea
- Dizziness
- Serotonin-related symptoms

### Serotonin Syndrome (Rare but Serious)

**What It Is:**
- Excess serotonin activity
- Can be dangerous
- Symptoms: agitation, rapid heart rate, high temperature

**With CBD:**
- Theoretical risk
- Not well-documented
- But caution warranted

### Enhanced Sedation

**If Both Are Sedating:**
- Increased drowsiness
- Impaired function
- Driving concerns
- Day-to-day impact

## Should You Use CBD with Antidepressants?

### The Conservative Approach

**Recommended:**
- Discuss with your doctor first
- Get medical supervision
- Start very low if approved
- Monitor closely

### Not Recommended

**Without Medical Guidance:**
- Starting CBD while on antidepressants
- Self-adjusting either medication
- Assuming it's safe because CBD is "natural"

---

## If Your Doctor Approves

### Harm Reduction

**Steps:**
1. Start with very low CBD dose
2. Increase slowly with monitoring
3. Watch for side effects
4. Regular check-ins with doctor
5. Report any changes

### Monitoring

**Watch For:**
- Increased drowsiness
- Mood changes
- Serotonin symptoms
- Any new or worsening symptoms

## Important Warnings

### Never Do These

**Without Medical Guidance:**
- Stop your antidepressant to use CBD
- Reduce antidepressant dose on your own
- Assume CBD is a replacement
- Use high doses of CBD

### Mental Health Priority

**Remember:**
- Antidepressants serve important purpose
- CBD is not a proven antidepressant replacement
- Mental health stability matters
- Work with your healthcare team

## My Take

Combining CBD with antidepressants is not something to take lightly. The interaction potential is real, and antidepressants serve a critical function for those who need them. If you're interested in CBD while taking antidepressants, the only appropriate path is discussing it with your prescribing doctor. They can evaluate your specific situation, adjust monitoring, or advise against it if needed. Never make this decision alone, and never stop or reduce antidepressants without medical guidance.
