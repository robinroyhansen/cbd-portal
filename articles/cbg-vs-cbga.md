# CBG vs CBGA: What's the Difference?

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Answer

CBG (Cannabigerol) and CBGA (Cannabigerolic Acid) are the same cannabinoid in different forms. CBGA is the acidic precursor that converts to CBG through heat or time. CBGA is also called "the mother of all cannabinoids" because it converts into THCA, CBDA, and CBCAâ€”the precursors to THC, CBD, and CBC. Neither produces psychoactive effects.

---

## The Relationship Explained

Think of CBGA and CBG as the same molecule in different stages:

**CBGA (Acidic Form)**
- Raw, unheated form found in live cannabis
- Contains a carboxyl group (-COOH)
- Converts to other cannabinoid acids
- "Mother cannabinoid" of the plant

**CBG (Neutral Form)**
- Created when CBGA loses its carboxyl group
- Happens through heat (decarboxylation) or time
- More stable than CBGA
- Found in cured, dried, or heated cannabis

## CBG vs CBGA Comparison

| Property | CBGA | CBG |
|----------|------|-----|
| Form | Acidic (raw) | Neutral (decarboxylated) |
| Found in | Fresh/live cannabis | Cured/heated cannabis |
| Psychoactive | No | No |
| Stability | Less stable | More stable |
| Research | Limited | Growing |
| Products | Rare | Emerging |

---

## Why CBGA Matters

CBGA serves a unique role in cannabis:

1. **Precursor to major cannabinoids:** Enzymes convert CBGA into THCA, CBDA, or CBCA
2. **Limited presence:** Most CBGA converts to other acids, leaving little in mature plants
3. **Harvesting challenge:** Getting high CBGA requires harvesting young plants

## Potential Benefits

**CBGA Research Suggests:**
- Anti-inflammatory properties
- Potential metabolic effects
- Cardiovascular research interest
- COVID-19 receptor binding studies (preliminary)

**CBG Research Suggests:**
- Antibacterial properties (including against MRSA)
- Neuroprotective potential
- Anti-inflammatory effects
- Appetite stimulation without THC's high

Both remain under-researched compared to CBD and THC.

---

## Which Should You Choose?

**Consider CBG if:**
- You want a stable, widely available product
- Products are easier to find
- More research exists on effects

**Consider CBGA if:**
- You prefer raw/unprocessed cannabinoids
- You're making juices or raw preparations
- Note: Pure CBGA products are rare

For most people, CBG products are more practical and accessible.

---

## How to Get Each Form

**For CBG:**
- CBG oils and tinctures
- CBG isolate
- Broad-spectrum products with CBG
- Flower from high-CBG strains (when heated)

**For CBGA:**
- Raw cannabis juice
- Fresh/frozen cannabis products
- Specialized CBGA extracts (uncommon)

---

## My Take

The CBG vs CBGA distinction matters mainly for those interested in raw cannabinoids. For most consumers, CBG products (the decarboxylated form) are what you'll encounter and use. CBG shows interesting potential, particularly for antibacterial applications, but remains less researched than CBD. If you're curious about CBG, start with quality CBG oil from a reputable source.
