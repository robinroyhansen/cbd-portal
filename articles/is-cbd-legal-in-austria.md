# Is CBD Legal in Austria? Complete 2026 Guide

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Answer

CBD is **legal in Austria** with products containing less than 0.3% THC. Austria follows EU hemp regulations but has faced some regulatory changes over the years. CBD flowers have faced restrictions, but oils and other products remain available. The market is well-developed with many purchase options.

---

## Legal Framework

### Current Status

Austria's CBD regulations:
- Hemp cultivation legal (EU varieties)
- THC limit: 0.3% (slightly higher than EU standard 0.2%)
- CBD extracts and products permitted
- Novel Food regulations apply
- Some restrictions on CBD flowers

### Recent History

Austria's CBD regulations have evolved:
- CBD was widely available
- 2018-2019: Restrictions on flowers introduced
- Ongoing regulatory clarifications
- Market adapted and continues

---

## THC Limit

### The 0.3% Rule

Austria permits slightly higher THC than some EU neighbors:
- 0.3% THC limit (vs 0.2% in many countries)
- Applied to finished products
- Allows for broader product selection
- Still considered non-psychoactive

## My Take

Austria offers a good CBD market, particularly in Vienna where you'll find numerous specialized shops. The 0.3% THC limit provides slightly more flexibility than the 0.2% standard in some neighboring countries.

The CBD flower situation has been more complicated than oils and other products, so if that's your preference, check current availability. For oils, capsules, and topicals, you'll have plenty of quality options.

Austrian consumers tend to value quality, and this shows in the products available. Stick to established shops and brands with proper testing, and you'll find good options.
