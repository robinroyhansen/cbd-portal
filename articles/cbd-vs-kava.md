# CBD vs Kava: Comparing Relaxation Supplements

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Comparison

| Factor | CBD | Kava |
|--------|-----|------|
| **Source** | Hemp plant | Piper methysticum root |
| **Origin** | Various (global) | South Pacific islands |
| **Primary Use** | Anxiety, pain, sleep | Anxiety, relaxation |
| **Mechanism** | ECS, serotonin, GABA | GABAergic, dopaminergic |
| **Effect Feel** | Calm without sedation | Relaxed, mild euphoria |
| **Liver Concerns** | Minimal | Yes (discussed below) |
| **Legal Status** | Mostly legal (varies) | Legal in most countries |

---

## How They Work

### CBD's Mechanism

CBD works through multiple pathways:
- Endocannabinoid system modulation
- Serotonin receptor interaction
- GABA enhancement
- Anti-inflammatory effects

CBD promotes calm without significant psychoactive effects.

### Kava's Mechanism

Kava works primarily through:
- GABA receptor modulation
- Dopamine receptor effects
- Norepinephrine reuptake
- Kavalactones are active compounds

Kava produces noticeable relaxation and mild euphoria for many users.

## For Anxiety

### CBD for Anxiety

**Strengths:**
- Multiple mechanisms
- Research supporting anxiety use
- Versatile for different anxiety types
- Can be used daily
- Non-intoxicating

**Limitations:**
- Effects may be subtle
- May take time to notice
- Individual response varies

### Kava for Anxiety

**Strengths:**
- Noticeable effect for most users
- Rapid onset
- Traditional use for social anxiety
- Effective for acute anxiety

**Limitations:**
- Liver safety questions
- Can cause drowsiness
- Tolerance may develop
- Some find effects too strong

**For Anxiety:** Both work; kava may be more immediately noticeable, CBD potentially safer for long-term use.

---

## For Sleep

### CBD for Sleep

- Addresses anxiety preventing sleep
- Promotes relaxation
- Non-sedating at moderate doses
- Higher doses more sleep-supportive
- Good for anxiety-related insomnia

### Kava for Sleep

- Directly sedating
- Relaxes muscles
- May cause drowsiness
- Traditional sleep use
- More "knock-out" effect

**For Sleep:** Kava more directly sedating; CBD better for anxiety-related sleep issues without heavy sedation.

---

## The Liver Question

### Kava and Liver Safety

This is the significant concern with kava:
- Reports of liver damage (rare but serious)
- Led to bans in some countries (reversed in most)
- Risk factors unclear
- Quality of kava matters
- Traditional preparation may be safer

**Current consensus:**
- Noble kava varieties are safer
- Proper preparation important
- Alcohol use increases risk
- Pre-existing liver conditions: avoid
- Not for daily long-term use

### CBD and Liver Safety

- High doses may affect liver enzymes
- Generally well-tolerated at typical doses
- Drug interactions possible (liver enzymes)
- Safer profile for long-term use
- Routine use generally not concerning

## Effect Experience

### What CBD Feels Like

- Subtle calm
- Reduced anxiety
- May not notice dramatic change
- Clarity maintained
- Not intoxicating

### What Kava Feels Like

- Noticeable relaxation
- Muscle relaxation
- Mild euphoria (sometimes)
- Social ease
- Can be sedating

## Side Effects

### CBD Side Effects

Generally mild:
- Dry mouth
- Drowsiness (dose-dependent)
- Potential drug interactions
- Mild GI effects sometimes

### Kava Side Effects

More notable:
- Drowsiness
- Liver concerns (discussed above)
- "Kava dermopathy" (skin issues with heavy use)
- Coordination effects
- Not safe with alcohol
- Can interact with medications

---

## Drug Interactions

### CBD Interactions

- Affects CYP450 enzymes
- Multiple potential interactions
- Check with pharmacist
- Common medications may interact

### Kava Interactions

- Avoid with alcohol (important)
- Sedatives/benzodiazepines interaction
- Liver-processed medications
- Antidepressants (some)
- More interaction concerns

## Legality

### CBD Legal Status

- Legal in most countries (with THC limits)
- Varies by region
- Generally accessible

### Kava Legal Status

- Legal in most countries
- Was banned in some European countries (most reversed)
- Generally available
- Australia: legal but regulated

## Quality Considerations

### CBD Quality

- Third-party tested
- THC content verified
- Reputable brand
- Extraction method matters

### Kava Quality

**Critical for safety:**
- "Noble" kava varieties only
- Root only (not leaves/stems)
- Reputable source
- Traditional or CO2 extraction
- Avoid alcohol-extracted products

Poor quality kava is linked to safety concerns. Noble varieties from root are traditional and safer.

---

## Dosing

### CBD Dosing

- 20-50mg for anxiety/relaxation
- Start low, adjust
- Can use daily
- Flexible timing

### Kava Dosing

- 100-250mg kavalactones typical
- Don't exceed recommended
- Not for daily long-term use
- Better for occasional/situational use

---

## Using Together

### Can You Combine?

Possible but:
- Both affect GABA system
- Combined sedation risk
- Start with low doses
- Not recommended initially
- If combining, reduce both doses

---

## Which to Choose?

### Choose CBD If:

- You want daily wellness support
- Liver health is a concern
- You take other medications
- You want subtle, sustained effects
- Long-term use planned
- You also have pain or inflammation

### Choose Kava If:

- You want noticeable relaxation
- Occasional/situational use
- Social anxiety specifically
- You don't drink alcohol
- Liver is healthy
- You want a traditional approach

### Consider Neither If:

- Liver problems (especially kava)
- Pregnant/nursing
- On medications (check interactions)
- Alcohol use is regular (especially kava)

## Key Takeaways

1. **Both reduce anxiety** — different approaches
2. **Kava more immediately noticeable** in effect
3. **CBD likely safer** for long-term use
4. **Kava has liver concerns** — quality matters critically
5. **CBD more versatile** (pain, inflammation, etc.)
6. **Kava better for occasional use** rather than daily

---

## My Take

These are quite different supplements despite both being used for relaxation.

CBD is the safer choice for regular, long-term use. Its effects are more subtle but come without the liver concerns that follow kava. If you want something you can take daily for general anxiety management, CBD makes more sense.

Kava is genuinely relaxing in a way you'll notice—it's not subtle. For occasional use, especially social situations or acute anxiety, quality kava can be very effective. But I wouldn't use it daily, and the liver concerns mean quality matters enormously. Only use noble kava from reputable sources.

If you drink alcohol regularly, avoid kava entirely. The combination is problematic for liver health.

For most people seeking long-term anxiety support, CBD is the more practical choice. For occasional, noticeable relaxation when you need it, quality kava has a role—just be informed about the considerations.
