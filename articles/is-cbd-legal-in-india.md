# Is CBD Legal in India? Complete 2026 Guide

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Answer

CBD exists in a **legal gray area in India**. While the Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances (NDPS) Act controls cannabis, hemp-derived CBD with negligible THC is not explicitly prohibited. Some CBD products are sold, but the market operates without clear regulatory framework. Confusion remains about what's definitively legal.

---

## India's Legal Framework

### The NDPS Act

The 1985 Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act:
- Controls cannabis (charas, ganja, hashish)
- Specifically regulates flowering tops and resin
- Hemp fiber and seeds not controlled
- CBD not explicitly mentioned

### The Gray Area

Key ambiguity:
- CBD not scheduled as controlled substance
- Hemp cultivation possible with permits
- No specific CBD regulations
- Products exist in legal limbo

## My Take

India's CBD situation is genuinely confusing. Products are sold openly in some contexts, yet there's no clear regulatory framework that definitively permits them. The NDPS Act focuses on parts of the cannabis plant (flowering tops, resin) rather than CBD specifically, creating ambiguity.

For visitors, I'd strongly recommend not bringing CBD to India. The gray area offers no protection, and customs interactions aren't worth the risk. For residents interested in CBD, products exist but without the quality assurances you'd find in regulated markets.

The hemp cultivation permissions in states like Uttarakhand suggest India may be moving toward clearer regulations eventually, but for now, uncertainty prevails.
