# Why CBD Works Differently for Different People

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

CBD's effects vary significantly between individuals due to genetics, body composition, endocannabinoid system differences, metabolism, and other factors. Understanding this variability helps explain why some people experience strong effects while others notice little, and why dosing is so personal.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Individual response to CBD varies widely
- Genetics play a significant role
- Body composition affects dosing
- Endocannabinoid systems differ
- Personal experimentation often necessary

---

## Genetic Factors

### Cytochrome P450 Enzymes

**Why It Matters:**
- CBD metabolised by CYP enzymes
- CYP2C19, CYP3A4 key enzymes
- Genetic variations affect activity
- Some people fast/slow metabolisers

**Implications:**
- Fast metabolisers: Lower CBD levels
- Slow metabolisers: Higher CBD levels
- Same dose, different effects
- May need dose adjustment

### Endocannabinoid System Genes

**Variations In:**
- CB1 receptor density
- CB2 receptor expression
- FAAH enzyme activity
- Anandamide production

**Result:**
- Different baseline ECS function
- Varying sensitivity to CBD
- Individual optimal doses
- Personal response patterns

## Body Composition

### Body Weight

**General Principle:**
- Larger bodies may need more CBD
- Not linear relationship
- Starting point for dosing
- Adjust based on response

### Body Fat

**CBD Is Lipophilic:**
- Stores in fat tissue
- Higher fat may mean more storage
- Affects duration and accumulation
- Influences steady-state levels

### Metabolism Rate

**Faster Metabolism:**
- Quicker CBD processing
- May need more frequent dosing
- Different optimal timing
- Individual variation
