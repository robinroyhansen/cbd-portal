# What is Humulene?

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Answer

Humulene is a [terpene](/knowledge/what-are-terpenes) with an earthy, woody, hoppy aroma. Best known as the primary terpene in hops (hence the name, from *Humulus lupulus*), humulene is associated with anti-inflammatory, appetite-suppressing, and antibacterial properties. Unlike many terpenes, humulene may reduce rather than stimulate appetite.

---

## Humulene Profile

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Chemical formula** | C₁₅H₂₄ |
| **Aroma** | Earthy, woody, hoppy, subtle spice |
| **Boiling point** | 106°C (223°F) |
| **Prevalence in cannabis** | Moderate |
| **Also found in** | Hops, sage, ginseng, coriander |

---

## Humulene and Caryophyllene

Humulene is chemically related to [beta-caryophyllene](/knowledge/what-is-caryophyllene)—they're isomers (same formula, different structure). They're often found together in cannabis and may work synergistically:

- Both have anti-inflammatory properties
- Both contribute to earthy/spicy profiles
- Caryophyllene activates CB2; humulene's mechanism differs
- Combined presence may enhance effects

---

## Potential Effects and Benefits

Research on humulene suggests:

**Anti-inflammatory**
- Well-documented anti-inflammatory activity
- Comparable to dexamethasone in some studies
- May complement CBD's effects

**Appetite Suppression**
- Unlike most cannabis compounds
- May counteract THC's appetite stimulation
- Interest for weight management research

**Antibacterial**
- Active against various bacteria
- Including Staphylococcus aureus
- Traditional use supports this

**Antitumor (Preclinical)**
- Early research shows promise
- Animal and in-vitro studies only
- Much more research needed

---

## The Appetite Angle

Cannabis is often associated with increased appetite ("the munchies"). Humulene is interesting because it may do the opposite:

**Research shows:**
- Appetite-suppressing effects in animal studies
- May explain why some strains don't increase appetite
- Potential for weight management applications

This makes humulene-rich products potentially useful for those concerned about appetite effects.

---

## Humulene in CBD Products

Products high in humulene may be marketed as:
- Anti-inflammatory formulas
- "Balanced" profiles
- Earthy/herbal blends
- Products for those avoiding appetite stimulation

---

## The Beer Connection

Humulene gives beer its characteristic hoppy bitterness and aroma. The relaxing effect often attributed to beer (beyond alcohol) may partly stem from humulene and other terpenes in hops. This historical use demonstrates long human exposure to humulene.

---

## Research Status

Humulene research includes:
- Anti-inflammatory mechanism studies
- Appetite and metabolism research
- Antibacterial activity studies
- Early antitumor investigations

However, human clinical trials specifically on humulene remain limited.

---

## My Take

Humulene doesn't get the attention of more aromatic terpenes, but its potential for anti-inflammatory effects without appetite stimulation makes it particularly interesting. For users who want CBD's benefits but not increased hunger, paying attention to humulene content may be worthwhile. The earthy, grounded quality it adds to products is also pleasant without being overpowering.
