## Quick Summary

Plantar fasciitis causes stabbing heel pain that's worst in the morning. CBD's anti-inflammatory properties and potential pain relief make it appealing for this common condition. Here's how to approach using CBD for plantar fasciitis.

---

## Understanding Plantar Fasciitis

### What It Is

Condition:
- Inflammation of plantar fascia
- Band connecting heel to toes
- Causes heel pain
- Common in runners, workers who stand

### Typical Symptoms

Experience:
- Sharp heel pain
- Worst with first steps in morning
- Pain after long standing
- Improves with movement then worsens

---

## How CBD May Help

### Anti-Inflammatory Action

Potential benefit:
- Addresses inflammation
- Key driver of plantar fasciitis
- May reduce pain
- Supports healing environment

### Pain Management

Effects:
- General analgesic properties
- May reduce discomfort
- Allows continued activity
- Part of management approach

---

## Using CBD for Plantar Fasciitis

### Topical Application

Recommended:
- CBD cream/balm to heel and arch
- Massage into foot
- Apply morning and evening
- Before and after activity

### Oral CBD

Systemic approach:
- Supports overall inflammation reduction
- 20-30mg typical
- Consistent daily use
- Addresses from inside

### Combination Approach

Most effective:
- Topical to foot directly
- Oral for systemic support
- Along with other treatments
- Comprehensive strategy

---

## Complementary Treatments

### Essential

Also needed:
- Stretching (calf and plantar fascia)
- Proper footwear
- Arch supports/orthotics
- Ice after activity
- Rest when needed

### Physical Therapy

May include:
- Stretching protocols
- Strengthening exercises
- Manual therapy
- Guidance on activity

### When Severe

Medical options:
- Night splints
- Cortisone injections
- Physical therapy
- Rarely surgery

---

## Morning Routine

### For First-Step Pain

Approach:
- Stretch foot before getting up
- Apply CBD topical
- Wear supportive shoes immediately
- Gentle warm-up before walking far

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **CBD may help** through anti-inflammatory effects
2. **Topical application** to heel and arch
3. **Combine with** stretching and proper footwear
4. **Addresses symptoms** not the root cause alone
5. **Persistent cases** need professional evaluation
