By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry

## Quick Summary

The gut has abundant cannabinoid receptors, making it responsive to CBD. Early research suggests CBD may support digestive health through anti-inflammatory effects and gut motility regulation. While research is limited, many people report digestive benefits from CBD use.

---

## The Gut-Endocannabinoid Connection

### Cannabinoid Receptors in the Gut

**Rich presence:**
- CB1 and CB2 receptors throughout GI tract
- Enteric nervous system involvement
- Gut-brain axis connection
- Regulatory role in digestion

### What This Means

**Implications:**
- Gut responds to cannabinoids
- CBD may influence gut function
- Inflammation modulation possible
- Multiple mechanisms involved

## How to Use CBD for Digestion

### Product Selection

**Recommended forms:**
- CBD oil (flexible dosing)
- Capsules (consistent, gut delivery)
- Avoid edibles with sugar/additives

**Considerations:**
- Full-spectrum may provide entourage effect
- Some prefer capsules for gut delivery
- Oil allows dose adjustment

### Dosing

**Starting approach:**
- Begin low: 10-20mg daily
- Take with meals
- Assess over 2-4 weeks
- Increase gradually if needed

**For specific conditions:**
- Higher doses may be needed
- Work with healthcare provider
- Monitor symptoms carefully

### Timing

**When to take:**
- With meals for better absorption
- Consistent timing helps
- Split doses may maintain levels
- Evening if also using for sleep

## My Take

The gut-endocannabinoid connection is fascinating, and CBD's potential for digestive support makes biological sense. The anti-inflammatory effects and gut-brain axis modulation provide plausible mechanisms for benefit.

I've heard many accounts of people finding relief from IBS symptoms, stress-related digestive issues, and general gut discomfort with CBD. The research, while limited, is encouraging.

That said, don't use CBD as a substitute for proper diagnosis and treatment. If you have significant digestive symptoms, see a gastroenterologist. CBD can be a helpful addition to a comprehensive approach, but it's not a cure-all for gut issues.
