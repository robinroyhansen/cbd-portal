# Is CBD Safe? What the Research Shows

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Answer

Yes, **CBD is generally considered safe** based on extensive research and real-world use. The World Health Organization (WHO) states CBD is "generally well tolerated with a good safety profile." It's non-intoxicating, non-addictive, and has no known lethal dose. The main concerns are drug interactions and product quality.

## Research Evidence

### Clinical Trial Data

From Epidiolex trials (FDA-approved CBD):
- Doses up to 1,500mg/day studied
- Side effects generally mild
- Liver enzymes monitored (elevated in some)
- No life-threatening adverse events

### Real-World Use

Millions of people use CBD:
- Generally positive safety record
- Side effects typically mild
- No pattern of serious harm
- Years of commercial use

## What Are the Risks?

### Known Risks (Low)

| Risk | Level | Who's Affected |
|------|-------|---------------|
| **Drug interactions** | Moderate | Those on medications |
| **Side effects** | Low (mild) | Some users |
| **Liver effects** | Low | High-dose users, liver conditions |
| **Product quality** | Variable | Users of untested products |

### Drug Interactions

The main safety concern:
- CBD affects liver enzymes (CYP450)
- Can increase levels of some medications
- Blood thinners, seizure meds particularly relevant
- Always check interactions

### Mild Side Effects

Generally manageable:
- Dry mouth
- Drowsiness (dose-dependent)
- Diarrhea (some users)
- Appetite changes

### Liver Considerations

At high doses:
- Liver enzymes may elevate
- Usually reversible
- Clinical trial doses (not typical wellness doses)
- Monitor if on other liver-affecting drugs

## Who Should Be Cautious?

### Higher Risk Groups

| Group | Concern | Recommendation |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| **Pregnant/nursing** | Insufficient safety data | Avoid |
| **Children** | Limited research | Medical supervision only |
| **On medications** | Drug interactions | Check with pharmacist |
| **Liver disease** | Liver metabolism | Consult doctor |
| **Upcoming surgery** | Possible bleeding/anesthesia interaction | Inform surgeon |

### When to Consult a Doctor

- Before starting if on medications
- If you have liver conditions
- If pregnant or planning pregnancy
- Before giving to children
- If you have serious health conditions

## Product Quality: A Real Concern

### The Quality Problem

Not all CBD products are equal:
- Unregulated market (especially US)
- Some products don't contain claimed CBD
- Some contain undisclosed THC
- Contaminants possible (pesticides, heavy metals)

### Why It Matters

Poor quality products can:
- Not provide expected benefits
- Contain unexpected THC
- Contain harmful contaminants
- Undermine confidence in CBD

### How to Ensure Safety

1. **Choose tested products** — Third-party lab testing
2. **Verify THC content** — Check lab reports
3. **Buy from reputable brands** — Established companies
4. **Check for contaminants** — Heavy metals, pesticides
5. **Read reviews** — Real user experiences

## Safe Use Guidelines

### Best Practices

1. **Start with low dose** — 10-20mg
2. **Increase gradually** — Allow time to assess
3. **Check drug interactions** — Especially blood thinners
4. **Use quality products** — Third-party tested
5. **Monitor your response** — Note effects
6. **Consult provider** — If on medications or have conditions

### Red Flags

Stop use and consult doctor if:
- Unusual symptoms develop
- Signs of liver problems (jaundice, pain)
- Severe side effects
- Interactions with medications noticed

## My Take

Based on the research and real-world evidence, CBD is remarkably safe. It's non-intoxicating, non-addictive, and has no known lethal dose—properties many commonly used substances can't claim.

The two things to pay attention to are drug interactions and product quality. If you take medications, particularly blood thinners or seizure medications, checking interactions is essential. And buying from reputable sources with third-party testing ensures you're getting what you expect.

For most healthy adults not on significant medications, CBD presents minimal risk. The side effects that do occur are generally mild and manageable. This excellent safety profile is one reason CBD has become so widely used globally.
