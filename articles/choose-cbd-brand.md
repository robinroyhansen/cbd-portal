# How to Choose a CBD Brand

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Choosing a CBD brand requires evaluating third-party testing, transparency, reputation, product quality, and customer service. Look for brands that publish current Certificates of Analysis (COAs), have clear sourcing information, and maintain a solid track record. Avoid brands that make medical claims or lack transparency.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Third-party testing is non-negotiable
- Transparency indicates trustworthiness
- Check reputation and reviews
- Avoid exaggerated claims
- Customer service matters

---

## Essential Criteria

### 1. Third-Party Testing

**Must Have:**
- Current lab reports (COAs)
- Independent laboratory
- Batch-specific testing
- Easy to access results

**What Should Be Tested:**
- Cannabinoid potency
- Pesticides
- Heavy metals
- Residual solvents
- Microbial contaminants

### 2. Transparency

**Look For:**
- Clear ingredient lists
- Source information
- Extraction method stated
- Company information visible
- Contact details available

**Red Flag:**
- Hidden company details
- Vague sourcing
- No lab reports
- Difficult to contact

---

## Evaluating Brand Quality

### Company Background

**Research:**
- How long in business?
- Who runs it?
- Where are they based?
- Industry reputation

**Good Signs:**
- Years of operation
- Industry involvement
- Clear leadership
- Physical address

### Product Range

**Positive Indicators:**
- Focused product line
- Quality over quantity
- Clear purpose for each product
- Consistent formulations

**Concerning:**
- Too many products
- Constantly changing line
- Copied formulas
- No product focus

---

## Reading Lab Reports

### What to Check

**Cannabinoid Content:**
- CBD matches label claim
- THC within legal limits
- Other cannabinoids listed
- Accuracy within 10%

**Contaminant Testing:**
- All pass (not just some)
- Recent date
- Matches batch number
- Full panel testing

### Lab Report Red Flags

- Old or undated reports
- Different batch number
- Missing tests
- In-house testing only

---

## Customer Reviews and Reputation

### Where to Look

**Review Sources:**
- Independent review sites
- Trustpilot
- Google reviews
- Reddit discussions
- Industry publications

**What Matters:**
- Consistency across platforms
- Recent reviews
- Detailed feedback
- Company responses

### Interpreting Reviews

**Good Signs:**
- Mostly positive
- Specific product feedback
- Good customer service mentions
- Issues resolved

**Warning Signs:**
- Fake-sounding reviews
- No negative reviews (suspicious)
- Customer service complaints
- Quality inconsistency

---

## Pricing Considerations

### Understanding CBD Pricing

**Reasonable Range:**
- £0.05-0.15 per mg CBD
- Higher isn't always better
- Lower needs scrutiny

**Price Should Reflect:**
- Quality testing
- Proper sourcing
- Good manufacturing
- Customer support

### Too Cheap = Too Risky

If significantly cheaper than competitors, question:
- Testing standards
- Actual CBD content
- Source quality
- Manufacturing practices

---

## Brand Claims to Scrutinise

### Acceptable Claims

- "Contains X mg CBD"
- "Full-spectrum hemp extract"
- "Third-party tested"
- "Organic hemp"

### Unacceptable Claims

**Avoid Brands That:**
- Make medical/disease claims
- Promise to cure conditions
- Use patient testimonials
- Make unrealistic promises

**Examples of Bad Claims:**
- "Cures anxiety"
- "Treats cancer"
- "Guaranteed results"
- "Miracle" anything

## Quick Brand Checklist

### Before Buying

- [ ] COAs available and current
- [ ] Independent lab used
- [ ] Clear contact information
- [ ] Transparent sourcing
- [ ] Reasonable pricing
- [ ] No medical claims
- [ ] Positive reviews
- [ ] Professional website
- [ ] Clear return policy
- [ ] Responsive customer service

### Score Your Brand

- 8-10 checks: Strong choice
- 5-7 checks: Proceed with caution
- Below 5: Consider alternatives

---

## Questions to Ask Customer Service

### Test Their Knowledge

- Where is your hemp sourced?
- What extraction method do you use?
- Can I see the COA for this batch?
- What's your return policy?
- How do you determine dosing?

### Good Answers Show

- Specific, confident responses
- Willingness to help
- Knowledge of products
- No medical advice
- Transparency

---

## Regional Considerations

### EU/UK Specific

**Look For:**
- Novel Food compliance
- EU-sourced hemp
- UK/EU based company
- MHRA awareness
- Proper labelling

### Why It Matters

- Legal compliance
- Quality standards
- Easier returns
- Better accountability

---

## My Take

After 12+ years in this industry, I can spot a quality brand quickly: they're transparent, test thoroughly, make no medical claims, and have been around for a while. Don't get swayed by fancy marketing or celebrity endorsements. The best brands let their testing and reputation speak for themselves. When in doubt, contact customer service—their response tells you a lot about the company.
