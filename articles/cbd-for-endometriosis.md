# CBD for Endometriosis: What We Know

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Endometriosis causes chronic pelvic pain affecting millions of women. While research is limited, CBD's pain-relieving and anti-inflammatory properties may offer symptom support. Here's what the evidence shows.

---

## Understanding Endometriosis

### What It Is

Condition:
- Uterine-like tissue grows outside uterus
- Causes chronic inflammation
- Results in significant pain
- Can affect fertility

### Symptoms

Common experiences:
- Chronic pelvic pain
- Painful periods
- Pain during intercourse
- Digestive issues
- Fatigue

---

## Can CBD Help Endometriosis?

### Theoretical Basis

CBD may address:
- Inflammation (key driver)
- Pain perception
- Muscle tension
- Associated symptoms

### Research Status

Current evidence:
- No endometriosis-specific trials
- General pain and inflammation research
- Endocannabinoid system involvement suggested
- Anecdotal reports from patients

### Endocannabinoid Connection

Interesting research:
- ECS may be dysregulated in endometriosis
- Cannabinoid receptors found in endometrial tissue
- Theoretical basis for benefit
- More research needed

---

## Using CBD for Endometriosis

### Approach

If considering:
- Consistent daily use recommended
- Higher doses may be needed for chronic pain
- Track symptoms over menstrual cycles
- Part of broader management

### Delivery Methods

Options:
- Oral CBD for systemic effects
- Topicals for localized pelvic pain
- Suppositories for direct application
- Combination approach common

### Dosing

For chronic pain:
- Higher range often needed (40-60mg+ daily)
- Build up gradually
- Consistency important
- Work with healthcare provider

---

## What CBD Won't Do

### Limitations

Be realistic:
- Won't cure endometriosis
- Doesn't address underlying tissue growth
- Not a replacement for medical treatment
- Symptom support only

### Medical Treatment Still Important

Continue:
- Working with gynecologist/specialist
- Standard treatments as recommended
- Regular monitoring
- Fertility considerations if applicable

## My Take

Endometriosis is challenging to manage, and I understand why many turn to CBD for relief. The anti-inflammatory properties and emerging endocannabinoid research provide theoretical basis. Some women report meaningful symptom improvement.

However, endometriosis requires proper medical management. Use CBD as a potential complement to, not replacement for, treatment recommended by your specialist.
