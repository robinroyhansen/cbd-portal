# What is Bisabolol (Alpha-Bisabolol)?

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Answer

Alpha-bisabolol is a [terpene](/knowledge/what-are-terpenes) with a gentle, floral aroma, best known as the primary active compound in chamomile. Extensively used in skincare for its soothing properties, bisabolol research supports anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and skin-healing effects. It's particularly valued in topical CBD products.

---

## Bisabolol Profile

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Chemical formula** | C₁₅H₂₆O |
| **Aroma** | Floral, sweet, subtle, slightly honey-like |
| **Boiling point** | 153°C (307°F) |
| **Prevalence in cannabis** | Less common |
| **Also found in** | Chamomile (primary source), candeia tree |

## Research Status

Bisabolol is well-researched for skincare:
- Extensive cosmetic industry research
- Anti-inflammatory mechanism studies
- Wound healing clinical data
- Safety established through widespread use

However, research specifically combining bisabolol with cannabinoids is limited.

## Safety and Tolerance

Bisabolol has excellent safety data:

This makes it suitable for even the most sensitive applications.
