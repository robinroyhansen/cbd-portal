# CBD and Genetics: How Your Genes Affect Response

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Genetic variations influence how individuals respond to CBD, from metabolism speed to receptor sensitivity. Key genes affecting CBD response include those coding for metabolising enzymes (CYP450), cannabinoid receptors, and related proteins. Understanding this helps explain why CBD works differently for different people.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Genes significantly influence CBD response
- CYP450 enzyme genes affect metabolism
- Cannabinoid receptor genes affect sensitivity
- Genetic testing increasingly available
- Personalised dosing may follow

## Key Genetic Factors

### CYP450 Enzyme Genes

**Relevant Genes:**
- CYP2C19
- CYP3A4
- CYP2C9

**Variations:**
- Poor metabolisers (slow)
- Intermediate metabolisers
- Extensive metabolisers (normal)
- Ultra-rapid metabolisers

### Implications

**Poor Metabolisers:**
- CBD stays in system longer
- Higher blood levels
- May need lower doses
- Increased effect duration

**Rapid Metabolisers:**
- Clear CBD quickly
- Lower blood levels
- May need higher doses
- Shorter effect duration

## FAAH Gene

### What FAAH Does

**Enzyme Function:**
- Breaks down anandamide
- Regulates endocannabinoid levels
- Key ECS component
- CBD inhibits FAAH

### Genetic Variations

**FAAH C385A Variant:**
- Reduces FAAH activity
- Higher natural anandamide
- Associated with lower anxiety
- May affect CBD response

**Implications:**
- Those with variant may have naturally higher endocannabinoids
- May respond differently to CBD
- Research still developing
- Personalisation potential
