## Quick Summary

Valencene is a sesquiterpene named after Valencia oranges, where it contributes to the distinctive citrus aroma. Found in cannabis and various citrus fruits, valencene is associated with uplifting effects, anti-inflammatory properties, and may act as a natural insect repellent.

## Key Takeaways

- Sweet citrus aroma reminiscent of Valencia oranges
- Associated with uplifting, energising effects
- Anti-inflammatory and potential skin-protective properties
- Effective insect repellent
- Less common in cannabis but valued when present

---

## What Is Valencene?

Valencene is a sesquiterpene responsible for the characteristic aroma of Valencia oranges. It's less common in cannabis than other citrus terpenes like limonene but adds unique citrus complexity when present.

Natural sources:
- **Valencia oranges** — Primary source (namesake)
- **Grapefruits** — Citrus contribution
- **Tangerines** — Citrus family
- **Cannabis** — Some strains
- **Chinese bayberry** — Traditional use

Studies demonstrate:
