# Is CBD Legal in Saudi Arabia? Complete 2026 Guide

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Answer

CBD is **illegal in Saudi Arabia**. The Kingdom maintains strict prohibitions on all cannabis-derived products, including CBD. Penalties are severe and can include imprisonment or worse. Do not bring CBD to Saudi Arabia.

---

## Legal Framework

### Cannabis Classification

Saudi position:
- All cannabis products prohibited
- No distinction between CBD and THC
- No medical cannabis program
- Zero tolerance enforcement

### Penalties

Consequences:
- Imprisonment (lengthy terms)
- Potential death penalty for trafficking
- Deportation for foreigners
- Criminal record

---

## For Visitors

### Absolute Warning

Critical:
- **Never bring CBD to Saudi Arabia**
- No exceptions for any products
- Airport screening is thorough
- Not worth any risk

### At Airports

Reality:
- Drug detection equipment
- Careful customs inspection
- All cannabinoids treated same
- Severe consequences

## Why So Strict?

### Legal System

Context:
- Sharia law basis
- Conservative drug policies
- No reform movement
- Regional alignment

### No Exceptions

Reality:
- Medical prescriptions don't help
- Foreign products not exempt
- THC-free claims irrelevant

## Key Takeaways

1. **CBD is completely illegal** in Saudi Arabia

3. **Never bring CBD** under any circumstances

---

## My Take

This is one of the clearest "no" situations globally. Saudi Arabia maintains strict prohibition on all cannabis products, and the penalties are among the harshest in the world. There is absolutely no scenario where bringing CBD to Saudi Arabia is advisable.

If you use CBD regularly and are traveling to Saudi Arabia, plan to go without for the duration of your trip. Do not attempt to bring any products, regardless of THC content or labeling.
