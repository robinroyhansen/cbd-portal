# Import and Export of CBD: International Trade Guide

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

International CBD trade involves navigating complex regulations including THC limits, Novel Food requirements, customs classifications, and country-specific rules. Both importers and exporters must ensure compliance with origin and destination country regulations to legally move CBD products across borders.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Both origin and destination rules apply
- THC limits vary by country
- Novel Food affects EU imports
- Proper documentation essential
- Customs classification matters

---

## Key Regulatory Considerations

### THC Limits

**Varies by Destination:**
- EU: 0.2% (moving to 0.3%)
- US: 0.3%
- Switzerland: 1%
- Other countries: Varies widely

**Compliance Required:**
- Meet destination limit
- Documentation proving compliance
- Testing from accredited labs
- Batch-specific results

### Product Classification

**Customs Categories:**
- Food supplement
- Cosmetic
- Novel Food
- Other preparations

**Classification Affects:**
- Duty rates
- Required permits
- Testing requirements
- Entry procedures

---

## Exporting CBD

### From EU

**Requirements:**
- EU-GMP for pharmaceuticals
- Novel Food status for foods
- Proper export documentation
- Destination country compliance
- Customs declarations

**Documentation Needed:**
- Commercial invoice
- Certificate of Analysis
- Phytosanitary certificate (if applicable)
- Export permits (if required)
- Product specifications

### From US

**Considerations:**
- 2018 Farm Bill compliance
- State-specific regulations
- FDA guidance
- Destination requirements
- Export restrictions

### From Switzerland

**Unique Position:**
- 1% THC products problematic for export
- Lower THC products easier
- Non-EU procedures
- Bilateral agreements apply

---

## Importing CBD

### Into EU

**Novel Food Regulation:**
- Authorisation required
- Applications pending
- Enforcement varies
- Market uncertainty

**Requirements:**
- Importer responsibility
- THC testing
- Food safety compliance
- Proper labelling (local language)
- Customs clearance

### Into UK (Post-Brexit)

**Current Framework:**
- Novel Food registry
- FSA oversight
- THC limits
- Separate from EU now

**Requirements:**
- Novel Food compliance
- UK-specific COAs
- Importer of record
- Customs procedures

### Into US

**FDA Considerations:**
- CBD in food/supplements uncertain
- Cosmetics more permissive
- State regulations vary
- Import alerts possible

## Documentation Requirements

### Essential Documents

**Certificate of Analysis**
- Third-party lab
- THC confirmation
- Full cannabinoid profile
- Contaminant testing

**Commercial Documents**
- Commercial invoice
- Packing list
- Bill of lading/airway bill
- Customs declarations

**Compliance Documents**
- GMP certificates
- Product specifications
- Facility registrations
- Phytosanitary certificates

## Customs Classification

### HS Codes

**Common Classifications:**
- 1302: Plant extracts
- 2106: Food preparations
- 3004: Medicaments (pharmaceutical)
- 3304: Cosmetics

**Implications:**
- Duty rates
- Required permits
- Inspection likelihood
- Processing speed

### Country Variations

Different countries may classify similarly products differently, affecting:
- Entry requirements
- Tax treatment
- Regulatory oversight
- Documentation needs

- Higher scrutiny

### High-Value Shipments

- Lower scrutiny for samples
- Quantity limits
