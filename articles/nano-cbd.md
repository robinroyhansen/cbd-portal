# Nano CBD: What Is It and Does It Work?

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Nano CBD refers to CBD that has been processed into extremely small particles (nanometres) to improve water solubility and potentially increase absorption. The technology uses various methods to reduce CBD particle size, making it more bioavailable. While the science is promising, claims about nano CBD often exceed the evidence, and not all nano products are equal.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Nano CBD = smaller CBD particles
- Aims to improve absorption
- Uses nanoemulsion technology
- Claims often exceed evidence
- Quality varies significantly

---

## What Is Nano CBD?

### Definition

Nano CBD is CBD that has been processed to create particles measured in nanometres (billionths of a metre). Regular CBD oil has particles around 2000 nanometres; nano CBD particles are typically 20-200 nanometres.

### The Technology

**Nanoemulsion:**
- CBD oil broken into tiny droplets
- Suspended in water
- Uses emulsifiers/surfactants
- Creates stable mixture

**Goal:**
Make fat-soluble CBD more water-compatible and potentially easier to absorb.

---

## How Nano CBD Is Made

### Nanoemulsion Process

**Methods Include:**
- High-pressure homogenisation
- Ultrasonic processing
- Microfluidisation
- Solvent displacement

**Result:**
CBD particles small enough to be suspended in water and potentially absorbed more easily.

### Key Components

**In a Nano CBD Product:**
- CBD extract
- Emulsifiers (to stabilise)
- Carrier liquid (often water-based)
- Stabilisers

## What Does the Science Say?

### Supporting Evidence

**Some Studies Show:**
- Improved absorption in lab settings
- Faster peak blood levels
- Better water dispersibility

### Limitations

**However:**
- Limited human clinical trials
- Varies by formulation
- Not all nano products equal
- Marketing often exceeds evidence

### Reality Check

The technology is real, but specific claims about any particular product need verification through that product's testing.

## Nano CBD vs. Regular CBD Oil

### Comparison

| Factor | Nano CBD | Regular CBD Oil |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| Particle size | 20-200nm | ~2000nm |
| Water soluble | Yes | No |
| Onset | Potentially faster | Standard |
| Bioavailability | Potentially higher | ~20-35% sublingual |
| Price | Usually higher | Standard |
| Research | Limited | More extensive |

### Trade-offs

**Nano Pros:**
- May absorb better
- Mixes with drinks
- Potentially faster

**Regular Oil Pros:**
- Better researched
- More affordable
- Proven effectiveness
- Simpler formulation

## My Take

Nano CBD technology is real and potentially beneficial, but the marketing often outpaces the science. Yes, smaller particles can improve absorption in theory, and some data supports this. But "nano" has become a buzzword, and not all products using the term deliver meaningful benefits. If regular CBD oil works for you, there's no strong reason to switch. If you're curious, choose a product from a reputable brand with actual data to support their claims. Don't pay a premium just for the "nano" label.
