# What is Phytol?

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Answer

Phytol is a [diterpene](/knowledge/what-are-terpenes) alcohol with a mild, grassy, floral aroma. As a breakdown product of chlorophyll, phytol is present in all green plants. Research suggests anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and sedating properties, with particular interest in its potential for anxiety relief and as a precursor to synthetic vitamin E and K.

---

## Phytol Profile

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Chemical formula** | C₂₀H₄₀O |
| **Aroma** | Mild, grassy, slightly floral, green |
| **Boiling point** | 203°C (397°F) |
| **Type** | Diterpene alcohol (larger than typical terpenes) |
| **Prevalence in cannabis** | Common (from chlorophyll) |
| **Also found in** | All green plants (chlorophyll component) |

---

## The Chlorophyll Connection

Phytol is unique because it comes from chlorophyll:

**How it forms:**
- Chlorophyll molecules contain phytol as a "tail"
- When chlorophyll breaks down, phytol is released
- More present in aged or processed plant material
- Universal to all photosynthetic plants

This means phytol is present in virtually every plant-derived product, not just cannabis.

## Potential Effects and Benefits

Research on phytol suggests:

**Sedative/Relaxing**
- GABA-related sedative effects
- May enhance relaxation
- Contributes to calming profiles
- Sleep research ongoing

**Anti-anxiety**
- Anxiolytic effects documented
- May complement CBD
- GABA mechanism involvement
- Potential for anxiety applications

**Anti-inflammatory**
- Reduces inflammation markers
- Cytokine modulation
- May enhance overall effects
- Multiple mechanisms involved

**Antioxidant**
- Strong antioxidant activity
- Protects against oxidative damage
- May support cellular health
- Common in plants for this reason

**Antimicrobial**
- Antibacterial properties
- Activity against various pathogens
- May contribute to preservation
- Traditional uses support this

---

## Vitamin Precursor Role

Phytol has industrial significance:
- Precursor for synthetic vitamin E (tocopherol)
- Precursor for synthetic vitamin K
- Important in pharmaceutical manufacturing
- Demonstrates biological relevance

---

## Phytol in CBD Products

Phytol presence in CBD products:
- Present in full-spectrum extracts
- More in less-processed products
- Contributes to "green" taste
- Part of the whole-plant profile

Highly processed isolates may have less phytol.

---

## Different from Typical Terpenes

Phytol differs from common cannabis terpenes:
- Diterpene (20 carbons) vs monoterpenes (10 carbons)
- Higher boiling point
- From chlorophyll, not terpene synthesis
- Less aromatic, more subtle

---

## Research Status

Phytol research includes:
- GABA receptor interaction studies
- Anti-inflammatory mechanism research
- Antioxidant activity studies
- Metabolic pathway research

Clinical research is limited but growing.

---

## Safety

Phytol has a good safety profile:
- Consumed in all green vegetables
- GRAS status for food use
- Long history of exposure
- Generally well-tolerated

---

## My Take

Phytol doesn't get the attention of aromatic terpenes, but its presence in full-spectrum products may contribute to their effects. The GABA-related sedation mechanism interests me—it provides another pathway through which whole-plant extracts might work better than isolates. When evaluating CBD products, phytol is a reminder that even the "background" compounds from plants may matter.
