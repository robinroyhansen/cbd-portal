# CBD Inflammation Research: Current Evidence

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Research on CBD for inflammation shows strong preclinical evidence across multiple inflammatory models, with well-documented mechanisms. Human clinical data is more limited but growing, with studies in conditions like inflammatory bowel disease, arthritis, and neuroinflammation.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Strong preclinical anti-inflammatory evidence
- Multiple mechanisms identified
- Human trials developing
- Various conditions being studied
- Translation to clinical use ongoing

---

## Preclinical Evidence

### Animal Models

**Positive Results In:**
- Arthritis models
- Inflammatory bowel disease
- Neuroinflammation
- Skin inflammation
- Cardiovascular inflammation

### Consistency

- Replicated across laboratories
- Multiple species
- Various inflammatory triggers
- Dose-dependent effects

## Mechanism Research

### Established Pathways

**CB2 Receptor Modulation:**
- Immune cell effects
- Cytokine reduction
- Well-documented

**Other Mechanisms:**
- PPARγ activation
- Adenosine signalling
- TRPV1 modulation
- Antioxidant effects

### Cytokine Effects

**Reduced In Studies:**
- TNF-α
- IL-1β
- IL-6
- IFN-γ

---

## Human Clinical Studies

### Inflammatory Bowel Disease

**Irving et al. (2018):**
- Ulcerative colitis study
- CBD-rich extract
- Clinical remission in some
- Promising but mixed

### Arthritis

**Limited Controlled Data:**
- Mostly preclinical
- Case reports positive
- Topical studies emerging
- RCTs needed

### Neuroinflammation

**Multiple Sclerosis:**
- Sativex (THC:CBD) approved
- CBD contribution unclear
- Promising signal
- Neuroprotection researched

## Limitations

### What's Missing

- Large-scale human RCTs
- Long-term data
- Optimal dosing
- Comparison to standard treatments

### Translation Challenges

- Animal models may not predict humans
- Doses differ significantly
- Route of administration matters
- Individual variation

---

## My Take

CBD's anti-inflammatory properties are well-established in laboratory research, with solid mechanistic understanding. The challenge is translating this to proven human treatments. The evidence supports CBD as having real anti-inflammatory activity, but we need more clinical trials to know how to best use it for specific conditions.
