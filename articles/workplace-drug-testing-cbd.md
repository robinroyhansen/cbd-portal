# Workplace Drug Testing and CBD: What You Need to Know

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Workplace drug tests typically screen for THC, not CBD. However, full-spectrum CBD products contain trace THC that can accumulate with heavy use, potentially triggering positive results. Understanding your testing situation and choosing appropriate products is essential for employed CBD users.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Standard tests detect THC, not CBD
- Full-spectrum products pose highest risk
- Isolate and verified broad-spectrum are safer
- Employer policies vary significantly
- Know your situation before using CBD

---

## Understanding Workplace Drug Tests

### What They Test For

**Standard Panels:**
- 5-panel: THC, cocaine, opiates, PCP, amphetamines
- 10-panel: Above plus benzodiazepines, barbiturates, methadone, propoxyphene, quaaludes
- CBD is NOT tested for

### THC Detection

**What's Detected:**
- THC-COOH (metabolite)
- Indicates past cannabis use
- Doesn't distinguish source
- Accumulates in body

### Common Cutoff Levels

| Test Type | Standard Cutoff |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Screening | 50 ng/mL |
| Confirmation | 15 ng/mL |
| Federal/DOT | 50 ng/mL |
| Zero tolerance | Lower thresholds |

## Risk by Product Type

### Full-Spectrum CBD

**Risk Level: Higher**

- Contains trace THC (up to 0.2-0.3%)
- THC accumulates with regular use
- Heavy use increases risk
- Positive results possible

### Broad-Spectrum CBD

**Risk Level: Lower**

- THC removed (ideally)
- Must verify with COA
- Quality varies
- Safer if properly made

### CBD Isolate

**Risk Level: Lowest**

- Pure CBD, no THC
- Verify with testing
- Most conservative choice
- Safest for tested workers

---

## Factors Affecting Risk

### Usage Patterns

**Higher Risk:**
- Daily, heavy use
- High doses (100mg+ CBD)
- Extended periods
- Full-spectrum products

**Lower Risk:**
- Occasional use
- Lower doses
- THC-free products
- Short duration

### Individual Factors

- Body fat percentage
- Metabolism rate
- Hydration levels
- Time since last use

## Protecting Your Employment

### Strategies

**If Subject to Testing:**

1. **Choose wisely**
   - THC-free products only
   - Isolate or verified broad-spectrum
   - Third-party tested

2. **Verify products**
   - Check COA for THC
   - "ND" (not detected) preferred
   - Batch-specific testing
   - Reputable brands

3. **Keep documentation**
   - Purchase receipts
   - COAs
   - Product information
   - May help if questioned

### What to Avoid

- Full-spectrum products
- Unverified THC content
- Unknown brands
- Products without COAs

---

## If You Test Positive

### Immediate Steps

1. Remain calm
2. Request confirmation test (GC-MS)
3. Gather documentation
4. Consult HR or legal advice
5. Explain CBD use if appropriate

### Documentation to Have

- Product information
- Certificate of Analysis
- Purchase records
- Usage log (if maintained)

### Reality Check

- "It was just CBD" may not help
- Policies are policies
- Documentation is crucial
- Legal advice may be needed

## Alternative Approaches

### If CBD Is Important

**Consider:**
- Topicals (minimal absorption)
- Isolate products only
- Lower doses
- Understand your risk

### When to Avoid CBD

- Zero-tolerance employers
- Safety-sensitive positions
- Before pre-employment tests
- When risk exceeds benefit
