# EU Novel Food Regulations and CBD: What You Need to Know

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

The EU Novel Food Regulation requires CBD extracts and products to be authorised before being legally sold as foods or supplements. This regulation has significantly impacted the European CBD market, with many products operating in legal grey areas while applications are processed.

---

## Key Takeaways

- CBD extracts classified as "Novel Foods" in the EU
- Products need authorisation for legal sale
- Application process is lengthy and expensive
- Enforcement varies by member state
- Many products in market pre-date enforcement

---

## What Are Novel Foods?

### Definition

Novel Foods are:
- Foods not consumed significantly in EU before May 1997
- Require safety assessment before sale
- Must be authorised by EFSA
- Include CBD extracts (per EU ruling)

### Why CBD Qualifies

- No history of significant EU consumption before 1997
- Extracted CBD treated differently than hemp seeds
- EFSA decision in 2019 classified CBD as novel

---

## The Authorisation Process

### Steps Required

1. Submit application to EU member state
2. Safety assessment by EFSA
3. Review by European Commission
4. Authorisation decision
5. Product can be legally sold

### Challenges

- Expensive (hundreds of thousands of euros)
- Time-consuming (18+ months)
- Requires extensive safety data
- Many applications pending

---

## Current Market Reality

### Legal Situation

- Many products on market without authorisation
- Enforcement varies by country
- Large backlog of applications
- Uncertainty for consumers and businesses

### What This Means for Consumers

- Products from established brands generally safer
- Look for companies with Novel Food applications
- Regulations protect consumer safety
- Market consolidation likely

---

## My Take

Novel Food regulations make the EU CBD market complex. While intended to ensure safety, they've created uncertainty. Until more authorisations are granted, buying from reputable brands with pending applications is the pragmatic approach.
