# Travelling with CBD in Europe: Complete Guide

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

Travelling with CBD within Europe is generally possible but requires preparation. While EU countries largely permit hemp-derived CBD with low THC, regulations vary by country. Carrying documentation, staying within THC limits, and understanding airline policies helps ensure smooth travel.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Most EU countries allow low-THC CBD
- Carry COA and proof of THC content
- Keep products in original packaging
- Check airline policies before flying
- Country-specific rules may apply

---

## General EU Framework

### What's Generally Permitted

- Hemp-derived CBD products
- THC below 0.2% (or 0.3% in some)
- Personal use quantities
- Proper documentation

### Schengen Zone

**Within Schengen:**
- No routine border checks
- Still subject to local laws
- Random checks possible
- Keep documentation anyway

**Entering/Leaving Schengen:**
- Customs controls apply
- Declaration may be needed
- Non-EU rules differ
- More scrutiny possible

---

## Essential Documentation

### What to Carry

**Certificate of Analysis (COA)**
- Recent, batch-specific
- Shows THC content
- From third-party lab
- Matches your product

**Additional Documents**
- Proof of purchase
- Product information
- Company contact details
- Prescription (if medical)

### Why Documentation Matters

- Proves legal THC content
- Demonstrates legitimate product
- Resolves questions quickly
- Shows due diligence

## Country-Specific Considerations

### Generally Permissive

**Countries:**
- Germany
- Netherlands

**Notes:**
- CBD widely available

### More Restrictive

**Countries:**

- Some Eastern European states

**Caution Advised:**
- Research current status
- More conservative products
- Extra documentation

## Flying with CBD

### Airline Policies

**Check Beforehand:**
- Individual airline rules
- Departure country regulations
- Arrival country laws
- Transit country considerations

### Practical Tips

**Carry-On:**
- Generally preferred
- Subject to liquid rules
- Declare if asked
- Keep accessible

**Checked Luggage:**
- Higher quantity possible
- Less accessible if questions
- Risk of loss
- Still needs documentation

### Security Screening

**What to Expect:**
- May be flagged for inspection
- Questions about contents
- Document request possible
- Generally resolved with COA

---

## Land Travel

### Driving Within EU

**Generally Simple:**
- No routine checks (Schengen)
- Local laws apply
- Random stops possible
- Keep documentation

### Train Travel

- Minimal checks usually
- Cross-border trains same rules
- Keep products accessible
- Documentation ready

---

## Quantity Guidelines

### Personal Use

**Reasonable Amounts:**
- What you'd use during trip
- Typically 30-day supply max
- Avoid bulk quantities
- Commercial quantities problematic

### What Raises Concerns

- Large quantities
- Many products
- Obvious commercial intent
- Missing documentation

---

## Product Types

### Lower Risk

- CBD oils in small bottles
- Capsules in pharmacy-style packaging
- Topicals/creams
- Professional packaging

### Higher Risk

- CBD flower (looks like cannabis)
- Large quantities
- Unprofessional packaging
- Missing labels

### CBD Flower Warning

- Visually identical to cannabis
- May cause confusion
- Some countries prohibit
- Consider avoiding when travelling

---

## Problem Resolution

### If Questioned

1. Stay calm and cooperative
2. Present documentation
3. Explain product clearly
4. Show COA and THC content
5. Provide company contact

### If Confiscated

- Request written documentation
- Note officer information
- Contact company
- Consult legal advice if needed
- Document everything

- [ ] Verify airline policy
- [ ] Obtain current COA

- [ ] Carry documentation accessibly

## Non-EU European Countries

### Switzerland

- 1% THC limit (higher)
- CBD widely available
- Entering EU with Swiss products may need lower THC

### UK (Post-Brexit)

- Novel Food rules
- THC limits similar to EU
- Customs between EU and UK
- Check current regulations

### Norway

- Non-EU, own rules
- Stricter generally
- Research before travelling
- Medical exceptions possible
