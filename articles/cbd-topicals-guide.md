# CBD Topicals Guide: Creams, Balms, and Lotions Explained

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

CBD topicals are products applied directly to the skin—creams, balms, lotions, and more. They work **locally** without entering the bloodstream, making them ideal for targeted relief. Best for joint pain, muscle soreness, skin conditions, and localized discomfort.

---

## How CBD Topicals Work

### Local Mechanism

When you apply CBD topically:
- CBD absorbs through skin
- Interacts with local cannabinoid receptors
- Affects nearby tissue
- Doesn't reach bloodstream (systemic circulation)

### Why This Matters

**Pros of local action:**
- Targeted to specific area
- No systemic effects
- No drowsiness or other body effects
- Can use alongside oral CBD

**Limitations:**
- Only affects application area
- Won't help whole-body issues
- Need to reapply
- Absorption limited to skin depth

## Types of CBD Topicals

### CBD Creams

**Characteristics:**
- Water-based
- Lightweight, absorbs quickly
- Less greasy
- Good for larger areas

**Best for:**
- Daily use
- Face and sensitive skin
- Light coverage
- Quick absorption

### CBD Balms/Salves

**Characteristics:**
- Oil/wax-based
- Thicker consistency
- Longer-lasting on skin
- Creates protective layer

**Best for:**
- Intense targeted relief
- Dry skin
- Specific joints or muscles
- Overnight application

### CBD Lotions

**Characteristics:**
- Thinner than creams
- Very absorbent
- Often with moisturizers
- Good spread

**Best for:**
- Large areas
- Daily skincare
- General moisturizing with CBD
- Body-wide application

### CBD Oils (for skin)

**Characteristics:**
- Pure oil carrier
- Concentrated
- Can be greasy
- Often for face

**Best for:**
- Facial skincare
- Mixing with other products
- Concentrated application

### CBD Roll-Ons

**Characteristics:**
- Easy application
- No-mess
- Portable
- Pre-measured

**Best for:**
- On-the-go use
- Travel
- Quick application
- Target small areas

### CBD Patches

**Characteristics:**
- Transdermal (may enter bloodstream)
- Sustained release
- Hands-free
- Long-lasting

**Best for:**
- Extended relief
- Consistent dosing
- When hands-free needed
- Longer-term application

## What CBD Topicals Help

### Joint Pain

Common use:
- Arthritis
- General joint discomfort
- Stiff joints
- Apply directly to joint

**How to use:**
- Massage into joint area
- Apply 2-3 times daily
- May take days to notice effect
- Consistency helps

### Muscle Soreness

Common use:
- Post-workout
- Exercise recovery
- Muscle tension
- Overuse soreness

**How to use:**
- Apply to sore muscles
- Massage in well
- After exercise or before bed
- Combine with stretching

### Skin Conditions

Common use:
- Eczema
- Psoriasis
- Dry skin
- Acne (some products)

**How to use:**
- Apply to affected areas
- Gentle application
- Test patch first
- Consistent use

### Localized Pain

Common use:
- Back pain (localized)
- Knee pain
- Shoulder pain
- Any specific area

**How to use:**
- Apply directly to painful area
- Generous application
- Massage in
- Reapply as needed

## How to Use CBD Topicals

### Application Tips

1. **Clean the area** — Remove dirt, sweat
2. **Apply generously** — Don't skimp
3. **Massage in** — Helps absorption
4. **Wait before covering** — Let absorb
5. **Reapply as needed** — Every 4-6 hours
6. **Wash hands after** — Unless treating hands

### Dos and Don'ts

**Do:**
- Apply to clean skin
- Use enough product
- Be consistent
- Target specific areas
- Give it time to work

**Don't:**
- Apply to broken skin
- Use on open wounds
- Expect systemic effects
- Use sparingly (won't work as well)
- Give up after one use

## CBD Topical Strength

### How Strength Is Measured

Usually mg per container:
- 100-300mg: Low strength
- 300-600mg: Medium strength
- 600-1000mg: High strength
- 1000mg+: Extra strength

### What Strength to Choose

| Use | Suggested Strength |
|-----|-------------------|
| **Daily moisturizing** | Low (100-300mg) |
| **Mild discomfort** | Medium (300-600mg) |
| **Significant pain** | High (600-1000mg) |
| **Intense relief needed** | Extra strength (1000mg+) |

## Topicals vs. Oral CBD

### When to Use Topicals

- Localized issue
- Don't want systemic effects
- Targeting specific area
- Skin conditions
- Adding to oral regimen

### When to Use Oral

- Whole-body effects needed
- Anxiety, sleep, general wellness
- Widespread pain/inflammation
- Systemic conditions

### Using Both

Many people use both:
- Oral for baseline support
- Topical for targeted areas
- Comprehensive approach
- Different mechanisms

## Quality Indicators

### What to Look For

- **CBD concentration listed** (mg)
- **Third-party tested**
- **Ingredient list** (what else is in it?)
- **Reputable brand**
- **Appropriate price**

### Beneficial Additional Ingredients

| Ingredient | Benefit |
|------------|---------|
| **Menthol** | Cooling sensation |
| **Camphor** | Warming sensation |
| **Arnica** | Traditional pain relief |
| **Capsaicin** | Heating effect |
| **Essential oils** | Additional benefits |

### Avoid

- Artificial fragrances (if sensitive)
- Products without CBD amount listed
- Unrealistically cheap options
- No testing available

## Key Takeaways

1. **Topicals work locally** — Don't enter bloodstream
2. **Best for targeted relief** — Joints, muscles, skin
3. **Different types** — Creams, balms, lotions, patches
4. **Massage in well** — Helps absorption
5. **Reapply as needed** — Effect is localized
6. **Can combine with oral** — Different mechanisms

## My Take

Topicals are the unsung heroes of CBD products. They're straightforward—apply where it hurts—and they work without any systemic effects to worry about.

I find them most useful for joint pain and muscle soreness. After exercise, a CBD balm massaged into sore muscles feels good and may genuinely help recovery. For arthritis in hands or knees, regular topical application can provide meaningful relief.

Don't expect topicals to help anxiety, sleep, or anything requiring whole-body effects. That's not what they do. But for targeted, localized relief, they're excellent and very safe.

My tip: don't skimp on application. A thin smear won't do much. Apply generously and massage it in well.
