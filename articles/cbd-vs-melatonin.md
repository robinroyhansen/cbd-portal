# CBD vs Melatonin: Which Is Better for Sleep?

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Comparison

| Factor | CBD | Melatonin |
|--------|-----|-----------|
| **Primary Action** | Calms nervous system, reduces anxiety | Signals sleep time to brain |
| **Best For** | Anxiety-related sleep issues | Circadian rhythm problems |
| **Onset Time** | 30-60 minutes | 30-60 minutes |
| **Duration** | 4-8 hours | 4-6 hours |
| **Dependency Risk** | Very low | Low (but possible timing dependency) |
| **Research Level** | Moderate | Strong |
| **Natural Source** | Hemp plant | Body produces it naturally |

---

## How They Work Differently

### CBD's Sleep Mechanism

CBD doesn't directly induce sleep. Instead, it may:
- Reduce anxiety that prevents sleep
- Promote calm through ECS interaction
- Address underlying sleep disruptors
- Support relaxation without sedation

Think of CBD as removing barriers to sleep rather than forcing it.

### Melatonin's Sleep Mechanism

Melatonin is a sleep hormone your body naturally produces:
- Signals to your brain that it's time to sleep
- Regulates circadian rhythm
- Levels rise naturally in evening darkness
- Supplements boost this natural signal

Melatonin tells your body when to sleep; CBD helps create conditions for sleep.

## When CBD Works Better

### Anxiety-Driven Insomnia

If you can't sleep because your mind races:
- CBD may calm anxious thoughts
- Addresses the root cause
- Promotes mental relaxation
- Doesn't just force drowsiness

### Pain-Related Sleep Problems

If discomfort keeps you awake:
- CBD's potential anti-inflammatory effects
- May reduce pain perception
- Allows body to relax
- Complements sleep indirectly

### General Restlessness

If you feel wound up at night:
- CBD may promote overall calm
- Supports relaxation
- Helps transition to sleep mode
- Addresses tension

---

## When Melatonin Works Better

### Jet Lag

Melatonin excels here:
- Resets body clock to new timezone
- Signals sleep at appropriate new time
- Short-term use effective
- Well-researched for this purpose

### Shift Work

If you need to sleep during daylight:
- Melatonin signals "sleep time" regardless of light
- Helps override natural wake signals
- Useful for irregular schedules
- Supports non-standard sleep times

### Delayed Sleep Phase

If your natural sleep time is too late:
- Melatonin can shift sleep timing earlier
- Helps reset circadian rhythm
- Taken before desired bedtime
- Gradually adjusts schedule

### Older Adults

Melatonin production decreases with age:
- Supplements restore natural levels
- May be especially helpful for seniors
- Addresses physiological decline
- Well-tolerated in older populations

## Research Comparison

### Melatonin Research

**Strengths:**
- Decades of research
- Well-understood mechanism
- Proven for jet lag and shift work
- FDA recognized for sleep (in dietary supplements)

**Limitations:**
- Less effective for anxiety-based insomnia
- Doesn't address pain
- Short-term effectiveness well-proven; long-term less clear

### CBD Research

**Strengths:**
- Growing evidence for anxiety reduction
- Promising sleep studies
- May address multiple sleep barriers
- Generally well-tolerated

**Limitations:**
- Less research than melatonin
- Mechanism less precisely understood
- Quality varies between products
- Optimal dosing not established

## Side Effects Comparison

### CBD Side Effects

Generally mild:
- Dry mouth
- Drowsiness (sometimes desired)
- Potential drug interactions
- Mild GI effects in some people

### Melatonin Side Effects

Generally mild:
- Morning grogginess
- Vivid dreams
- Headache (some people)
- Short-term use well-tolerated
- Timing-dependent effects

### Safety Profile

Both have favorable safety profiles for short-term use. Long-term data:
- **Melatonin:** Generally safe but questions about prolonged high-dose use
- **CBD:** Limited long-term data but no significant concerns identified

Many sleep products combine CBD and melatonin:
- CBD addresses anxiety/tension

## How to Choose

### Choose CBD If:

- Racing thoughts keep you awake
- Anxiety contributes to sleep issues
- Physical tension prevents relaxation
- You want to address root causes
- Pain affects your sleep

### Choose Melatonin If:

- Your sleep timing is off
- You're dealing with jet lag
- You work shifts or irregular hours
- You're an older adult
- You need circadian rhythm adjustment

### Consider Both If:

- Single approach hasn't worked
- You have multiple sleep barriers
- Your healthcare provider approves
- You want comprehensive support

## Dosing Considerations

### CBD for Sleep

- Typical range: 25-75mg
- Higher doses may be more sedating
- Timing: 30-60 minutes before bed
- Start lower, adjust as needed

### Melatonin for Sleep

- Typical range: 0.5-5mg
- Lower doses often equally effective
- Timing: 30-60 minutes before desired sleep
- More isn't always better

## My Take

Both CBD and melatonin have their place in sleep support, but they work very differently.

If anxiety or racing thoughts keep you awake, CBD is worth trying—it addresses the mental component that melatonin doesn't touch. But if your issue is purely timing (jet lag, shift work, or an off-kilter schedule), melatonin is the more logical choice.

For many people with modern sleep problems—stress-driven, anxiety-related, compounded by screen use and irregular schedules—a combination approach makes sense. Just start with one, understand how it affects you, then consider adding the other if needed.

Neither is a magic solution, and both work best alongside good sleep hygiene: consistent schedule, dark room, no screens before bed, limited caffeine. These fundamentals matter more than any supplement.
