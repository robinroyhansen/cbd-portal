# Is CBD Legal? A Global Overview

By Robin Roy Krigslund-Hansen | 12+ years in CBD industry
Last updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Quick Summary

CBD legality varies significantly worldwide. In the EU, hemp-derived CBD is generally legal with THC limits of 0.2-0.3%. In the US, hemp-derived CBD is federally legal under the 2018 Farm Bill. Many other countries have varying regulations, and some prohibit CBD entirely.

---

## Key Takeaways

- EU: Generally legal, 0.2% THC limit (some countries 0.3%)
- UK: Legal, Novel Food authorisation required for sale
- US: Federally legal (hemp-derived), state laws vary
- Canada: Legal, regulated like cannabis
- Some countries: Completely prohibited
- Always check current local laws before purchase or travel

---

## European Union

### General Framework

- Hemp-derived CBD legal in most member states
- THC limit: 0.2% (increasing to 0.3%)
- Novel Food Regulation applies to CBD products
- Products need authorisation for legal sale
- Medical claims prohibited

### Key Markets

- **Germany** — Large market, pharmacies and online
- **France** — Legal after court ruling
- **Italy** — Legal with restrictions
- **Spain** — Cosmetic/topical focus
- **Netherlands** — Liberal approach

---

## United Kingdom

Post-Brexit framework:
- CBD legal with <0.2% THC
- Novel Food authorisation required (FSA)
- Many products in compliance process
- Available widely in shops and online

---

## United States

### Federal Law

- 2018 Farm Bill legalised hemp-derived CBD
- Must contain <0.3% THC
- FDA hasn't approved CBD foods/supplements
- Enforcement is limited

### State Variation

- Most states allow CBD sales
- Some restrictions in certain states
- Regulations evolving rapidly
- Check state-specific rules

---

## Other Regions

### Where CBD Is Legal

- Canada (regulated cannabis framework)
- Australia (prescription or certain OTC)
- Switzerland (more liberal than EU)
- Many South American countries

### Where CBD Is Prohibited/Restricted

- Russia
- China (despite being major producer)
- Most Middle Eastern countries
- Singapore, Malaysia
- Some African nations

---

## Travel Considerations

### Before Travelling

- Research destination laws
- Check airline policies
- Consider whether worth the risk
- Leave products home if uncertain

---

## My Take

CBD legality is a patchwork that changes frequently. The trend is toward legalisation, but there are still places where carrying CBD could cause serious problems. When in doubt, check current laws for your specific location and don't assume legality transfers across borders.
